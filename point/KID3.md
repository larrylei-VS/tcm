### Supreme Stream TÀIXĪ
![[KID-3 太溪.png|250]]
- Shu-Stream, Yuan-Source and Earth point of the Kidney channel

#### Location

In the depression between the medial malleolus and the Achilles tendon, level with the prominence of the medial malleolus.

#### Location Notes

Locate in the centre of the depression, midway between the prominence of the medial malleolus and the posterior border of the Achilles tendon.

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion 0.5 to 1 cun or join to [Kūnlún BL-60](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-60).

#### Actions

- Nourishes Kidney yin and clears deficiency heat
- Tonifies Kidney yang
- Anchors the qi and benefits the Lung
- Strengthens the lumbar spine

#### Indications

- Deafness, tinnitus, headache and dizziness, toothache, nosebleed, sore throat, heat sensation in the mouth, phlegm in the mouth that feels like glue, wasting and thirsting disorder, hot disease with copious sweating, chronic malaria, emaciation.
- Cough, coughing blood, cough with no pleasure in eating, wheezing, dyspnoea, asthma, chest pain.
- Insomnia, excessive dreaming, poor memory, heat sensation in the palms, stabbing pain in the Heart.
- Seminal emission, impotence, premature ejaculation, sexual taxation, irregular menstruation.
- Frequent and copious urination, enuresis, heat in the hypogastrium with yellow urine, abdominal distention, difficult defecation, cold shan disorder, damp itchy skin lesions on the inner thigh.
- Lumbar pain, pain of the abdomen and lateral costal region, injury by cold with inversion counterflow of the hands and feet, coldness of the lower limbs, numbness and pain of the legs, swelling and pain of the ankle, swelling and pain of the heel.

#### Commentary

The Kidneys occupy a unique position among the zangfu, both storing the yin essence and conserving and controlling the ming men fire. The Kidneys are thus the root of the yin and yang of the whole body. Because of this fundamental role, deficiency of the Kidneys may both cause and result from disharmony of any of the other zangfu.

The Spiritual Pivot in Chapter 6 recommends the use of the shu-stream points in the treatment of disorders of the zang, whilst in Chapter 1 it says "When the five zang are diseased, select [from] the twelve yuan-source [points]". Tàixī KID-3, the shu-stream and yuan-source point, is therefore the principal point on the Kidney channel to treat disharmony of the Kidney zang, which due to the unique nature of the Kidneys always involves deficiency, principally of Kidney yin or Kidney yang.

Kidney yin deficiency may affect the Kidneys alone, or by failing to nourish the Liver, Heart or Lung, all of which are reached by the Kidney channel, affect these zang also. Yin deficiency may manifest in two main ways: failure to nourish and moisten, or failure to restrain fire. The art of treating yin deficiency lies in carefully assessing the relative importance of these two aspects, and placing appropriate emphasis on nourishing yin and cooling fire.

Tàixī KID-3 is the foremost point to treat yin deficiency affecting those areas of the body either reached by the Kidney channel or dominated by the Kidneys, specifically the throat, intestines, brain, teeth and ears. The Kidney primary channel ascends to the throat, and if heat from deficiency scorches and dries this region there may be chronic low-grade sore throat which is typically worse with tiredness, at the end of the day and at night. The Kidneys dominate the two lower yin (anus and urethra), and if deficient fluids and deficiency heat dessicate the intestines, there will be difficult defecation, illustrated by Wu Ju-tong's graphic image in the Systematic Differentiation of Warm Diseases "When the fluids are deficient there is not enough water to make the boat move". The Spiritual Pivot[7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-kidney-channel/references) states "The brain is the sea of marrow". Kidney essence produces marrow to fill the brain and spinal cord, and in cases where the sea of marrow is deficient there will be headache and dizziness. According to a saying of Chinese medicine "The teeth are the surplus of the bones". If the Kidneys fail to nourish the teeth, and heat from deficiency rises to this area, there will be chronic toothache accompanied by looseness of the teeth. The Spiritual Pivot[8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-kidney-channel/references) says "Kidney qi opens into the ears, when the Kidney is in harmony the ears can hear the five sounds". Deficiency of the Kidneys, therefore, can result in tinnitus and deafness. In all these cases, Tàixī KID-3 is the primary point to tonify and nourish the Kidneys.

The intimate relationship between the Kidney yin and Liver yin, and the Kidney essence and Liver blood, is emphasised in the saying "the Kidneys and the Liver share the same origin", and the statement in Comprehensive Medicine According to Master Zhang "[When] the essence is not discharged it will return to the Liver and transform into clear blood". Tàixī KID-3 is commonly used in prescriptions to treat Kidney-Liver disharmony (whether due to failure of water to nourish wood, or to injury to the Kidneys from prolonged Liver disease) giving rise to such symptoms as headache, dizziness, tinnitus and deafness.

The Kidneys pertain to water and the Heart to fire, and both belong to shaoyin. The Heart stores the spirit and the Kidneys store the will. When the Kidneys and the Heart function harmoniously, Heart fire and Kidney water, the spirit and the will, mutually support each other. When the Heart and Kidneys lose harmony, Heart yang is unable to descend to warm the Kidneys, and Kidney yin is unable to nourish Heart yin and restrain Heart fire. This loss of contact between water and fire disturbs the spirit and gives rise to insomnia, excessive dreaming and poor memory, usually of the severe and chronic kind. Tàixī KID-3 is an important point to bolster the Kidneys and enable them to support the Heart.

The Lung is the 'canopy' and the Kidneys are the root. The Lung descends qi and fluids to the Kidneys below, the Kidneys anchor the qi and steam the fluids to moisten the Lung above. Kidney-Lung disharmony commonly manifests in two main ways, i. Kidney yin deficiency fails to moisten and cool the Lung giving rise to symptoms such as wheezing, dry cough and blood-streaked sputum, and ii. the Kidneys fail to receive and anchor the qi from the Lung giving rise to such symptoms as dyspnoea, coughing and asthma. Tàixī KID-3 is indicated in both these patterns of "excess above and deficiency below" and was specifically recommended by Sun Si-miao in the Supplement to the Thousand Ducat Formulas for Kidney cough.

Although subdivided into Kidney yin and Kidney yang, the Kidneys are of course one zang. Even though in clinical practice deficiency of either yin or yang will usually predominate, when one aspect of the Kidneys is deficient, it is frequently the case that the other will also become deficient. This is clearly spelt out both by the Essential Questions[9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-kidney-channel/references) which states "Injury to yin will reach the yang, injury to yang will reach the yin", and by the Essential Readings in Medicine which says "Without yang, yin cannot engender; without yin, yang cannot transform". It is a long-standing principle of tonifying the Kidneys that in Kidney yang deficiency one should also nourish Kidney yin, and that in nourishing Kidney yin one should also pay attention to the Kidney yang. Thus the Essential Questions[10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-kidney-channel/references) says "[For] yang diseases treat the yin, [for] yin diseases treat the yang", and Zhang Jing-yue echoes this in his statement "Nourishing yin to help yang is a skilful way to strengthen yang". For this reason, almost all acupuncture points that tonify the Kidneys will benefit both Kidney yin and Kidney yang. Tàixī KID-3, the principal point on the Kidney channel to tonify the Kidneys, exemplifies this principle.

When Kidney yang fails to warm the body and transform fluids, there may be coldness of the lower limbs, backache, and urinary symptoms such as frequent and copious urination and incontinence. The close relationship between the Kidneys and the libido and sexual function is underscored by the application of Tàixī KID-3 in the treatment of impotence, seminal emission and sexual taxation (depletion and exhaustion due to excessive sexual activity).

According to the Essential Questions[11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-kidney-channel/references) "The Kidneys dominate hibernation and are the root of sealed storage and the residence of essence; their radiance manifests in the hair and fills the bones". The Kidney primary, luo-connecting and sinew channels all ascend through the spine, and any pattern of Kidney deficiency may lead to malnourishment of the sinews, muscles and bones, especially in the lumbar region, the 'residence' of the Kidneys. Tàixī KID-3 has long been used for the treatment of Kidney deficiency lumbar pain, and is also indicated for heel pain, which may be due to Kidney deficiency, traumatic injury or painful obstruction. In the treatment of heel pain, Tàixī KID-3 should be needled to obtain sensation radiating strongly to the heel region.

The Penetrating and Conception vessels are both nourished by and have their root in the Kidneys, and the Essential Questions says "the vessel of the uterus connects with the Kidneys"[12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-kidney-channel/references). Tàixī KID-3 may be used when Kidney deficiency leads to disharmony of these extraordinary vessels resulting in irregular menstruation, although it is interesting to note that this is a modern indication and that no gynaecological indications or combinations appear in any major classical texts.

Finally Tàixī KID-3 is cited in the Song of the Nine Needles for Returning the Yang for the treatment of collapse of yang characterised by loss of consciousness, aversion to cold, cold counterflow of the limbs, purple lips etc.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Shu-stream and yuan-source point therefore the principal point to tonify any Kidney deficiency (of yin, yang or qi): low-grade sore throat, difficult defecation, constipation due to fluid deficiency, headache, dizziness, chronic toothache, looseness of the teeth, tinnitus, deafness, impotence, seminal emission, sexual taxation, coldness of the lower limbs, frequent and copious urination, incontinence of urine, lumbar pain, heel pain.
- Harmonises the relationship of the Kidneys with the Liver, Heart and Lung: headache, dizziness, insomnia, excessive dreaming, poor memory, wheezing, dry cough, blood-streaked sputum, dyspnoea, coughing, asthma.

### **[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** (TÀIXĪ)

* **英文名称：** Supreme Stream
* **中文名称：** 太溪
* **国际代码：** KID-3
* **穴位属性：** **[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 是肾经的输穴、原穴和土穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于内踝与跟腱之间的凹陷中，与内踝尖平齐。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 位于凹陷中央，内踝尖与跟腱后缘之间的中点。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

垂直刺0.5至1寸，或透刺至**[BL60 昆仑](/point/BL60.md)**。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 滋养肾阴，清虚热
* 补益肾阳
* 纳气，宣利肺脏
* 强健腰脊

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

耳聋，耳鸣，头痛眩晕，牙痛，鼻出血，咽喉肿痛，口中灼热感，口中痰涎如胶，消渴病，热病大汗出，慢性疟疾，消瘦。
咳嗽，咳血，咳嗽伴食欲不振，喘息，呼吸困难，哮喘，胸痛。
失眠，多梦，记忆力差，掌心发热，心痛如刺。
遗精，阳痿，早泄，房劳（房事过度所致劳损），月经不调。
小便频数而量多，遗尿，小腹热黄尿，腹胀，排便困难，寒疝，大腿内侧皮肤湿痒疮疡。
腰痛，腹部和胁肋部疼痛，伤寒伴手足逆冷，下肢寒冷，腿部麻木疼痛，踝关节肿痛，足跟痛。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

肾脏在脏腑中具有独特地位，既藏阴精，又固摄和调控命门之火。因此，肾是全身阴阳之根本。由于其根本作用，肾虚既可能导致也可能源于其他脏腑的失调。

《灵枢·本输》第六篇推荐输穴用于治疗脏腑疾病，而第一篇则说“五脏有疾，取十二原穴”。**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)**，作为输穴和原穴，因此是肾经上治疗肾脏失调的主要穴位，而肾脏由于其独特的性质，总是涉及虚证，主要是肾阴虚或肾阳虚。

肾阴虚可能仅影响肾脏，或因不能滋养肝、心、肺（肾经均循行至这些脏腑）而影响这些脏腑。阴虚可能表现为两种主要方式：不能滋养濡润，或不能制约虚火。治疗阴虚的关键在于仔细评估这两个方面的相对重要性，并适当侧重滋阴和清火。

**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 是治疗影响肾经所及或肾脏所主身体部位的阴虚的首要穴位，特别是咽喉、肠道、脑、牙齿和耳朵。肾经正经上行至咽喉，如果虚热灼伤和干燥此区域，可能出现慢性低热性咽喉痛，通常在疲劳后、一天结束时和夜间加重。肾主二阴（肛门和尿道），如果津液不足和虚热耗伤肠道，则会出现排便困难，正如吴鞠通在《温病条辨》中生动地描绘：“津枯则舟不载水”。《灵枢·海论》记载：“脑者，髓之海也”。肾精生髓以充养脑和脊髓，当髓海不足时，会出现头痛和眩晕。中医有句俗语：“齿为骨之余”。如果肾不能滋养牙齿，且虚火上炎至此区域，则会出现慢性牙痛伴牙齿松动。《灵枢·本神》记载：“肾气通于耳，肾和则耳能闻五音”。因此，肾虚可导致耳鸣耳聋。在所有这些情况下，**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 都是补益肾脏的主要穴位。

肾阴与肝阴，肾精与肝血之间密切的关系，体现在“肝肾同源”的俗语中，以及张景岳《张氏医通》中“精不泄则归肝，化为清血”的论述。**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 常用于治疗肝肾不和（无论是水不涵木，还是肝病久损及肾）引起的头痛、眩晕、耳鸣耳聋等症状。

肾属水，心属火，两者同属少阴。心藏神，肾藏志。心肾功能协调时，心火与肾水，神与志相互滋养。当心肾失调时，心阳不能下温肾脏，肾阴不能上滋心阴并制约心火。这种水火不济导致心神不安，引起多种情志障碍，从相对轻微的（烦躁、失眠、记忆力差、易恐惧）到严重的（发狂、狂怒欲杀人）。

肺为“华盖”，肾为根。肺将气和津液下输至肾，肾纳气，蒸腾津液上润肺。肾肺不和通常表现为两种主要方式：i. 肾阴不足不能滋润和清凉肺脏，引起喘息、干咳和痰中带血；ii. 肾不能纳气，引起呼吸困难、咳嗽和哮喘。**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 适用于这两种“上实下虚”的证型，孙思邈在《千金方翼方》中明确推荐其治疗肾咳。

尽管细分为肾阴和肾阳，但肾脏本质上是一个脏腑。虽然在临床实践中通常以阴虚或阳虚为主，但当肾脏的某一方面虚损时，另一方面也常会随之虚损。这在《素问·阴阳应象大论》中明确指出：“伤阴者及阳，伤阳者及阴”，以及《医宗必读》中说：“无阳则阴不生，无阴则阳不化”。补肾的一个长期原则是，肾阳虚时也应滋养肾阴，滋养肾阴时也应注意肾阳。因此，《素问·脏气法时论》记载：“阳病治阴，阴病治阳”，张景岳也回应道：“滋阴以助阳，乃培阳之巧也。”正因如此，几乎所有补益肾脏的穴位都能益肾阴肾阳。**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)**，作为肾经上补益肾脏的主要穴位，就是这一原则的体现。

当肾阳不能温煦身体、运化水液时，可能出现下肢寒冷、腰痛，以及小便频数而量多和尿失禁等泌尿系统症状。**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 在治疗阳痿、遗精和房劳（房事过度所致的精气耗损）中的应用，强调了肾与性欲和性功能的密切关系。

根据《素问·宣明五气论》记载：“肾主蛰而为精之处，其华在发，其充在骨”。肾经正经、络脉和经筋都上行通过脊柱，任何肾虚证型都可能导致筋、肌、骨的失养，特别是在肾的“居所”——腰部。**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 长期以来用于治疗肾虚腰痛，也主治足跟痛，足跟痛可能由肾虚、外伤或痹痛引起。在治疗足跟痛时，针刺**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 应引发出向足跟区域强烈放射的感传。

冲脉和任脉都受肾的滋养并根植于肾，《素问·热病论》记载：“胞脉者，系于肾”。**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 可用于治疗肾虚导致这些奇经失调引起的月经不调，但值得注意的是，这是一个现代主治，且在任何主要经典文献中都没有妇科主治或组合。

最后，**[KID3 太溪](/point/KID3.md)** 在《回阳九针歌》中被引用于治疗亡阳证，特点是昏厥、恶寒、四肢厥逆、口唇青紫等。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 输穴和原穴，因此是补益任何肾虚（阴、阳或气）的主要穴位：慢性低热性咽喉痛，排便困难，因津液不足引起的便秘，头痛，眩晕，慢性牙痛，牙齿松动，耳鸣，耳聋，阳痿，遗精，房劳，下肢寒冷，小便频数而量多，尿失禁，腰痛，足跟痛。
* 调和肾与肝、心、肺的关系：头痛，眩晕，失眠，多梦，记忆力差，喘息，干咳，痰中带血，呼吸困难，咳嗽，哮喘。

### 一、 核心地位：肾脏原气的“调节器”

- **定位与解剖**：内踝尖与跟腱之间的凹陷处。深层布有**胫神经**。
    
- **科研身份**：它是足少阴肾经的**输穴**（五行属土）和**原穴**。在现代研究中，它被视为调节神经-内分泌-免疫（NEI）网络的重要靶点。
    

---

### 二、 现代科研的三大支柱机理

#### 1. 脑中枢机制：调理“健忘”与“认知障碍”

这是目前太溪穴最火的研究方向（基于 fMRI 磁共振成像技术）。

- **激活海马体与 DMN 网络**：多项研究发现，针刺太溪穴能特异性激活**海马体（Hippocampus）**、前扣带回和额叶等与记忆、认知密切相关的脑区。
    
- **MCI（轻度认知障碍）的治疗潜力**：针对阿尔茨海默病或 MCI 患者，针刺太溪可调节“默认模式网络（DMN）”的连通性。简单说，就是通过刺激脚踝部的穴位，远程重塑大脑的认知环路。这为中医“肾主骨生髓，脑为髓之海”提供了现代神经科学证据。
    

#### 2. 下丘脑-肾轴：调节慢性肾病（CKD）

在针对肾损伤（如慢性肾小球肾炎）的动物实验中，太溪穴展现了精准的调控力：

- **PVN（室旁核）调节**：研究发现，电针太溪能削弱**下丘脑室旁核（PVN）**神经元的过度活动。PVN 是调节内分泌和自主神经的核心枢纽。
    
- **抑制炎症因子**：通过该轴线，能显著降低血清中的促炎因子（如 $IL-6$, $TNF-\alpha$）以及尿蛋白、肌酐（Cre）水平。
    
- **抗纤维化**：研究证实太溪穴能下调肾组织中 **TGF-β1** 的表达，从而减缓肾间质纤维化的过程。
    

#### 3. 循环系统：降压与血管保护

太溪常与太冲（LR3）配伍治疗高血压：

- **NO（一氧化氮）机制**：针刺太溪可提升血管内皮的一氧化氮水平，促进血管舒张，达到降压效果。
    
- **植物神经调节**：它能通过调节交感/副交感神经的平衡，缓解高血压带来的头晕、耳鸣等临床症状。
    

---

### 三、 实际体现：临床应用的“质感”

作为学生，在临床操作太溪穴时，可以从以下三个“现代视角”去观察：

|**应用场景**|**实际体现（机理）**|
|---|---|
|**生殖与内分泌**|用于**围绝经期综合征**。研究显示其能调节下丘脑-垂体-性腺轴（HPG），改善激素水平，缓解潮热盗汗。|
|**足跟痛与下肢循环**|局部针刺可刺激**胫神经**支。现代解剖显示，太溪位于胫后动、静脉附近，通过局部轴突反射改善足底血供。|
|**“补法”的物理学**|临床常用**艾灸太溪**。现代热学研究发现，太溪穴对红外辐射有特殊的敏感性，艾灸的热效应能更深层地通过神经传导至盆腔脏器。|

---

### 四、 进阶理解：与足三里的“联动”

马秋富教授的研究揭示了足三里的抗炎通路，而针对太溪的研究则更多揭示了**“虚证调节”**的逻辑。

- **足三里**更像是一个“强力调节开关”，用于对抗急性炎症和代谢紊乱。
    
- **太溪**则更像是一个“能量补给与系统修复器”，通过脑中枢的长程调节，修复慢性损耗。
    

### 学习建议

在练习针刺太溪时，注意**“深刺”**的重要性。

由于胫神经位置较深，临床上若要达到改善认知或调节肾轴的效果，往往需要针尖穿过深筋膜，产生明显的酸、胀并放射至足底的感觉（即“得气”）。这种深层刺激正是激活“脑-肾轴”神经信号的生物学前提。


关于“太溪穴（KI3）激活海马体”的研究，是近年来针灸神经影像学（Neuroimaging）领域的重点。这些研究通过**功能性磁共振成像（fMRI）**技术，为中医“肾主骨生髓，通于脑”的理论提供了具象的生物学证据。

以下是此类实验中常见的核心细节、设计思路及科学发现：

---

### 1. 实验对象与对照设计

为了验证太溪穴对海马体的特异性影响，研究者通常采用以下设计：

- **受试人群**：通常分为三组：**健康志愿者**、**轻度认知障碍（MCI）患者**、以及**阿尔茨海默病（AD）患者**。
    
- **对照位点**：通常选取非穴位点（Sham Point，即不在经络上的皮肤点）作为对照，以排除单纯皮肤针刺刺激带来的干扰。
    
- **针刺手法**：要求产生**“得气”**感（酸、麻、胀、重），因为实验证明，只有“得气”才能诱发显著的中枢神经反应。
    

---

### 2. 关键观察指标：ReHo 与 ALFF

在 fMRI 实验中，研究者主要观察大脑中的两个参数：

- **ReHo（局部一致性）**：反映大脑某个区域神经元活动的同步性。太溪穴被证实能显著提高海马区神经元的 ReHo 值，说明该区域的神经细胞活动变得更加“整齐划一”。
    
- **ALFF（低频振幅）**：反映神经元活动的强度。针刺太溪后，海马体及周边的内嗅皮层（Entorhinal Cortex）的 ALFF 值通常会升高，暗示能量代谢和神经元兴奋性增强。
    

---

### 3. 具体实验发现：海马体与默认模式网络（DMN）

海马体并不是孤立工作的，它是大脑**默认模式网络（DMN）**的核心节点。太溪穴的影响主要体现在以下三个细节：

#### A. 边缘系统的激活

多项研究（如中国中医科学院等团队的研究）显示，针刺太溪穴能即时激活**海马回（Hippocampal Gyrus）**和**旁海马回**。这两个区域是处理近期记忆和空间定向的核心。

#### B. 功能连接（Functional Connectivity）的重建

对于 MCI 患者，其海马体与大脑其他区域（如前额叶、后扣带回）的信号联系是断裂或减弱的。

- **实验细节**：通过长期的针刺治疗（如每周 3 次，持续 4-8 周），fMRI 显示海马体与前额叶之间的**信号传导相干性增强**。
    
- **临床意义**：这种连接的增强直接对应着患者记忆量表（MMSE/MoCA）分数的提升。
    

#### C. 对侧与同侧效应

研究发现，针刺太溪穴对大脑的激活具有一定的**交叉效应**。例如针刺右侧太溪穴，往往能观察到左侧海马体的信号增强更加明显，这与人体神经传导的交叉投射规律相吻合。

---

### 4. 分子层面的“辅助证据”

除了影像学，实验室研究（动物实验）进一步揭示了海马体被激活后的化学变化：

- **BDNF（脑源性神经营养因子）**：针刺太溪能提高海马区 BDNF 的水平。这就像是给神经元施肥，促进突触再生。
    
- **Aβ 蛋白沉积减少**：在 AD 模型大鼠中，长期刺激太溪穴能显著减少海马区类淀粉样蛋白（Aβ）的沉积，从而保护神经元不被毒害。
    

---

### 5. 临床实操启示：为什么“深度”很重要？

在这些 fMRI 实验中，研究者发现了一个有趣的物理细节：

刺激强度与脑区反应成正比。

太溪穴下方紧贴胫神经。实验中，如果针尖只是浅刺在皮肤层，海马体的激活非常微弱；只有当针尖深入并产生向足底放射的电感（触及神经分支或深层筋膜）时，海马体的血氧水平（BOLD 信号）才会产生剧烈波动。

---

### 总结

“太溪激活海马体”的实验细节证明了：**太溪穴不是直接“补”了大脑，而是通过外周神经信号的传入，重新唤醒了大脑中负责记忆的核心环路。** 这对于你作为针灸学生来说，意味着在治疗失眠、健忘或老年认知障碍时，太溪穴的**针感传导**是决定疗效的关键，必须通过精准的定位和手法，诱发出那股通向大脑的“经气”。
