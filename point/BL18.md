### Liver Shu GĀNSHŪ
![[BL-18 肝俞.png|250]]
- Back-Shu point of the Liver

#### Location

1.5 cun lateral to the lower border of the spinous process of the ninth thoracic vertebra (T9).

#### Location Notes

Locate at the visible highest point of the paraspinal muscles.

#### Needling

Oblique insertion towards the spine, 0.5 to 1 cun, or transverse-oblique insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.  
Caution: perpendicular needling or oblique needling away from the spine carries a substantial risk of causing a pneumothorax.

#### Actions

- Spreads Liver qi
- Regulates and nourishes Liver blood
- Pacifies wind
- Cools fire and clears damp-heat
- Benefits the eyes and sinews

#### Indications

- Distention and pain of the lateral costal region, epigastric pain, abdominal (ji ju) masses, focal distention, hypogastric fullness and pain, lower abdominal pain, abdominal cramps, shan disorder.
- Fullness of the chest, cough with chest pain, cough with fullness of the lateral costal region and inability to catch the breath, shortness of breath, jaundice, dry mouth.
- Much anger, mania-depression, epilepsy.
- Coughing blood, spitting blood, vomiting blood, nosebleed.
- Blurred vision, redness of the eyes, night blindness, excessive lacrimation, redness pain and itching of the inner canthus, superficial visual obstruction, upward staring eyes, dizziness, visual dizziness, pain of the nose, pain of the supraorbital region.
- Rigidity of the neck and spine, pain of the spine, lumbar pain, pain of the neck and shoulders, lockjaw, opisthotonos, cramps, pain of the sinews, tetany.

#### Commentary

Gānshū BL-18 is the back-shu point of the Liver, where the qi of the Liver emanates from the interior to the body surface, and like all the back-shu points (especially those of the yin zang) Gānshū BL-18 has a strong action on regulating and tonifying its corresponding zangfu at the deepest level. The principal functions of the Liver are to maintain the free flow of qi, store blood, dominate the sinews and open into the eyes. By spreading Liver qi, nourishing and cooling Liver blood, clearing damp-heat and pacifying wind, Gānshū BL-18 is able to regulate all these aspects of the Liver function.

According to Master Dan-xi of the Jin-Yuan dynasty "The Liver governs spreading". This refers to the Liver's function of ensuring the free and easy flow of qi throughout the body and assisting the normal qi movement of all the zangfu, for example the descending of the Lung and Stomach qi and the ascending of the Spleen qi. Stagnation of Liver qi is considered the pre-eminent pathological pattern of the Liver since it is frequently the starting point of other Liver patterns (for example the transformation of Liver qi into fire) and it commonly accompanies any other pattern of Liver disharmony. The ability of Gānshū BL-18 to promote the free flow of Liver qi is reflected in various ways. Stagnant qi in the upper, middle or lower jiao may give rise to distention and pain of the chest, lateral costal region, epigastrium and hypogastrium as well as to shan disorder. According to the Spiritual Pivot[20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "with anger the qi rebels upwards and accumulates in the chest". If Liver qi or Liver fire invade the Lung there will be cough with chest pain, coughing of blood, cough with fullness of the lateral costal region and inability to catch the breath, or shortness of breath. According to Correcting Errors in Medicine by Wang Qing-ren of the Qing dynasty "qi is without form and thus unable to congeal into masses; the development of masses requires form in the shape of blood". If stagnation of qi leads to blood stasis, therefore, there will be abdominal masses. If Liver qi stagnation inhibits the function of the Gall Bladder in secreting bile there will be damp-heat of the Liver and Gall Bladder and consequent jaundice. According to the Spiritual Pivot[21](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "The Liver stores blood, the blood is the residence of the ethereal soul (hun); when Liver qi is deficient there is fear, when excess there is anger". If stagnation of qi is pronounced, and especially if it transforms to fire, there will be emotional manifestations such as anger, mania-depression, and even epilepsy.

The increasing pressure of prolonged qi stagnation may easily transform to fire. According to the Essential Questions[22](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "Anger will cause the qi to surge upwards; if it is extreme there is vomiting of blood". Since the Liver stores the blood, the heat of Liver fire or Liver yang may easily transmit to the blood and, given the tendency of the Liver qi to rise excessively, cause reckless upward movement of blood leading to symptoms such as vomiting blood, nosebleed, and spitting and coughing blood.

When Liver blood or Liver yin are insufficient, they will be unable to nourish and moisten those regions of the body dominated by the Liver and irrigated by Liver blood, principally the eyes and the sinews. It is a special property of the back-shu points of the zang that they nourish and regulate their corresponding sense organs. According to the Spiritual Pivot[23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "Liver qi opens into the eyes; when the Liver is in harmony, the eyes are able to distinguish the five colours", whilst the Essential Questions[24](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) says "The Liver receives blood so there is sight". Insufficiency of Liver blood or Liver yin may lead to disorders such as blurred vision, night blindness, lacrimation and dizziness. If heat from Liver fire or Liver yang rises to disrupt the eyes, there will be redness, itching and pain. If Liver fire leads to the stirring of wind, there will be upward staring of the eyes. The Investigation into Points Along the Channels therefore states that Gānshū BL-18 is indicated for "all diseases of the eyes pertaining to the Liver". According to the Essential Questions "The Liver dominates the sinews of the entire body"[25](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references), and "When a person sleeps the blood returns to the Liver"[26](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references). If Liver blood or Liver yin fail to nourish the sinews and thus maintain their softness and flexibility, there may be chronic stiffness and contraction, cramps or pain of the sinews. These symptoms may be especially pronounced both when the body is exhausted and the blood is less effective at nourishing, or during or following sleep or inactivity when the blood returns to the Liver and less is available to circulate throughout the body. This kind of stiffness, contraction, cramping or pain will be even more pronounced when there is concurrent stagnation of Liver qi, or when deficiency of yin or blood leads to stirring up of Liver wind.

According to sayings of Chinese medicine "The Liver governs uprising" and "Anger makes the Liver qi rise to the neck and shoulders". Stagnation of Liver qi or uprising of Liver yang therefore easily lead to stiffness and pain of the neck and shoulders. If Liver fire or high fever consume Liver yin and generate wind there may be such acute spasmodic symptoms as opisthotonos, lockjaw, rigidity of the neck and spine and tetany. Gānshū BL-18 is indicated for all of these symptoms.

Finally the Illustrated Supplement to the Classic of Categories states that Gānshū BL-18 is one of five points ([Fèishū BL-13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-13), [Xīnshū BL-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-15), Gānshū BL-18, [Píshū BL-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-20) and [Shènshū BL-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-23)) which "drain heat from the five zang".

#### Summary of clinical application

- Back-shu point of the Liver for all Liver patterns, whether deficient or excess, affecting the chest, Lung, lateral costal region and abdomen: fullness of the chest, cough with chest pain or fullness of the lateral costal region, inability to catch the breath, shortness of breath, distention and pain of the lateral costal region, epigastric pain, abdominal masses, lower abdominal pain and cramps, shan disorder, jaundice.
- Clears Liver and Heart fire: ‘much anger’, mania-depression, epilepsy, coughing, spitting or vomiting blood, nosebleed.
- Benefits the eyes: blurred vision, redness of the eyes, night blindness, excessive lacrimation, upward staring eyes.
- Benefits the sinews and spine: rigidity of the neck and spine, pain of the spine, lumbar pain, pain of the neck and shoulders, lockjaw, opisthotonos, cramps, pain of the sinews.

### **[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** (Gānshū)

* **英文名称：** Liver Shu
* **中文名称：** 肝俞
* **国际代码：** BL-18
* **穴位属性：** **[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** 是肝的背俞穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于第九胸椎（T9）棘突下缘旁开1.5寸。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 位于脊柱旁肌肉的最高点。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

斜刺，向脊柱方向0.5至1寸，或横斜刺1至1.5寸。

* **注意 (Caution):** 垂直针刺或向远离脊柱方向斜刺有很大风险引起气胸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 疏泄肝气
* 调理滋养肝血
* 平息肝风
* 清泻肝火，清利湿热
* 明目，利筋

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

胁肋部胀痛，胃脘痛，腹部癥瘕积聚，痞块，少腹胀满疼痛，小腹疼痛，腹部痉挛，疝气。
胸中胀满，咳嗽伴胸痛，咳嗽伴胁肋部胀满呼吸困难，气短，黄疸，口干。
多怒，癫狂，癫痫。
咳血，咯血，呕血，鼻出血。
视物模糊，眼睛红赤，夜盲，泪液过多，内眼角红肿疼痛瘙痒，外障眼病，眼睛上视，眩晕，视物眩晕，鼻痛，眶上区疼痛。
颈项和脊柱强直，脊柱疼痛，腰痛，颈肩疼痛，牙关紧闭，角弓反张，痉挛，筋脉疼痛，破伤风。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** 是肝的背俞穴，肝气从内向体表输布。像所有背俞穴一样（特别是阴脏的背俞穴），**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** 在最深层次上调理和补益其相应的脏腑。肝的主要功能是维持气机疏泄，藏血，主筋，开窍于目。通过疏泄肝气、滋养凉血、清利湿热和平息肝风，**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** 能够调理肝的所有这些功能。

根据金元时期丹溪大师的说法：“肝主疏泄”。这指的是肝脏在确保全身气机自由顺畅运行，并协助所有脏腑正常气机运动的功能，例如肺气和胃气的下降以及脾气的上升。肝气郁结被认为是肝脏最重要的病理证型，因为它常常是其他肝脏证型（例如肝气化火）的起点，并且常伴随肝脏失调的任何其他证型。**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** 促进肝气自由流动的能力以多种方式体现。上、中、下焦气滞可能引起胸、胁肋部、胃脘和少腹的胀痛以及疝气。
根据《灵枢·本神》记载：“怒则气上逆，积于胸。”如果肝气或肝火侵犯肺脏，则会出现咳嗽伴胸痛、咳血、咳嗽伴胁肋部胀满呼吸困难，或气短。根据清代王清任《医林改错》记载：“气无形不能凝结成块；结块的形成需要血的形质。”如果气滞导致血瘀，则会出现腹部肿块。如果肝气郁滞抑制胆囊分泌胆汁的功能，则会出现肝胆湿热和继发性黄疸。
根据《灵枢·本神》记载：“肝藏血，血者，魂之居也；肝气虚则恐，实则怒。”如果气滞明显，特别是化火时，则会出现情志表现，如愤怒、癫狂，甚至癫痫。

长期气滞的压力增加很容易化火。根据《素问·生气通天论》记载：“怒则气上冲；甚则呕血。”由于肝藏血，肝火或肝阳的热邪很容易传导至血分，并且鉴于肝气过度上亢的趋势，可能造成血液妄行上冲，导致呕血、鼻出血、咯血和咳血等症状。

当肝血或肝阴不足时，它们将无法滋养和濡润肝脏所主导和肝血所灌注的身体区域，主要是眼睛和筋。脏腑背俞穴的一个特殊特性是它们滋养和调理其相应的七窍。根据《灵枢·脉度》记载：“肝气通于目；肝和则目能知五色”，而《素问·经脉别论》记载：“肝受血而能视”。肝血或肝阴不足可能导致视物模糊、夜盲、流泪和眩晕等疾病。如果肝火或肝阳热邪上扰眼睛，则会出现红赤、瘙痒和疼痛。如果肝火导致风动，则会出现眼睛上视。《循经考穴编》因此指出，**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** 主治“一切肝之目疾”。根据《素问·素问·经脉别论》记载：“肝主身之筋膜”，又曰：“人卧血归于肝。”如果肝血或肝阴不能滋养筋，从而维持其柔软和灵活性，则可能出现慢性僵硬和挛缩、痉挛或筋痛。这些症状可能尤其明显，当身体疲惫、血液滋养作用减弱时，或在睡眠期间或不活动之后（此时血液回肝，循环至全身的量减少）时。当同时伴有肝气郁滞，或阴虚血虚导致肝风内动时，这种僵硬、挛缩、痉挛或疼痛将更加明显。

根据中医俗语，“肝主升发”和“怒则肝气上冲颈肩”。因此，肝气郁滞或肝阳上亢很容易导致颈肩僵硬疼痛。如果肝火或高热耗伤肝阴并产生风，则可能出现急性痉挛症状，如角弓反张、牙关紧闭、颈项和脊柱强直以及破伤风。**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** 主治所有这些症状。

最后，《类经图翼》记载，**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)** 是“泻五脏之热”的五个穴位之一（**[BL13 肺俞](point/BL13.md)**、**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)**、**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)**、**[BL20 脾俞](point/BL20.md)** 和**[BL23 肾俞](point/BL23.md)**）。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 肝的背俞穴，治疗所有肝脏证型，无论是虚证或实证，影响胸、肺、胁肋部和腹部：胸中胀满，咳嗽伴胸痛或胁肋部胀满，呼吸困难，气短，胁肋部胀痛，胃脘痛，腹部癥瘕积聚，小腹疼痛和痉挛，疝气，黄疸。
* 清泻肝火和心火：多怒，癫狂，癫痫，咳血，咯血，呕血，鼻出血。
* 明目：视物模糊，眼睛红赤，夜盲，泪液过多，眼睛上视。
* 利筋骨：颈项和脊柱强直，脊柱疼痛，腰痛，颈肩疼痛，牙关紧闭，角弓反张，痉挛，筋脉疼痛。
