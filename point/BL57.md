### Support the Mountain CHÉNGSHĀN
![[BL-57 承山.png|250]]
- Ma Dan-yang Heavenly Star point

#### Location

On the lower leg, in the depression formed below the bellies of the gastrocnemius muscle when the muscle is flexed, approximately 8 cun distal to [Wěizhōng BL-40](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-40), i.e. midway between [Wěizhōng BL-40](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-40) and [Kūnlún BL-60](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-60).

#### Location Notes

Run your finger upwards from the Achilles tendon along the midline until it falls into the depression formed between the two origins of the gastrocnemius muscle bellies; this depression may be easier to palpate if you ask the patient to press the ball of their foot against the resistance of your hand.

#### Needling

Perpendicular or oblique insertion directed proximally or distally, 1 to 1.5 cun.

#### Actions

- Relaxes the sinews, activates the channel and alleviates pain
- Benefits the calf and heel
- Treats haemorrhoids

#### Indications

- Haemorrhoids, bleeding haemorrhoids, swollen and painful haemorrhoids, constipation, prolapse of the rectum.
- Pain and stiffness of the lumbar region, sciatica, difficulty sitting and standing, inability to stand for long, cramps, pain and contraction of the sinews, leg qi with swollen knee, heavy legs, flaccidity and weakness of the leg, pain of the heel, heat sensation in the soles with inability to stand for long.
- Shivering, malaria, sudden turmoil disorder, nosebleed, sore throat, heat in the head.

#### Commentary

Chéngshān BL-57 was included by Ma Dan-yang, the great physician of the Jin dynasty, among the 'eleven heavenly star points'[67](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references), his grouping of the most vital acupuncture points. Clinically, Chéngshān BL-57 is used in three principal situations: i. haemorrhoids, ii. contraction and pain of the calf, and iii. heel pain. The Bladder divergent channel winds around the anal region. Classically, Chéngshān BL-57 has been regarded as the primary distal point in the treatment of all kinds of haemorrhoids. For example, the Song to Keep Up Your Sleeve says "for the five types of haemorrhoids due to hot blood, select Chéngshān BL-57 and await disappearance of the disease without a trace", whilst Ma Dan-yang recommends it for " ... haemorrhoids and difficulty in defecation ... ".

Pain and contraction of the calf may be due to traumatic injury, painful obstruction, or the sequela of sudden turmoil disorder, but is most commonly encountered in the context of deficiency of Liver blood. The Essential Questions[68](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) says "When a person sleeps the blood returns to the Liver". Pain and contraction of the calf therefore tends to occur at night when the body is at rest, the blood returns to the Liver, and the relative insufficiency of circulating blood is unable to nourish and soften the sinews and muscles of the extremities. This symptom is more commonly encountered in those who tend to suffer from blood deficiency, particularly women because of the loss of blood entailed in menstruation, and the elderly because of the inevitable decline of essence and blood inherent in ageing. Although having no action on the Liver blood itself, Chéngshān BL-57 is an important local point in the treatment of this disorder.

Heel pain may be due to traumatic injury, painful obstruction or Kidney deficiency. Chéngshān BL-57 is an important proximal point for this disorder and may be combined with other suitable points in the treatment of heel pain of whatever aetiology.

Finally, Ma Dan-yang and other classical sources emphasise the use of Chéngshān BL-57 in the treatment of lumbar pain. Clinically it is now more commonly used for sciatic pain radiating to the calf region.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Important distal point for haemorrhoids.
- Commonly used for spasm and pain of the calf muscles, whether due to traumatic injury, sprain, sciatica or Liver blood deficiency.
- For pain of the heel.

### **[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** (Chéngshān)

* **英文名称：** Support the Mountain
* **中文名称：** 承山
* **国际代码：** BL-57
* **穴位属性：** **[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 是马丹阳天星穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于小腿部，腓肠肌收缩时，其肌腹下方形成的凹陷中，距**[BL40 委中](point/BL40.md)** 下方约8寸，即在**[BL40 委中](point/BL40.md)** 与**[BL60 昆仑](point/BL60.md)** 之间中点。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 从跟腱向上沿中线滑动手指，直到其落入腓肠肌肌腹两个起点之间形成的凹陷中；如果请患者将足掌抵住您的手施加阻力，此凹陷可能更容易触及。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

垂直或斜刺，针尖向近端或远端，1至1.5寸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 舒筋活络，止痛
* 宣利小腿和足跟
* 治疗痔疮

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

痔疮，痔疮出血，痔疮肿痛，便秘，脱肛。
腰部疼痛僵硬，坐骨神经痛，坐卧困难，不能久站，痉挛，筋脉疼痛挛缩，腿气伴膝关节肿胀，腿部沉重，腿部痿软无力，足跟疼痛，足底热感不能久站。
战栗，疟疾，霍乱吐泻，鼻出血，咽喉疼痛，头热。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 被金代名医马丹阳列入其“十一穴天星穴”中，是他认为人体最重要的穴位组合。临床上，**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 主要用于三种情况：i. 痔疮，ii. 小腿挛缩疼痛，以及 iii. 足跟痛。膀胱经别绕行肛门区域。传统上，**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 被认为是治疗各种痔疮的主要远端穴位。例如，《袖珍赋》记载：“热血五痔，但取**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)**，坐待病消无影踪”，而马丹阳推荐其治疗“……痔疮和排便困难……”。

小腿疼痛和挛缩可能由于外伤、痹痛或霍乱吐泻后遗症引起，但最常见于肝血不足的情况。《素问·血气形志篇》记载：“人卧血归于肝”。因此，小腿疼痛和挛缩倾向于在夜间身体休息时发生，此时血液回肝，循环血液相对不足，不能滋养和濡润四肢的筋和肌肉。此症状在血虚倾向者中更常见，特别是女性（因月经失血）和老年人（因衰老导致的精血不可避免的衰退）。尽管**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 本身对肝血没有直接作用，但它是治疗此症的重要局部穴位。

足跟痛可能由于外伤、痹痛或肾虚引起。**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 是治疗此症的重要近端穴位，可与治疗各种病因的足跟痛的其他合适穴位联合使用。

最后，马丹阳和其他经典文献强调**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 在治疗腰痛中的应用。临床上，它现在更常用于治疗放射至小腿区域的坐骨神经痛。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 治疗痔疮的重要远端穴位。
* 常用于小腿肌肉痉挛和疼痛，无论是由于外伤、扭伤、坐骨神经痛还是肝血不足。
* 治疗足跟痛。
