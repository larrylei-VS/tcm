## ST-37 SHÀNGJÙXŪ

### Upper Great Void 上巨虛
![[ST-37 上巨虚.png|250]]
- Lower He-Sea point of the Large Intestine
- Point of the Sea of Blood

#### Location

On the lower leg, 3 cun inferior to [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36), one finger-breadth lateral to the anterior crest of the tibia.

#### Location Notes

Locate one handbreadth below [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36).

#### Needling

Perpendicular or oblique insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.

#### Actions

- Regulates the intestines and transforms stagnation
- Clears damp heat and alleviates diarrhoea and dysenteric disorder
- Regulates the Spleen and Stomach
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications

- Borborygmus, diarrhoea, diarrhoea containing undigested food, diarrhoea due to attack of cold in winter, dysenteric disorder, heat in the Large Intestine, cold of the Large Intestine, deficiency of Large Intestine qi, intestinal abscess, cutting pain of the intestines, constipation, abdominal pain, abdominal distention, periumbilical pain with inability to stand for long, dark urine.
- Heat in the Stomach, deficiency and weakness of the Spleen and Stomach, insufficiency of zang qi, consumption, shortness of breath, dyspnoea with difficulty in walking, qi rushes up to the chest, fullness of the chest and lateral costal region, swelling of the face.
- Mania, aversion to the sound of people talking.
- Hemiplegia, leg qi, numbness and painful obstruction of the lower limb, coldness and pain of the bone marrow, weakness of the leg, contraction and pain of the front of the shin, swelling of the knee.

#### Commentary

The Yellow Emperor's Inner Classic stated "The Large and Small intestines fall under the influence of the Stomach"[34](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) and classified Shàngjùxū ST-37 as the lower he-sea point of the Large Intestine. The three yang channels of the arm (Large Intestine, Small Intestine and Sanjiao) are unique in that whilst the channels traverse the upper jiao, their respective fu belong to the lower jiao. Thus although the Spiritual Pivot states that the "He-sea points treat disorders of the internal fu", the he-sea points of these three channels ([Qūchí LI-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-11), [Xiǎohǎi SI-8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-8), and [Tiānjǐng SJ-10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-10) respectively) have relatively little action on their related fu. The Large and Small Intestines and the Sanjiao, however, each have a lower he-sea point (Shàngjùxū ST-37, [Xiàjùxū ST-39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-39) and [Wěiyáng BL-39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-39)) which predominantly treat disorders of their respective fu.

Shàngjùxū ST-37 is an important point in the treatment of a wide range of intestinal disorders. It is especially effective in the treatment of diarrhoea and dysenteric disorder, whether due to deficiency, cold, dampness, or damp-heat, and in recent times has been much used in the treatment of bacillary dysentery. Shàngjùxū ST-37 is also used in the treatment of stagnation and obstruction of the intestines, giving rise to distention and pain of the abdomen, constipation and intestinal abscess. This comprehensive ability of Shàngjùxū ST-37 to regulate the intestines was referred to in the earliest classics. For example, the Systematic Classic of Acupuncture and Moxibustion recommends Shàngjùxū ST-37 for "heat in the Large Intestine, diarrhoea containing undigested food and borborygmus", whilst the tenth century classic Sagelike Prescriptions from the Taiping Era recommends it for insufficiency of Large Intestine qi.

Some classics ascribe qi tonifying actions to this point, for example "deficiency and weakness of Spleen and Stomach, insufficiency of zang qi, shortness of breath etc." These may be explained by the beneficial effect that firming the intestines in cases of loose stool and diarrhoea will have on the transportation and transformation function of the Spleen. It is also worth noting that acupuncture points immediately distal or proximal to very powerful points, often share some of their functions and indications. It is as though the influence of [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36) is so great that it can resonate onto Shàngjùxū ST-37.

According to the Spiritual Pivot[35](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) Shàngjùxū ST-37 is a point of the 'sea of blood'. This passage says "The Penetrating vessel is the sea of the twelve channels (i.e. the sea of blood). In the upper it is conveyed to [Dàzhù BL-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-11), and in the lower it emerges at both Shàngjùxū ST-37 and [Xiàjùxū ST-39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-39) ... When the sea of blood is in excess, there is a sensation as if the body were big; one feels disquiet, but does not know what disease there is; when the sea of blood is insufficient, one has the sensation of one's body being small; one feels reduced but does not know what could be the illness". Despite this passage it is worth noting that such indications are not found in later texts.

In common with many points of the Stomach channel, Shàngjùxū ST-37 is especially effective in treating disorders of the lower limb as a whole. This action is explained by the saying "Yangming channels are abundant in qi and blood". The ability of Shàngjùxū ST-37 to promote the flow of smooth qi and blood throughout the leg renders it effective in the treatment of hemiplegia, leg qi, weakness of the leg, swelling of the knee and all kinds of atrophy disorder and painful obstruction, whether due to excess or deficiency patterns.

Finally, the Essential Questions[36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) includes Shàngjùxū ST-37 among the eight points to clear heat from the Stomach (bilateral [Qìchōng ST-30](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-30), [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36), Shàngjùxū ST-37 and [Xiàjùxū ST-39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-39)).

#### Summary of clinical application

- Important point for the treatment of a wide range of intestinal disorders, especially diarrhoea and dysentery, whether deficient, excess, cold or hot: borborygmus, diarrhoea, dysenteric disorder, bacillary dysentery, heat in the Large Intestine, cold of the Large Intestine, deficiency of Large Intestine qi, intestinal abscess, cutting pain of the intestines, constipation, abdominal pain and distention, periumbilical pain with inability to stand for long.
- For disorders of the lower leg: hemiplegia, painful obstruction, atrophy disorder, numbness, coldness, pain, weakness, contraction and swelling.

#### Combinations

- Yellow and difficult urination: Shàngjùxū ST-37 and [Xiàjùxū ST-39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-39) (Systematic Classic).
- Bacillary dysentery: Shàngjùxū ST-37, [Tiānshū ST-25](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-25) and [Qūchí LI-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-11).

### [ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) (SHÀNGJÙXŪ)

* **英文名称：** Upper Great Void
* **中文名称：** 上巨虚
* **国际代码：** ST-37
* **穴位属性：** [ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 是大肠经的下合穴，血海穴。

#### 定位 (Location)

位于小腿部，距[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md) 下方3寸，胫骨前嵴旁开一横指。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 在[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md) 下方一横掌处定位。

#### 针刺方法 (Needling)

垂直或斜刺1至1.5寸。

#### 作用 (Actions)

* 调理肠道，化积消滞
* 清利湿热，止泻痢
* 调理脾胃
* 通经活络，止痛

#### 主治病症 (Indications)

肠鸣，腹泻，泻下完谷不化，冬月感受寒邪腹泻，痢疾，大肠有热，大肠虚寒，大肠气虚，肠痈，肠绞痛，便秘，腹痛，腹胀，脐周疼痛不能久站，小便色深。
胃热，脾胃虚弱，脏气不足，虚劳，气短，呼吸困难伴行走困难，气冲上胸，胸胁胀满，面部肿胀。
癫狂，厌恶人声。
半身不遂，腿气，下肢麻木痹痛，骨髓寒痛，腿部无力，小腿前侧挛缩疼痛，膝关节肿胀。

#### 阐释 (Commentary)

《黄帝内经》指出“大小肠皆属于胃”，并将[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 归类为大肠的下合穴。手臂的三条阳经（大肠经、小肠经和三焦经）独特之处在于，虽然经脉循行上焦，但其各自的腑却属于下焦。因此，尽管《灵枢》记载“合治内腑”，但这些经脉的合穴（分别是[LI11 曲池](/point/LI11.md)、[SI8 小海](/point/SI8.md) 和[SJ10 天井](/point/SJ10.md)）对其各自的腑作用相对较小。然而，大肠、小肠和三焦各自都有一个下合穴（[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md)、[ST39 下巨虚](/point/ST39.md) 和[BL39 委阳](/point/BL39.md)），主要治疗其各自腑的疾病。

[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 是治疗多种肠道疾病的重要穴位。它在治疗腹泻和痢疾方面特别有效，无论是由于虚证、寒证、湿证还是湿热证，近年来广泛用于治疗细菌性痢疾。[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 也用于治疗肠道积滞和阻塞，引起腹部胀痛、便秘和肠痈。[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 这种全面的调理肠道能力在最早的经典中就有提及。例如，《针灸甲乙经》推荐[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 治疗“大肠有热，泻下完谷不化，肠鸣”，而十世纪经典《太平圣惠方》则推荐其治疗大肠气虚。

一些经典著作将补气作用归因于此穴，例如“脾胃虚弱，脏气不足，气短”等。这可以用治疗便溏和腹泻时，固肠对脾脏运化功能的有益影响来解释。此外，值得注意的是，非常强大的穴位附近（或远近）的穴位，通常会分享一些它们的功能和主治。仿佛[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md) 的影响力如此之大，以至于能共振到[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md)。

根据《灵枢·血络论》记载，[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 是“血海”穴之一。该篇记载“冲脉者，十二经脉之海也（即血海）。其上出于[BL11 大杼](/point/BL11.md)，其下别于[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md)、[ST39 下巨虚](/point/ST39.md)……血海有余，令人身大，不知所苦；血海不足，令人身小，不知所病”。尽管有此段记载，但值得注意的是，此类主治在后世文献中并未提及。

与胃经的许多穴位一样，[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 在治疗整个下肢疾病方面特别有效。这一作用可以用“阳明经多气多血”的说法来解释。[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 能够促进气血在腿部的顺畅流通，使其在治疗半身不遂、腿气、腿部无力、膝关节肿胀以及各种痿证和痹证方面有效，无论是实证还是虚证。

最后，《素问·热论》将[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 列为清胃热的八个穴位之一（双侧[ST30 气冲](/point/ST30.md)、[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md)、[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 和[ST39 下巨虚](/point/ST39.md)）。

#### 临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)

* 治疗各种肠道疾病的重要穴位，特别是腹泻和痢疾，无论是虚证、实证、寒证或热证：肠鸣，腹泻，痢疾，细菌性痢疾，大肠有热，大肠虚寒，大肠气虚，肠痈，肠绞痛，便秘，腹痛腹胀，脐周疼痛不能久站。
* 治疗下肢疾病：半身不遂，痹证，痿证，麻木，寒冷，疼痛，无力，挛缩和肿胀。

#### 穴位组合 (Combinations)

* 小便黄而困难：[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md) 和[ST39 下巨虚](/point/ST39.md)（《针灸甲乙经》）。
* 细菌性痢疾：[ST37 上巨虚](/point/ST37.md)，[ST25 天枢](/point/ST25.md) 和[LI11 曲池](/point/LI11.md)。
