### Sea of Blood XUÈHǍI
![[SP-10 血海.png|250]]
#### Location

2 cun proximal to the superior border of the patella, in the tender depression on the bulge of the vastus medialis muscle, directly above [Yīnlíngquán SP-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-9).

#### Location Notes

i. Place the heel of your right palm on the inferior border of the patient's left patella, with the fingers and thumb fully extended and the thumb at 45° to the index finger. This point is found beneath the tip of the thumb, directly above [Yīnlíngquán SP-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-9); ii. Locate one patella's height (2 cun) above the superior border of the patella, in the tender depression directly above [Yīnlíngquán SP-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-9).

#### Needling

Perpendicular or oblique insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.

#### Actions

- Invigorates the blood and dispels stasis
- Cools blood
- Harmonises menstruation
- Benefits the skin

#### Indications

- Irregular menstruation, dysmenorrhoea, amenorrhoea, uterine bleeding, uterine bleeding containing clots, sudden uterine bleeding, post-partum qi and blood deficiency.
- Urticaria, eczema, erysipelas (cinnabar toxin), herpes zoster, painful hot sores, ulceration and itching of the scrotum, pain and itching of the genitals.
- Leucorrhoea, the five types of painful urinary dysfunction, qi painful urinary dysfunction, blood painful urinary dysfunction, pain of the inner aspect of the thigh.

#### Commentary

The name of Xuèhǎi SP-10 (Sea of Blood) reflects its pre-eminent role in treating various disorders at the blood level. It is generally recognised as having two principal actions: i. invigorating the blood and dispelling stasis, and ii. cooling the blood. These two actions find their expression in its ability to treat a wide range of gynaecological and dermatological disorders due either to blood stasis or to heat in the blood.

As far as gynaecology is concerned, normal menstruation depends on a number of factors including the smooth circulation of blood. Impairment of blood circulation and consequent stasis may be due to a variety of causes including stagnation or deficiency of qi, traumatic injury, haemorrhage, penetration of cold or heat, chronic disease or emotional factors, and may give rise to many different disorders of menstruation. These include dysmenorrhoea and uterine bleeding characterised by severe fixed pain and the discharge of dark or clotted blood, and amenorrhoea accompanied by distention and pain, a purple coloured tongue and a choppy pulse. By contrast, if heat enters the blood level, most frequently due to internally generated heat from the Liver or Heart or overconsumption of excessively heating foods, then two principal conditions may arise: i. the movement of blood may become reckless and overflow its bounds leading to profuse uterine bleeding, or less commonly, the heat may desiccate the blood leading to amenorrhoea. In both cases there will be signs of heat such as a red dry tongue and a rapid pulse. In fact, both the blood cooling and blood invigorating properties of Xuèhǎi SP-10 come together in its ability to treat uterine bleeding. Blood heat is the most commonly seen pattern of uterine bleeding, but as well as cooling blood, treatment must emphasise resolving stasis, since any pathological bleeding may lead to pooling and stagnation of extravasated blood. This is expressed in the Chinese medicine saying "where there is haemorrhage there is stasis". The close inter-relationship between blood heat and blood stasis is further illustrated by the fact that blood heat may condense and dry the blood giving rise to stasis, as stated in the Treatise on Epidemic Warm Febrile Disease[18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-sanjiao-channel/references) "Because latent stagnant fire evaporates the blood's fluid, the blood simmers and forms stasis".

Some authorities further attribute blood nourishing properties to Xuèhǎi SP-10 and incorporate it into prescriptions for the treatment of blood deficiency. In the light of Xuèhǎi SP-10's pre-eminence in invigorating blood, this action reflects the saying in Chinese medicine "if blood stasis is not transformed, new blood cannot be generated". This refers to situations where blood stasis has pooled outside the channels, leading directly to blood deficiency as sufficient blood is no longer available to nourish the body. This complex pattern of haemorrhage with both consequent blood stasis and blood deficiency is commonly encountered after childbirth and profuse uterine bleeding.

As far as dermatology is concerned, blood disharmony often plays a central role. The main dermatological patterns treated by Xuèhǎi SP-10 are heat in the blood level characterised by red lesions, and blood stasis characterised by purple lesions. Xuèhǎi SP-10 may also be used for the concomitant blood deficiency and stasis seen for example in eczema with thickening of the skin (lichenification) or post-herpetic neuralgia. Xuèhǎi SP-10 may also be used to treat the manifestations of wind in skin diseases, the most important of which is intolerable itching. This illustrates the Chinese medicine saying "to treat wind first treat the blood; once blood moves wind will be dispelled".

Although the action of Xuèhǎi SP-10 on invigorating and cooling blood is classically confined to the two principal areas of gynaecology and dermatology, there are in fact relatively few acupuncture points able to treat the blood directly. For this reason, its application may be extended to any disorder in the body with these pathologies. Thus the Song of the Primary Points of the Fourteen Channels says "Xuèhǎi SP-10 can treat all blood diseases".

#### Summary of clinical application

- One of the main points to move blood stasis and cool blood heat, principally in menstrual and skin disorders: irregular menstruation, dysmenorrhoea, amenorrhoea, uterine bleeding, uterine bleeding containing clots, urticaria, eczema, erysipelas (cinnabar toxin), herpes zoster, painful hot sores. “Xuèhǎi SP-10 can treat all blood diseases” (Song of the Primary Points of the Fourteen Channels).
- Ascribed some action of nourishing blood, especially when allied with blood stasis: “If blood stasis is not transformed, new blood cannot be generated”.

### **[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** (XUÈHǍI)

* **英文名称：** Sea of Blood
* **中文名称：** 血海
* **国际代码：** SP-10

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于髌骨上缘上方2寸，股内侧肌隆起处的压痛凹陷中，**[SP9 阴陵泉](/point/SP9.md)** 直上方。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 将您的右掌跟放在患者左髌骨下缘，手指和拇指完全伸展，拇指与食指成45°角。此穴位于拇指尖下方，**[SP9 阴陵泉](/point/SP9.md)** 直上方；
    * 也可以定位在髌骨上缘上方一髌骨高（2寸）处，位于**[SP9 阴陵泉](/point/SP9.md)** 直上方的压痛凹陷中。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

垂直或斜刺1至1.5寸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 活血化瘀
* 凉血
* 调经
* 润泽皮肤

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

月经不调，痛经，闭经，崩漏（子宫出血），崩漏夹血块，暴崩，产后气血不足。
荨麻疹，湿疹，丹毒（赤游丹），带状疱疹，痈疮疼痛，阴囊溃烂瘙痒，生殖器疼痛瘙痒。
带下，五淋（小便涩痛），气淋，血淋，大腿内侧疼痛。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** (Sea of Blood) 的名称反映了其在治疗血分各种疾病中的卓越作用。它通常被认为具有两个主要作用：i. 活血化瘀，ii. 凉血。这两个作用体现在它能够治疗多种由血瘀或血热引起的妇科和皮肤病。

就妇科而言，正常的月经取决于多种因素，包括血液的顺畅循环。血液循环受损并因此产生的瘀滞可能由多种原因引起，包括气滞或气虚、外伤、出血、寒热侵袭、慢性病或情志因素，并可能导致多种不同的月经失调。这些包括痛经和子宫出血，其特点是剧烈固定性疼痛和排出暗色或有血块的血液，以及伴有胀痛、舌质紫暗、脉涩的闭经。
相比之下，如果热邪进入血分，最常见是由于肝火或心火内生，或过量食用辛辣温热食物，则可能出现两种主要情况：i. 血液妄行，溢出脉外，导致大量子宫出血；或较不常见的是，热邪耗伤血液，导致闭经。在这两种情况下，都会有舌红口干、脉数等热象。实际上，**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** 的凉血和活血作用共同体现在它治疗子宫出血的能力。血热是子宫出血最常见的证型，但除了凉血外，治疗必须强调化瘀，因为任何病理性出血都可能导致溢出血液的积聚和瘀滞。这在中医俗语中表达为“离经之血，则为瘀血”。血热与血瘀之间密切的相互关系进一步体现在血热可能凝结和耗伤血液，导致瘀滞，正如《温病条辨》所言：“伏邪之火，煎熬津液，血亦熬成瘀也”。

一些医家进一步将补血作用归因于**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)**，并将其纳入治疗血虚的处方中。鉴于**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** 在活血方面的卓越地位，这一作用反映了中医俗语“瘀血不去，新血不生”。这指的是瘀血积聚于脉外，直接导致血虚，因为充足的血液不再能滋养身体。这种出血伴随血瘀和血虚的复杂证型常在产后和大量子宫出血后遇到。

就皮肤科而言，血分失调常扮演核心角色。**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** 治疗的主要皮肤病证型是血热（以红色皮损为特征）和血瘀（以紫色皮损为特征）。**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** 也可用于治疗伴随的血虚和血瘀，例如伴有皮肤增厚（苔藓样变）的湿疹或带状疱疹后神经痛。**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** 也可用于治疗皮肤病中风邪的表现，其中最重要的是难以忍受的瘙痒。这说明了中医俗语“治风先治血；血行风自灭”的道理。

尽管 **[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** 活血凉血的作用在经典中主要限于妇科和皮肤科这两个领域，但实际上，能够直接治疗血分的穴位相对较少。因此，其应用可扩展到身体任何部位具有这些病理的疾病。因此，《十四经穴歌》中说：“**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** 能治一切血证”。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 活血化瘀和凉血热的主要穴位，主要用于月经和皮肤病：月经不调，痛经，闭经，崩漏（子宫出血），崩漏夹血块，荨麻疹，湿疹，丹毒，带状疱疹，痈疮疼痛。
* “**[SP10 血海](/point/SP10.md)** 能治一切血证”（《十四经穴歌》）。
* 具有补血作用，特别是与血瘀并存时：“瘀血不去，新血不生”。
