### Gall bladder Shu DǍNSHŪ
![[BL-19 胆俞.png|250]]
- Back-Shu point of the Gall Bladder

#### Location

1.5 cun lateral to the lower border of the spinous process of the tenth thoracic vertebra (T10).

#### Location Notes

Locate at the visible highest point of the paraspinal muscles.

#### Needling

Oblique insertion towards the spine, 0.5 to 1 cun, or transverse-oblique insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.  
Caution: perpendicular needling or oblique needling away from the spine carries a substantial risk of causing a pneumothorax.

#### Actions

- Clears damp-heat from the Liver and Gall Bladder
- Clears pathogenic factors from the shaoyang
- Tonifies and regulates Gall Bladder qi
- Tonifies deficiency

#### Indications

- Jaundice, yellow eyes, bitter taste in the mouth with a dry tongue, distention and pain of the chest and lateral costal region with inability to turn, vomiting, difficult ingestion, dry retching.
- Fright palpitations with restless sleep, insomnia.
- Steaming bone taxation fever, deficiency-taxation, tidal fever, dryness and pain of the throat, cold shivering with absence of sweating, swelling of the axilla, headache.

#### Commentary

Dǎnshū BL-19 is the back-shu point of the Gall Bladder fu, where the qi of the Gall Bladder emanates from the interior to the body surface. It is an important point to clear damp-heat from the Gall Bladder and Liver which gives rise to the classic symptoms of jaundice with yellow eyes, vomiting, distention of the lateral costal region and bitter taste in the mouth. Damp-heat in the Gall Bladder and Liver may arise in the following ways: i. stagnation of Liver qi both generates heat and invades the Spleen, suppressing its transportation and transformation function and leading to the formation of dampness, ii. Spleen deficiency leads to failure of its transportation and transformation function and subsequent formation of dampness which either stagnates and ferments over time and transforms to damp-heat or combines with pre-existing Liver stagnant heat, iii. invasion of exterior damp-heat, iv. consumption of contaminated food, or overconsumption of greasy, rich or spicy food and alcohol. In all cases the damp-heat impairs the Gall Bladder function of secreting bile, and whatever the aetiology Dǎnshū BL-19 is indicated.

According to both the Spiritual Pivot[27](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) and the Essential Questions[28](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "Taiyang is the opening, yangming is the closing and shaoyang is the pivot". In the Treatise on Injury by Cold by Zhang Zhong-jing, shaoyang pattern denotes the stage where the pathogen is 'half-exterior, half-interior'. In other words the pathogenic factor resides between the taiyang (exterior) and yangming (interior) levels and in this sense shaoyang is the pivot or hinge between the exterior and interior. When the pathogenic factor disrupts the Gall Bladder function it gives rise to stagnation of qi, impairment of the Stomach descending function and uprising of Gall Bladder heat. The clinical manifestations of shaoyang pattern include alternating fever and chills, dry throat, blurred vision, bitter taste in the mouth, fullness and distention of the chest and lateral costal region, and vomiting and nausea. All but the first of these symptoms are classical indications for Dǎnshū BL-19.

According to five phase theory, wood is the mother of fire, and disorders of either the Liver or Gall Bladder wood may co-exist with or be transmitted to the Heart which belongs to fire. In the case of the Gall Bladder there are two main patterns of disharmony which may give rise to disturbance of the Heart and the spirit: i. Gall Bladder qi deficiency and ii. Gall Bladder damp-heat combined with Stomach phlegm-heat.

According to the Essential Questions[29](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "the Gallbladder is the upright official from where judgement emanates". If there is sudden fright, unusual or abrupt noise or shock, or continuous fear and fright, whether in the formative years of childhood or as an adult, the qi of the Gall Bladder will be injured, giving rise to palpitations, restless sleep or insomnia, inability to sleep alone, mental restlessness, susceptibility to timidity and fear, and indecisiveness. The Gall Bladder qi can only be injured, however, when the Heart qi is also weak. This was emphasised both in Achieving Longevity by Guarding the Source, the 17th Century classic by Gong Ting-xin, which said "Susceptibility to fright ... timidity in which the patient fears being apprehended, all result from deficiency of the qi of Heart and Gall Bladder", and the Essential Readings from the Medical Tradition which explained "When the qi of the Heart and Gall Bladder is strong, sudden fright and danger will not cause disease. However, if the qi is deficient, it will be harmful."

In the case of Gall Bladder damp-heat combined with Stomach phlegm-heat, there may be insomnia, palpitations and anxiety accompanied by bitter taste in the mouth, vomiting, and distention and pain of the chest and lateral costal region. In the treatment of both these patterns, Dǎnshū BL-19 forms an important part of the point prescription.

Finally, bilateral [Géshū BL-17](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-17) and Dǎnshū BL-19 are known as the 'four flowers', indicated for steaming bone taxation fever, deficiency-taxation, tidal fever etc. For a discussion of the history and application of these points see [Géshū BL-17](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-17).

#### Summary of clinical application

- For Liver and Gall Bladder damp-heat and shaoyang pattern: jaundice, yellow eyes, bitter taste in the mouth, distention and pain of the chest and lateral costal region, nausea, vomiting, dry throat, blurred vision.
- Tonifies the Gall Bladder qi and clears Gall Bladder and Stomach heat: fright palpitations with restless sleep, insomnia.
- Comprises the ‘four flowers’ with [Géshū BL-17](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-17) to tonify deficiency and treat fevers: steaming bone taxation fever, deficiency taxation, tidal fever.

### **[BL19 胆俞](point/BL19.md)** (Dǎnshū)

* **英文名称：** Gallbladder Shu
* **中文名称：** 胆俞
* **国际代码：** BL-19
* **穴位属性：** **[BL19 胆俞](point/BL19.md)** 是胆的背俞穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于第十胸椎（T10）棘突下缘旁开1.5寸。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 位于脊柱旁肌肉的最高点。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

斜刺，向脊柱方向0.5至1寸，或横斜刺1至1.5寸。

* **注意 (Caution):** 垂直针刺或向远离脊柱方向斜刺有很大风险引起气胸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 清利肝胆湿热
* 清泻少阳邪气
* 补益调理胆气
* 补虚

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

黄疸，眼睛发黄，口苦舌干，胸部和胁肋部胀痛不能转侧，呕吐，吞咽困难，干呕。
惊悸伴烦躁不安入睡困难，失眠。
骨蒸劳热，虚劳，潮热，咽喉干燥疼痛，恶寒发热无汗，腋窝肿胀，头痛。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[BL19 胆俞](point/BL19.md)** 是胆腑的背俞穴，胆气从内向体表输布。像所有背俞穴一样（特别是阳经背俞穴），**[BL19 胆俞](point/BL19.md)** 在最深层次上调理和补益其相应的脏腑。胆的两个主要生理功能是储存和排泄胆汁，以及主决断。因此，**[BL19 胆俞](point/BL19.md)** 能够调理肝胆，清利湿热，特别是胆经的湿热，故在治疗黄疸和胁肋痛方面效果显著。

胆在生理上与肝关系密切，互为表里。肝胆的疏泄功能失调，常引起肝气郁结化火，或湿热内生。**[BL19 胆俞](point/BL19.md)** 能够清泻肝胆之火，解除气机郁滞，因此主治胁肋部胀满疼痛、口苦、吞酸、胃脘痛、腹痛腹胀等消化道症状。

此穴也对清热利湿退黄有重要作用，适用于各种类型的黄疸，这反映了其清利肝胆湿热的功效。

在情志方面，胆主决断，胆气不足或胆火偏亢都可能导致神志不安。**[BL19 胆俞](point/BL19.md)** 能够宁心安神，主治惊悸、易受惊吓、失眠多梦，以及癫狂、狂言谵语等精神情志疾病，这与胆与心、脑的联系有关。

**[BL19 胆俞](point/BL19.md)** 与**[BL17 膈俞](point/BL17.md)** 组成“四花穴”，用于治疗虚劳，如疲乏、盗汗、骨蒸潮热等。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 胆的背俞穴，疏肝利胆，清热利湿退黄：黄疸，眼睛发黄，口苦，胸部和胁肋部胀痛，恶心，呕吐，咽喉干燥，视物模糊。
* 宁心安神：惊悸，易受惊吓，失眠多梦，癫狂。
* 与**[BL17 膈俞](point/BL17.md)** 组成“四花穴”，补虚退热：骨蒸劳热，虚劳，潮热。
