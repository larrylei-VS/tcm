### Yin Portals of the Foot ZÚQIÀOYĪN
![[GB-44 足窍阴.png|250]]
- Jing-Well and Metal point of the Gall Bladder channel

#### Location

On the dorsal aspect of the 4th toe, at the junction of lines drawn along the lateral border of the nail and the base of the nail, approximately 0.1 cun from the corner of the nail.

#### Needling

Perpendicular or oblique insertion directed proximally 0.1 to 0.2 cun, or prick to bleed.

#### Actions

- Clears heat and benefits the head
- Benefits the chest and lateral costal region
- Calms the spirit

#### Indications

- Headache, headache with agitation, stabbing pain of the head, dizziness, sudden deafness, tinnitus, redness swelling and pain of the eyes, pain of the outer canthus, throat painful obstruction, stiffness of the tongue or curled tongue with dry mouth, stiffness of the tongue with inability to speak.
- Pain of the lateral costal region, pain of the lateral costal region with cough and inability to catch the breath.
- Nightmares, insomnia, somnolence, agitation and heat of the hands and feet.
- Irregular menstruation, febrile disease, absence of sweating.
- Contracted sinews, contraction of the sinews of the four limbs, inability to raise the elbow.

#### Commentary

The name of Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 (Yin Portals of the Foot) is said to refer to its ability to treat diseases of the sense organs associated with the five zang, namely the eyes (Liver), ears (Kidneys), tongue (Heart), mouth (Spleen) and nose (Lung). In fact, classical indications appear for all of these except the nose. In this respect, Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 on the lower body mirrors [Tóuqiàoyīn GB-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-11) (Yin Portals of the Head) in the upper body.

Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 is the jing-well, and therefore most distal, point of the Gall Bladder channel. According to the Spiritual Pivot[23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-gall-bladder-channel/references) "for diseases of the head select [points from] the feet". Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 has long been cited by numerous classical sources for disorders due to Gall Bladder fire rising to disturb the head, or exterior wind-heat attacking the head, giving rise to symptoms such as headache, headache with agitation, sudden deafness, tinnitus, dizziness, swollen red and painful eyes and throat painful obstruction. As the terminal, and therefore most dynamic point of the channel, Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 is especially indicated when these conditions are severe and acute.

The Classic of Difficulties[24](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-gall-bladder-channel/references) states that jing-well points treat "fullness below the Heart". Whilst the region 'below the Heart' specifically refers to the apex of the epigastrium, like many of the jing-well points Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 treats stagnation and fullness throughout the chest region. When Liver qi stagnates in the chest and lateral costal region there can be distention and pain. Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 is the metal point of the Gall Bladder channel and its use was emphasised in classics such as the Systematic Classic of Acupuncture and Moxibustion and the Great Compendium of Acupuncture and Moxibustion for pain of the lateral costal region with cough and inability to catch the breath. These symptoms occur when Liver qi or Liver fire invade the Lung (wood insults metal).

The Gall Bladder divergent channel links with the Heart, and wood is the 'mother' of fire. Gall Bladder fire, therefore, may easily transmit to the Heart and disrupt the spirit (disease of the mother affecting the child). In common with many of the jing-well points, Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 is effective to calm the spirit and like other jing-well points of the feet (e.g. [Lìduì ST-45](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-45) and [Yǐnbài SP-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-1)) to treat disorders of sleep such as insomnia and nightmares. In discussing Zúqiàoyīn GB-44, the Investigation into Points Along the Channels said "[for] Gall Bladder heat somnolence reduce it, [for] Gall Bladder cold insomnia reinforce it".

Finally, unlike the majority of the jing-well points of the twelve channels, Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 does not appear to be indicated in the major classics for restoring consciousness.

#### Summary of clinical application

- For severe and acute attack of Gall Bladder fire or wind-heat in the head: headache, sudden deafness, tinnitus, dizziness, swelling, redness and pain of the eyes, throat painful obstruction.
- For pain of the lateral costal region with cough and inability to catch the breath.

#### Combinations

- Stabbing pain of the head with inability to move: Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 and [Qiángjiān DU-18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-18) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Deafness: Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 and [Guānchōng SJ-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-1) (Spiritual Pivot).
- Throat painful obstruction, curled tongue and dry mouth: Zúqiàoyīn GB-44, [Guānchōng SJ-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-1) and [Shàozé SI-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-1) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Absence of sweating: Zúqiàoyīn GB-44, [Fùliū KID-7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-7), [Qūzé PC-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/PC-3), [Yújì LU-10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-10), [Shàozé SI-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-1), [Shàngxīng DU-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-23), [Qūquán LIV-8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LIV-8), [Kūnlún BL-60](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-60) and [Xiáxī GB-43](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-43) (Great Compendium).
- Contraction and inability to extend the arm and elbow: Zúqiàoyīn GB-44 and [Shǒusānlǐ LI-10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-10) (Supplementing Life).


### 1. [GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)

#### 1.1. Yin Portals of the Foot 足竅陰

- 胆经的井穴和金穴  

#### 1.2. 定位

位于第4趾背侧，沿趾甲外侧缘与趾甲基底缘交汇处，距趾甲角约0.1寸。

#### 1.3. 针刺方法

i. 垂直或向近端斜刺，深0.1至0.2寸；  
ii. 或刺血。

#### 1.4. 作用

- 清热，益头部  
- 益胸胁  
- 安神  

#### 1.5. 主治

- 头痛、头痛伴烦躁、头部刺痛、眩晕、突发耳聾、耳鳴、目赤肿痛、外眦痛、咽痹、舌僵或卷舌伴口干、舌僵不能言。  
- 胁痛、胁痛伴咳嗽及气短。  
- 噩梦、失眠、嗜睡、手足烦热。  
- 月经不调、热病、无汗。  
- 筋挛、四肢筋挛、肘不能举。  

#### 1.6. 论述

[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)的名称据说反映其治疗与五脏相关的感官疾患的能力，即目（肝）、耳（肾）、舌（心）、口（脾）、鼻（肺）。事实上，除鼻外，经典适应症涵盖所有这些感官。在此方面，[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)在下半身对应于上半身的[GB11 头窍阴](/point/GB11.md)（头窍阴）。  

[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)是胆经的井穴，也是最远端的穴位。《灵枢》称：“头病取足。”[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)长期被众多经典文献引用，用于胆火上扰头部或外感风热侵头引起的症状，如头痛、头痛伴烦躁、突发耳聾、耳鳴、眩晕、目赤肿痛及咽痹。作为经脉的末端及最活跃的穴位，[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)尤其适用于这些病症的剧烈及急性发作。  

《难经》称井穴主“心下满”。“心下”特指胃脘顶端，但与许多井穴类似，[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)治疗整个胸部的郁滞和胀满。当肝气郁于胸胁部时，可出现胀痛。[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)是胆经金穴，《针灸系统经典》和《针灸大成》等经典强调其用于胁痛伴咳嗽及气短。这些症状出现在肝气或肝火侵肺时（木侮金）。  

胆经别与心相连，木为火之母。胆火因此易传至心，扰乱神志（母病及子）。与许多井穴相同，[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)能安神，且与其他足部井穴（如[ST45 厉兑](/point/ST45.md)和[SP1 隐白](/point/SP1.md)）一样，治疗睡眠障碍如失眠及噩梦。《经穴考》称：“胆热嗜睡泻之，胆寒失眠补之。”  

最后，与十二经大多数井穴不同，[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)在主要经典中未见用于恢复意识的明确指征。  

#### 1.7. 临床应用总结

- 用于胆火或风热剧烈急性侵头：头痛、突发耳聾、耳鳴、眩晕、目赤肿痛、咽痹。  
- 用于胁痛伴咳嗽及气短。  

#### 1.8. 配伍

- 头部刺痛伴不能动：[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md) + [DU18 强间](/point/DU18.md)（《千金方》）。  
- 耳聾：[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md) + [SJ1 关冲](/point/SJ1.md)（《灵枢》）。  
- 咽痹、卷舌口干：[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md) + [SJ1 关冲](/point/SJ1.md) + [SI1 少泽](/point/SI1.md)（《千金方》）。  
- 无汗：[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md) + [KID7 复溜](/point/KID7.md) + [PC3 曲泽](/point/PC3.md) + [LU10 鱼际](/point/LU10.md) + [SI1 少泽](/point/SI1.md) + [DU23 上星](/point/DU23.md) + [LIV8 曲泉](/point/LIV8.md) + [BL60 昆仑](/point/BL60.md) + [GB43 侠溪](/point/GB43.md)（《大成》）。  
- 手臂及肘挛缩不能伸：[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md) + [LI10 手三里](/point/LI10.md)（《补命》）。
