### Abundant Bulge FĒNGLÓNG
![[ST-40 丰隆.png|250]]
- Luo-Connecting point of the Stomach channel

#### Location

On the lower leg, midway between the tibiofemoral joint line (level with the popliteal crease) and the lateral malleolus, two finger-breadths lateral to the anterior crest of the tibia (i.e. one finger-breadth lateral to [Tiáokǒu ST-38](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-38)).

#### Needling

Perpendicular or oblique insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.

#### Actions

- Transforms phlegm and dampness
- Benefits the chest
- Clears phlegm from the Lung and alleviates cough and wheezing
- Clears phlegm from the Heart and calms the spirit
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications

- Copious phlegm, fullness, oppression and pain of the chest, stabbing pain of the chest, cutting pain of the abdomen, swelling of the face, dizziness, headache, wind-phlegm headache, plumstone qi (globus hystericus), swelling and pain of the throat, throat painful obstruction with sudden loss of voice.
- Damp body, slackness and heaviness of the body.
- Cough and shortness of breath, cough with copious phlegm, wheezing, dyspnoea, asthma.
- Mania-depression, mad laughter, great happiness, desires to ascend to high places and sing, discards clothing and runs around, restlessness, seeing ghosts, indolence, epilepsy, hypertension.
- Constipation, difficulty in passing urine and stools.
- Atrophy disorder and painful obstruction of the lower limb, pain of the knee and leg, difficulty in flexing and extending the knee, lack of control of the legs, withering of the lower legs.

#### Commentary

Fēnglóng ST-40 is the single most important acupuncture point to transform phlegm in the body, whatever its origin. Phlegm may be formed when i. stagnation of Liver qi impairs the circulation of fluids which then congeal into phlegm, ii. excess or deficiency heat condense body fluids (phlegm is known as the 'substantial part of fire', and fire the 'non-substantial part of phlegm') or iii. any or all of the three zang responsible for fluid transformation and transportation are deficient (the Lung in the upper jiao, the Spleen in the middle jiao and the Kidneys in the lower jiao). Of all these, disharmony of the transportation and transformation function of the Spleen is clinically the most important, thus the Essential Questions[39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) says "The Spleen is the origin of phlegm" and "When water, grain and body fluids do not flow, they will accumulate and form phlegm-fluid (tanyin)". When the Spleen transportation and transformation function is impaired, therefore, body fluids collect and over time transform to phlegm, which may then transmit to other zangfu and regions of the body. Furthermore, it is a general treatment principle that the Spleen should be regulated in the treatment of phlegm of any aetiology, to assist in its transformation. Fēnglóng ST-40 is the point from which the Stomach luo-connecting channel connects with its associated Spleen channel. The Guide to the Classics of Acupuncture states "the luo-connecting points are located between two channels ... if they are punctured, symptoms of the interiorly-exteriorly related channels can be treated"[40](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references). Traditionally, Fēnglóng ST-40 has been used to treat any accumulation of phlegm, especially affecting the Lung, Heart, throat and head.

According to a saying of Chinese medicine, "The Spleen is the origin of phlegm and the Lung is the container of phlegm". When phlegm obstructs the descent of Lung qi, or when Lung qi deficiency is unable to descend the fluids, there may be coughing, wheezing and asthma characterised by copious expectoration of phlegm. When cold predominates, the phlegm will be clear or white, whilst when heat predominates, the phlegm will be yellow, green or brown. Whether cold or hot, Fēnglóng ST-40 is an essential point to transform phlegm in the Lung.

Both the primary and sinew channels of the Stomach pass through the chest, and Fēnglóng ST-40 has long been considered a pre-eminent point in the treatment of chest pain. If phlegm obstructs the free movement of qi and blood in the chest there may be fullness, oppression and stabbing pain. Such is its affinity for the chest, however, that Fēnglóng ST-40 may be used in the treatment of any kind of chest pain including sprain and injury of the chest muscles.

The pattern of phlegm or phlegm-fire misting or deranging the Heart and spirit principally occurs when severe emotional depression leads to stagnation of qi. Stagnant qi can no longer distribute body fluids, which coagulate to form phlegm. Phlegm then obstructs the portals of the Heart, disrupting the spirit. Pronounced or prolonged stagnation of qi and phlegm may transform into fire resulting in phlegm-fire, which may be further complicated by over-consumption of greasy and spicy food and alcohol. When the fire aspect of phlegm-fire is intense, the patient suffers from 'kuang', or mania disorder, with such symptoms as mad laughter and wild and intense behaviour. When the phlegm aspect predominates, with relatively less fire, the patient suffers from 'dian', characterised by mental confusion, lethargy and stupor. The ability of Fēnglóng ST-40 to transform phlegm, combined with the fact that the Stomach divergent channel connects with the Heart, and the Stomach primary channel with the Governing vessel (and thus the brain) at [Shéntíng DU-24](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-24) and [Rénzhōng DU-26](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-26), renders it particularly suitable in treating these disorders.

The Stomach luo-connecting channel from Fēnglóng ST-40 joins with all the yang channels in the head region. If phlegm-damp obstructs the upper jiao and the head, it may impair the ascent of clear yang to the head and sensory orifices, giving rise to dizziness and headache characterised by a heavy and clouded sensation. If interior wind rushes upwards, carrying phlegm with it, this is known as wind-phlegm and may give rise to such symptoms as epilepsy and severe dizziness. The Stomach luo-connecting channel terminates at the throat, and if stagnant qi impairs the ability of the Lung and Stomach to descend the fluids, phlegm is formed and combines with stagnant qi to obstruct the throat, giving rise to plumstone qi (globus hystericus) a sensation of throat blockage which worsens or ameliorates according to fluctuations in the emotional state and which is normally associated with stagnation of qi and phlegm.

A great variety of disorders affecting any region of the body may be caused by phlegm. This is expressed in the Chinese medicine sayings "There is no place that phlegm cannot reach", "The hundred diseases all pertain to phlegm", "Strange diseases often involve phlegm", and "Chronic disease often involves phlegm". Whatever the manifestations, if phlegm is a component of the pathology, Fēnglóng ST-40 is indicated.

Like many points of the foot yangming Stomach channel which is abundant in qi and blood, Fēnglóng ST-40 is indicated for channel disorders such as atrophy disorder and painful obstruction of the lower leg. Due to its ability to transform phlegm, Fēnglóng ST-40 is especially indicated in the treatment of hemiplegia due to wind-phlegm in the channels.

Finally the Great Compendium of Acupuncture and Moxibustion gives specific indications for excess and deficiency of the luo-connecting points. In the case of Fēnglóng ST-40, these are mania-depression (excess); lack of control of the legs and withering of the lower legs (deficiency).

#### Summary of clinical application

- The principal point to resolve phlegm anywhere in the body.
- For Lung and chest phlegm: cough with copious phlegm, wheezing, dyspnoea, asthma, fullness, oppression and pain of the chest.
- For phlegm obstructing the Heart: mania-depression, mad laughter, great happiness, likes to ascend to high places and sing, discards clothing and runs around, restlessness, ‘sees ghosts’, indolence, epilepsy.
- For phlegm-damp: swelling of the face, dizziness, headache, wind-phlegm headache, slackness and heaviness of the body.
- For plumstone throat (globus hystericus).

#### Combinations

- All phlegm disease, head wind, dyspnoea and cough, all types of phlegm-fluid (tanyin): Fēnglóng ST-40 and [Zhōngwǎn REN-12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-12) (Outline of Medicine).
- Cough with phlegm: Fēnglóng ST-40 and [Fèishū BL-13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-13) (Ode of the Jade Dragon).
- Vomiting phlegm and watery saliva, dizziness that does not cease: Fēnglóng ST-40, [Yángxī LI-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-5), [Gōngsūn SP-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-4) and [Shānzhōng REN-17](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-17) (Complete Collection).
- Stabbing pain of the chest: Fēnglóng ST-40 and [Qiūxū GB-40](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-40) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Headache that is difficult to endure: Fēnglóng ST-40 and [Qiángjiān DU-18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-18) (One Hundred Symptoms).
- Head wind and dizziness: Fēnglóng ST-40, [Hégǔ LI-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-4), [Jiěxī ST-41](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-41) and [Fēngchí GB-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-20) (Great Compendium).
- Facial swelling: Fēnglóng ST-40, [Tiānshū ST-25](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-25), [Lìduì ST-45](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-45), [Xiàngǔ ST-43](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-43) and [Chōngyáng ST-42](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-42) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Mania disorder with rash behaviour, desires to ascend to high places and sing, discards clothing and runs around: Fēnglóng ST-40 and [Chōngyáng ST-42](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-42) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Loss of voice: Fēnglóng ST-40, [Tiāntú REN-22](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-22), [Rángǔ KID-2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-2), [Yīngǔ KID-10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-10), [Fùliū KID-7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-7) and [Língdào HE-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/HE-4) (Illustrated Supplement).

### [ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) (FĒNGLÓNG)

* **英文名称：** Abundant Bulge
* **中文名称：** 丰隆
* **国际代码：** ST-40
* **穴位属性：** [ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 是胃经的络穴。

#### 定位 (Location)

位于小腿部，胫股关节线（与腘横纹平齐）与外踝尖之间的中点，胫骨前嵴旁开二横指（即[ST38 条口](/point/ST38.md) 旁开一横指）。

#### 针刺方法 (Needling)

垂直或斜刺1至1.5寸。

#### 作用 (Actions)

* 化痰祛湿
* 宽胸
* 清肺化痰，止咳平喘
* 清心化痰，宁心安神
* 通经活络，止痛

#### 主治病症 (Indications)

痰多，胸部胀满憋闷疼痛，胸部刺痛，腹部绞痛，面部肿胀，眩晕，头痛，风痰头痛，梅核气（咽喉异物感），咽喉肿痛，咽喉肿痛伴突然失音。
身体湿困，身体倦怠沉重。
咳嗽气短，咳嗽痰多，喘息，呼吸困难，哮喘。
癫狂，狂笑，大喜，欲登高而歌，弃衣奔走，烦躁不安，见鬼，懒惰，癫痫，高血压。
便秘，大小便不利。
下肢痿证和痹证，膝腿疼痛，膝关节屈伸不利，两腿无力，小腿萎缩。

#### 阐释 (Commentary)

[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 是全身最重要的化痰穴位，无论痰的来源如何。痰的形成可归因于：i. 肝气郁结影响津液循环，津液凝结成痰；ii. 虚实之热炼液成痰（痰被称为“火之有形部分”，火被称为“痰之无形部分”）；或 iii. 负责津液运化的三脏（上焦肺、中焦脾、下焦肾）任何或全部不足。其中，脾的运化功能失调在临床上最为重要，因此《素问·异法方宜论》记载“脾为生痰之源”，以及“水谷津液不流，则积聚而为痰饮”。因此，当脾的运化功能受损时，津液聚积并随着时间转化为痰，痰随后可能传布到其他脏腑和身体部位。此外，治疗任何病因的痰证，调理脾脏以助其化痰，是一般治疗原则。[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 是胃经络脉与脾经相连的穴位。《针灸大成》记载“络穴位于两经之间……若刺之，可治表里经脉的病症”。传统上，[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 用于治疗各种痰湿积聚，尤其影响肺、心、咽喉和头部。

中医有句俗语，“脾为生痰之源，肺为贮痰之器”。当痰阻碍肺气肃降，或肺气虚弱不能肃降津液时，可能出现咳嗽、喘息和哮喘，特点是咳痰量多。当寒邪为主时，痰呈清稀或白色；当热邪为主时，痰呈黄色、绿色或棕色。无论是寒痰还是热痰，[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 都是化肺痰的关键穴位。

胃经正经和经筋都经过胸部，[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 长期以来被认为是治疗胸痛的首要穴位。如果痰阻碍胸部气血的自由运行，可能出现胀满、憋闷和刺痛。然而，其与胸部的特殊亲和力使得[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 可用于治疗各种胸痛，包括胸肌扭伤和损伤。

痰或痰火蒙蔽心神主要发生在严重情志抑郁导致气滞时。气滞不能再输布津液，津液凝结成痰。痰随后阻碍心窍，扰乱神志。明显或长期的气滞痰凝可能化火，形成痰火，并可能因过量食用油腻辛辣食物和酒精而进一步复杂化。当痰火的火性炽盛时，患者表现为“狂”，即癫狂病，伴有狂笑和狂躁行为等症状。当痰性为主，火性相对较轻时，患者表现为“癫”，特点是精神错乱、嗜睡和昏迷。[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 化痰的能力，结合胃经别连接心脏，以及胃经正经在[DU24 神庭](/point/DU24.md) 和[DU26 人中](/point/DU26.md) 处与督脉（从而与脑）交会的事实，使其特别适合治疗这些疾病。

[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 的胃经络脉与头部所有阳经交会。如果痰湿阻滞上焦和头部，可能影响清阳之气上达头目和七窍，导致眩晕和头痛，特点是头重如蒙。如果内风上冲，携痰上逆，这被称为风痰，可能引起癫痫和剧烈眩晕等症状。胃经络脉终止于咽喉，如果气滞影响肺胃肃降津液的能力，则痰形成并与气滞结合阻碍咽喉，引起梅核气（癔病性咽喉异物感），这是一种咽喉阻塞感，随情绪波动而加重或缓解，通常与气滞痰凝相关。

痰可引起身体任何区域的多种疾病。中医俗语中表达为“痰无处不到”、“百病皆由痰作祟”、“怪病多痰”、“久病多痰”。无论表现如何，如果痰是病理成分之一，[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 都可使用。

像足阳明胃经的许多多气多血穴位一样，[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 主治下肢经络疾病，如小腿的痿证和痹证。由于其化痰的能力，[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 在治疗因经络中风痰导致的半身不遂方面尤其重要。

最后，《针灸大成》列出了络穴虚实证的具体主治。[ST40 丰隆](/point/ST40.md) 在实证时主治癫狂；虚证时主治两腿无力，小腿萎缩。

#### 临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)

* 化解全身任何部位痰湿的主要穴位。
* 治疗肺部和胸部痰证：咳嗽痰多，喘息，呼吸困难，哮喘，胸部胀满憋闷疼痛。
* 治疗痰蒙心窍：癫狂，狂笑，大喜，欲登高而歌，弃衣奔走，烦躁不安，见鬼，懒惰，癫痫。
* 治疗痰湿：面部肿胀，眩晕，头痛，风痰头痛，身体倦怠沉重。
* 治疗梅核气（癔病性咽喉异物感）。
