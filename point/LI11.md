### Pool at the Crook QŪCHÍ
![[LI-11 曲池.png|250]]
- He-Sea and Earth point of the Large Intestine channel
- Sun Si-miao Ghost point
- Ma Dan-yang Heavenly Star point

#### Location

At the elbow, midway between [Chǐzé LU-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-5) and the lateral epicondyle of the humerus, at the lateral end of the transverse cubital crease.

#### Location Notes

This point should be located with the elbow flexed.

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion 1 to 1.5 cun, or joined by through-needling to [Shàohǎi HE-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/HE-3).

#### Actions

- Clears heat
- Cools the blood, eliminates wind, drains damp and alleviates itching
- Regulates qi and blood
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications

- High fever that does not recede, injury by cold with residual fever that does not recede, thirst with sweating on drinking and dry and hot skin when does not drink, malaria.
- Throat painful obstruction, loss of voice, toothache, redness and pain of the eyes, lacrimation, pain in the front of the ear.
- Agitation and oppression of the chest, manic disorders, poor memory, tongue thrusting, dizziness, hypertension, goitre, scrofula.
- Erysipelas (cinnabar toxin), urticaria, wind rash, dry skin, scaly skin, itching of the skin, shingles, pain and itching of the whole body as if bitten by insects, clove sores on the back.
- Distention and pain of the abdomen, vomiting and diarrhoea, dysenteric disorder, amenorrhoea.
- Numbness of the upper arm, painful obstruction, wind painful obstruction, hemiplegia, clonic spasm, contraction, immobility and pain of the elbow and shoulder, emaciation and weakness of the elbow, redness and swelling of the arm, atrophy disorder of the lower limbs, pain and swelling of the ankle.

#### Commentary

Qūchí LI-11 was included by Ma Dan-yang, the great physician of the Jin dynasty, among the 'eleven heavenly star points'[11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-large-intestine-channel/references), his grouping of the most vital acupuncture points. It is a powerful and essential point in the treatment of fevers and heat in the body, skin diseases, hypertension and disorders of the arm.

Yangming or 'yang brightness' channel is considered to be particularly replete with yang qi, and points of the Large Intestine channel are among the most important points to clear excess of yang in the form of heat. Qūchí LI-11 is the principal point on the channel, and indeed one of the foremost points on the body, to clear heat and fire. It is used in all cases of fever due to excess heat when pathogenic factors have penetrated to the yangming or qi levels, and chills are no longer present. In this respect it may be compared to [Hégǔ LI-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-4) which is mainly used when the pathogenic factors are still on the exterior in the form of wind-cold or wind-heat, when the fever is moderate, and when chills are still present. In clinical practice both points are commonly used together to control many different kinds of fever. Like [Hégǔ LI-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-4) and other points of the Large Intestine channel, Qūchí LI-11 is also used to clear heat from the yangming channel in the head, whether it affects the throat, eyes or teeth, and it is indicated for swelling and pain of the throat, redness and pain of the eyes, lacrimation and toothache. Like [Yángxī LI-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-5), it is able to clear yangming fire which transmits to the Heart and spirit, and it is indicated for agitation and oppression of the chest, mania disorder and tongue thrusting. For this reason, under its alternative name of Guitui (Ghost Leg) Qūchí LI-11 was included by Sun Si-miao among his 'thirteen ghost points' for the treatment of mania disorder and epilepsy.

According to the Spiritual Pivot[12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-large-intestine-channel/references) "When the disease is at the yang within yang [skin], needle the he-sea points of the yang channels". Whilst this is not applicable in all cases, it is notable that Qūchí LI-11 and [Wěizhōng BL-40](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-40), the he-sea points of the Large Intestine and Bladder channels respectively, are two of the most important points in the treatment of skin diseases. Qūchí LI-11 is classically indicated for a variety of skin disorders such as erysipelas (cinnabar toxin), urticaria, wind rash, shingles, clove sores and dry, scaly, and itchy skin. This reflects the ability of Qūchí LI-11 to expel wind, resolve damp, and clear heat, fire and fire-poison, the main components of excess-type skin disorders. In modern clinical practice, however, acupuncture is used as a primary therapy in relatively few dermatological diseases (pride of place being given to herbal medicine), and is considered to be of value principally in the treatment of urticaria, herpes zoster and itching of the skin.

According to the Classic of Difficulties[13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-large-intestine-channel/references) he-sea points are effective in the treatment of "counterflow qi and diarrhoea" whilst the Spiritual Pivot says "in disorders of the Stomach and in disorders resulting from irregular eating and drinking, select the he-sea point"[14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-large-intestine-channel/references). Although not having such a wide application in the treatment of these disorders as [Shàngjùxū ST-37](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-37), the lower he-sea point of the Large Intestine, Qūchí LI-11 is indicated for vomiting and diarrhoea, as well as abdominal pain, and is an essential point in the treatment of dysenteric disorder, especially when accompanied by fever. In modern clinical practice it is much used for bacillary dysentery.

In recent times, Qūchí LI-11 has been widely used in the treatment of hypertension, often in combination with [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36). Hypertension as a category did not exist in traditional medicine, its clinical manifestations being included in the categories of headache, dizziness etc., and the effect of reducing these points to lower excessively high blood pressure is a modern interpretation of the statement in the Spiritual Pivot[15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-large-intestine-channel/references) "Yangming channels are abundant in qi and blood".

Qūchí LI-11 is a major point in the treatment of disorders of the whole upper limb. According to Ma Dan-yang "Qūchí LI-11 is excellent for treating pain of the elbow, hemiplegia with inability to close the hand, inability to draw a bow, and flaccidity of the sinews so that a person cannot comb their hair". Qūchí LI-11 can both resolve obstruction in the channel resulting in pain and painful obstruction, and by regulating qi and blood can strengthen and nourish in all cases of weakness of the arm such as atrophy disorder. It is a vital point in the treatment of hemiplegia following windstroke, and for clonic spasm of the upper limb of any aetiology, and for this purpose is i. commonly combined with [Jiānyú LI-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-15) and [Hégǔ LI-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-4) in the 'chain and lock' point association method, and ii. joined by through-needling to [Shàohǎi HE-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/HE-3). This latter technique reflects the importance of [Shàohǎi HE-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/HE-3) as a point to treat disorders of the arm, as well as the clinical observation that while points from the yang channels are emphasised in the treatment of atrophy disorder and hemiplegia, better results are obtained when some points from yin channels are also included in the point prescription.

Finally it is worth noting that despite its status as the 'mother' point of the Large Intestine channel, the only tonifying application of Qūchí LI-11 is in nourishing the upper limb in this way, all its other actions being to reduce excess pathogenic factors of various kinds.

#### Summary of clinical application

- A principal point to reduce high fever (yangming or qi level). Often combined with Dazhui DU-14 and Hegu LI-4 for this purpose.
- Clears heat from the throat (throat painful obstruction, loss of voice), teeth (toothache) and eyes (redness and pain, lacrimation).
- One of the ‘thirteen ghost points’ for mania disorder and epilepsy.
- Treats skin disorders: erysipelas (cinnabar toxin), urticaria, wind rash, dry skin, scaly skin, itching of the skin, shingles, pain and itching of the whole body, clove sores on the back.
- Lowers high blood pressure.
- Major point for arm disorders: numbness, painful obstruction, wind painful obstruction, hemiplegia, atrophy disorder, contraction, immobility and pain of the elbow and shoulder.

### [LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) (QŪCHÍ)

- **英文名称：** Pool at the Crook
- **中文名称：** 曲池
- **国际代码：** LI-11
- **穴位属性：** [LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 是大肠经的合穴和土穴，孙思邈十三鬼穴之一（鬼腿），马丹阳天星穴。

#### 定位 (Location)

位于肘部，在[LU5 尺泽](point/LU5.md) 与肱骨外上髁连线中点，肘横纹外侧端处。

- **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    - 屈肘取穴。

#### 针刺方法 (Needling)

垂直刺1至1.5寸，或透刺至[HE3 少海](point/HE3.md)。

#### 作用 (Actions)

- 清热
- 凉血，祛风，利湿止痒
- 调理气血
- 通经活络，止痛

#### 主治病症 (Indications)

高热不退，伤寒余热不退，饮水则汗出不饮水则皮肤干燥发热，疟疾。

咽喉肿痛，失音，牙痛，眼睛红肿疼痛，流泪，耳前疼痛。

胸部烦躁憋闷，癫狂病，记忆力差，舌体强硬（吐舌），眩晕，高血压，瘿瘤，瘰疬。

丹毒，荨麻疹，风疹，皮肤干燥，皮肤起鳞，皮肤瘙痒，带状疱疹，全身疼痛瘙痒如虫咬，背部疔疮。

腹部胀痛，呕吐腹泻，痢疾，闭经。

上臂麻木，痹证，风痹，半身不遂，痉挛，肘肩部挛缩活动不利疼痛，肘部瘦弱无力，手臂红肿，下肢痿证，踝关节疼痛肿胀。

#### 阐释 (Commentary)

[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 被金代名医马丹阳列为“十一穴天星穴”之一，是他最重要的穴位组合。它是治疗发热、体内热证、皮肤病、高血压和手臂疾病的强大而关键的穴位。

阳明经“多气多血”，大肠经的穴位是清泻阳气亢盛（表现为热）的最重要穴位之一。[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 是该经脉上主要的穴位，实际上也是全身最重要的清热泻火穴位之一。它用于邪气已入阳明或气分的热盛所致的各种发热，此时已无恶寒。在这方面，它可以与主要用于邪气仍在肌表（风寒或风热）、发热中等、仍有恶寒的[LI4 合谷](point/LI4.md) 进行比较。在临床实践中，这两个穴位常联合用于控制多种不同类型的高热。与[LI4 合谷](point/LI4.md) 和大肠经其他穴位一样，[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 也用于清泻阳明经在头部的热邪，无论影响咽喉、眼睛还是牙齿，它主治咽喉肿痛、眼睛红肿疼痛、流泪和牙痛。像[LI5 阳溪](point/LI5.md) 一样，它能够清泻传导入心神的阳明火，主治胸部烦躁憋闷、癫狂病和舌体强硬（吐舌）。因此，[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 以其别名“鬼腿”，被孙思邈列入其“十三鬼穴”，用于治疗癫狂和癫痫。

根据《灵枢》记载，“病在阳之阳者 [皮肤]，刺其合。”虽然并非所有情况都适用，但值得注意的是，[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 和[BL40 委中](point/BL40.md)，分别是大肠经和膀胱经的合穴，是治疗皮肤病最重要的两个穴位。[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 在经典中主治多种皮肤病，如丹毒、荨麻疹、风疹、带状疱疹、疔疮以及皮肤干燥、起鳞和瘙痒。这反映了[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 具有祛风、化湿、清热、泻火解毒的能力，这些是实证皮肤病的主要病机成分。然而，在现代临床实践中，针灸作为主要疗法用于治疗的皮肤病相对较少（中药占主导地位），主要价值体现在治疗荨麻疹、带状疱疹和皮肤瘙痒。

根据《难经》记载，合穴有效治疗“逆气而泄”，而《灵枢》说“病在胃，及以饮食不节，邪在肠胃，皆调于合。”虽然在治疗这些疾病方面不如大肠经下合穴[ST37 上巨虚](point/ST37.md) 应用广泛，但[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 主治呕吐腹泻以及腹痛，是治疗痢疾的关键穴位，特别是伴有发热者。在现代临床实践中，它广泛用于细菌性痢疾。

近年来，[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 已广泛用于治疗高血压，常与[ST36 足三里](point/ST36.md) 联合使用。高血压作为一个类别在传统医学中并不存在，其临床表现包含在头痛、眩晕等范畴中。降低过高血压的作用是现代对《灵枢》中“阳明经多气多血”论述的现代解释。

[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 是治疗整个上肢疾病的主要穴位。根据马丹阳的记载，“[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 善治肘臂痛，半身不遂手不能握，不能引弓，筋缓人不能梳头”。[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 既能解除经络阻滞导致的疼痛和痹证，又能通过调理气血，在各种手臂无力的情况下（如痿证）起到强健和濡养作用。它是治疗中风后偏瘫以及各种原因引起的上肢痉挛的关键穴位，为此目的：i. 常与[LI15 肩髃](point/LI15.md) 和[LI4 合谷](point/LI4.md) 联合使用，采用“链锁法”；ii. 透刺至[HE3 少海](point/HE3.md)。后一种技术反映了[HE3 少海](point/HE3.md) 作为治疗手臂疾病的重要穴位，以及临床观察到虽然阳经穴位在治疗痿证和偏瘫中很重要，但加入一些阴经穴位能取得更好的效果。

最后值得注意的是，尽管[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 作为大肠经的“母穴”，但其唯一的补法应用是以上述方式濡养上肢，其其他作用皆为泻除各种过盛的病邪。

#### 临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)

清泻高热（阳明或气分）的主要穴位。常与[DU14 大椎](point/DU14.md) 和[LI4 合谷](point/LI4.md) 联合使用。

清泻咽喉（咽喉肿痛，失音）、牙齿（牙痛）和眼睛（红肿疼痛，流泪）的热邪。

孙思邈十三鬼穴之一，治疗癫狂和癫痫。

治疗皮肤病：丹毒，荨麻疹，风疹，皮肤干燥，皮肤起鳞，皮肤瘙痒，带状疱疹，全身疼痛瘙痒，背部疔疮。

降低高血压。

治疗手臂疾病的主要穴位：麻木，痹证，风痹，半身不遂，痿证，肘肩部挛缩活动不利疼痛。

治疗痢疾，特别是细菌性痢疾。
