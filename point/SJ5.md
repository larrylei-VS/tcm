### Outer Pass WÀIGUĀN
- Luo-Connecting point of the Sanjiao channel
- Confluent point of the Yang Linking vessel
![[SJ-5 外关.png|250]]


#### Location 

2 cun proximal to Yángchí SJ-4, in the depression between the radius and the ulna, on the radial side of the extensor digitorum communis tendons.

#### Location note 

The point is located between the radius and the extensor digitorum communis tendons, close to the border of the radius.

#### Needling 

i. Slightly oblique insertion towards the ulnar side or oblique proximal or distal insertion towards the elbow or wrist respectively, 0.5 to 1.5 cun, ii. Joined by through-needling to Nèiguān PC-6.  
**Caution:** movement of the patient's arm or hand after needling this point can result in a bent needle.

#### Actions 

- Expels wind and releases the exterior
- Benefits the head and ears
- Opens the Yang Linking vessel
- Clears heat
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications 

- Injury by cold, chills and fever, sweating with heat on the exterior, febrile disease.
- Headache, thunder head wind, head wind, Kidney deficiency headache, one-sided headache, pain of the vertex, frontal headache, pain of the nape, dizziness, phlegm inversion dizziness, wind dizziness, hypertension.
- Deafness, impaired hearing, tinnitus, pain of the ear, itching of the ear, redness, pain and swelling of the root of the ear.
- Redness, pain and swelling of the eyes, cold and pain of the eyes, superficial visual obstruction, lacrimation on exposure to wind, cold lacrimation.
- Swelling and pain of the cheek, stiffness of the tongue with difficulty in speaking, toothache, ulceration of the mouth, cracked lips, nosebleed, scrofula, mumps.
- Constipation, abdominal pain, oppression and tightness of the chest, pain of the lateral costal region, vomiting blood, knotting of heat in the five zang and six fu.
- Pain of the shoulder and back, stiff neck, pain of the ribs and lateral costal region, numbness and pain of the elbow and arm, contraction of the elbow, flaccidity of the elbow, soreness and heaviness of the elbow and wrist, swelling and redness of the arm, paralysis and numbness of the arm, hemiplegia, severe pain of the fingers with inability to grasp objects, coldness, numbness and pain of the hands and feet, tremor of the hand, redness, swelling and pain of the ankle, pain of the toes, pain of the hundred joints.

#### Commentary 

Wàiguān SJ-5, the confluent point of the Yang Linking vessel, is the most important and frequently used distal point of the Sanjiao channel with wide clinical application. The Yang Linking vessel, which has no points of its own, links the six yang channels and the Governing vessel. Yang corresponds to the exterior while yin corresponds to the interior, and the yang channels as a whole therefore relate more to the exterior portion of the body. According to the Classic of Difficulties [7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-sanjiao-channel/references) "when the Yang Linking vessel is diseased, there will be severe chills and fever", whilst the Song of Points for Miscellaneous Diseases says "use Wàiguān SJ-5 for all wind, cold, summer-heat and damp pathogens, headaches and fever". In the Ode of the Obstructed River the use of Wàiguān SJ-5 is referred to as one of the 'eight therapeutic methods'. In this description of the application of the eight confluent points of the extraordinary vessels to affect specific symptoms and areas of the body, Wàiguān SJ-5 is indicated for injury by cold to the exterior accompanied by headache. These classical references emphasise two of the principle applications of Wàiguān SJ-5 namely: i. dispelling exterior pathogenic factors, and ii. treating headache.

Exterior pathogenic factors attack the superficial portion of the body first, giving rise to the typical symptoms of chills and fever, and at this stage of progression the treatment principle is to release the exterior. In current clinical practice some authorities emphasise the special ability of Wàiguān SJ-5 to resolve wind-heat and Hégǔ LI-4 to resolve wind-cold, whilst others take the opposite view. This inevitably raises the question of when to use Wàiguān SJ-5 and when to use Hégǔ LI-4. Although there is no clear-cut answer, it is worth noting that whilst Hégǔ LI-4 is classically indicated for chills and fever accompanied by absence of sweating (characteristic of wind-cold), the only reference to sweating and Wàiguān SJ-5 in classical sources is 'sweating with heat on the exterior' (characteristic of wind-heat). Hégǔ LI-4 belongs to yangming channel which dominates the central portion of the face and is strongly indicated for pathogenic factors giving rise to sneezing and nasal discharge, whilst Wàiguān SJ-5 belongs to the Sanjiao channel which traverses the lateral portion of the neck and is indicated for swelling of the glands (including scrofula and mumps).

When wind-dampness attacks the body, in addition to chills and fever there is typically pain of the joints, and the Classic of the Jade Dragon recommends Wàiguān SJ-5 for 'chills and fever and pain of the hundred joints'. In terms of the four levels theory of febrile diseases, Wàiguān SJ-5 predominantly treats pathogens at the defensive and qi levels. In terms of the Sanjiao theory of differentiating fevers, it focuses on the more exterior upper jiao Lung pattern, rather than the Pericardium pattern. This is in contrast to points of its coupled Pericardium channel, which predominantly treat pathogens at the nutritive and blood levels according to four level theory, and the Pericardium pattern according to the Sanjiao theory.

As well as chills and fever, Wàiguān SJ-5 is indicated for either wind-cold or wind-heat attacking the eyes and ears and giving rise to symptoms such as redness, pain and swelling, lacrimation, deafness and tinnitus etc.

Wàiguān SJ-5 is classically indicated for many different kinds of headache, including temporal, vertex, frontal and occipital headaches as well as headache due to Kidney deficiency, head wind and 'thunder head wind' (severe headache with a thundering sound in the head). The widespread action of Wàiguān SJ-5 on such varied kinds of headache may be explained by a number of different factors: i. as stated above, Wàiguān SJ-5 is an important point to expel pathogenic factors and may be used in the treatment of headache due to penetration by wind-cold, wind-heat or wind-damp; ii. the Yang Linking vessel links all the yang channels (the occipital region is governed by the Governing vessel and taiyang channel, the temporal region by the Yang Linking vessel and shaoyang channel, and the frontal region by yangming channel); iii. the Sanjiao channel specifically connects with such important points for headaches as Běnshén GB-13 through to Fēngchí GB-20, Tóuwéi ST-8 and Fēngfǔ DU-16; iv. the Sanjiao and Gall Bladder shaoyang channels are linked according to six channel theory, and the Sanjiao channel intersects the Gall Bladder channel at points Tóngzǐliáo GB-1, Shàngguān GB-3, Hànyàn GB-4, Xuánlí GB-6 and Jiānjǐng GB-21, whilst the Gall Bladder and Liver channels are interiorly-exteriorly coupled. Wàiguān SJ-5 is therefore an especially important point in the treatment of headaches due to Liver disharmony, especially when this gives rise to one-sided temporal headache (shaoyang region). For this purpose Wàiguān SJ-5 is often combined with Gall Bladder channel points such as Zúlínqì GB-41 (predominantly for headaches due to Liver qi stagnation, including premenstrual headaches), and Yángfǔ GB-38 and Xiáxī GB-43 (predominantly for headaches due to Liver fire or Liver yang rising).

The Yang Linking vessel passes behind the ear whilst the Sanjiao channel both encircles and enters the ear. Wàiguān SJ-5 is therefore an important point in the treatment of various ear disorders including tinnitus, deafness, earache and itching of the ear, and along with Zhōngzhǔ SJ-3 is one of the principal distal points in the treatment of disorders of this region.

The Sanjiao channel belongs to fire and its internal pathway passes through all the three jiao. According to Methods of Acupuncture and Moxibustion from the Golden Mirror of Medicine Wàiguān SJ-5 resolves knotting of heat in the five zang and six fu. Its action on clearing heat from the middle and lower jiao is reflected in its ability to treat constipation, abdominal pain, pain of the lateral costal region and vomiting, but it is especially effective in treating heat disorders of the head (swelling and pain of the cheek, nosebleed, toothache, ulceration of the mouth, cracked lips etc.) and clearing heat and fire poison from the Sanjiao channel in the neck (scrofula and mumps).

Wàiguān SJ-5 is also a vital point in the treatment of a wide variety of channel disorders affecting the shoulder, arm, elbow, wrist, hand and fingers. In this respect it acts almost like a point of the hand yangming Large Intestine channel, and is often incorporated with Jiānyú LI-15, Qūchí LI-11 and Hégǔ LI-4 in the treatment of painful obstruction, atrophy disorder and hemiplegia of the upper limb. The action of Wàiguān SJ-5 on the elbow region is emphasised in the Great Compendium of Acupuncture and Moxibustion which gives specific indications for excess and deficiency of the luo-connecting points. In the case of Wàiguān SJ-5 these are contraction of the elbow (excess) and flaccidity of the elbow (deficiency).

Finally, Wàiguān SJ-5 is the luo-connecting point of the Sanjiao channel from where the luo-connecting channel rises to converge with the Pericardium channel in the chest. Although this strengthens the Sanjiao-Pericardium relationship, with the exception of oppression and tightness of the chest there are no specific indications of this linkage. In clinical practice, however, the points Nèiguān PC-6 and Wàiguān SJ-5 are often joined by through-needling for pain of the chest which radiates to the back.

#### Summary of clinical application 

- Important point to release the exterior: for wind-cold and especially wind-heat, particularly when accompanied by swollen glands and/or eye and ear disorders, and for acute wind-damp (painful obstruction) with joint pain.
- Important point for headaches due to many aetiologies and affecting any region of the head, but principally for temporal headaches and those due to Liver qi stagnation (e.g. with Zúlínqì GB-41) or Liver yang/fire (e.g. with Yángfǔ GB-38 or Xiáxī GB-43).
- Important distal point for ear disorders (tinnitus, deafness, earache, itching of the ear).
- Clears heat from the head (swelling and pain of the cheek, nosebleed, toothache, ulceration of the mouth, cracked lips), and heat and fire poison from the Sanjiao channel in the neck (scrofula and mumps).
- Much used for channel disorders affecting the shoulder, arm, elbow, wrist, hand and fingers.

### **[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** (WÀIGUĀN)

* **英文名称：** Outer Pass
* **中文名称：** 外关
* **国际代码：** SJ-5
* **穴位属性：** **[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 是三焦经的络穴，阳维脉的交会穴（八脉交会穴之一）。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于**[SJ4 阳池](/point/SJ4.md)** 上方2寸，在桡骨和尺骨之间凹陷中，指总伸肌腱桡侧。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 穴位位于桡骨和指总伸肌腱之间，靠近桡骨边缘。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

i. 稍斜向尺侧进针，或斜向近端或远端（分别向肘部或腕部）进针，0.5至1.5寸； ii. 透刺至**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)**。

* **注意 (Caution):** 针刺此穴后患者手臂或手部活动可能导致弯针。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 祛风解表
* 宣利头耳
* 通调阳维脉
* 清热
* 通经活络，止痛

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

伤寒，恶寒发热，汗出伴体表发热，热病。
头痛，雷头风，头风，肾虚头痛，偏头痛，头顶痛，前额头痛，颈项疼痛，眩晕，痰逆眩晕，风眩，高血压。
耳聋，听力障碍，耳鸣，耳痛，耳部瘙痒，耳根红肿疼痛。
眼睛红肿疼痛，眼冷痛，外障眼病（翳膜），迎风流泪，冷泪。
面颊肿痛，舌僵言语不利，牙痛，口舌溃烂，口唇皲裂，鼻出血，瘰疬，痄腮（腮腺炎）。
便秘，腹痛，胸部憋闷，胁肋部疼痛，呕血，五脏六腑热结。
肩背疼痛，颈项强直，肋骨和胁肋部疼痛，肘臂麻木疼痛，肘部挛缩，肘部痿软，肘腕酸痛沉重，手臂红肿，手臂麻痹，半身不遂，手指剧痛不能握物，手足寒冷、麻木疼痛，手颤，踝关节红肿疼痛，足趾疼痛，百节疼痛。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)**，阳维脉的交会穴，是三焦经最重要的常用远端穴位，临床应用广泛。阳维脉本身没有穴位，它联络六条阳经和督脉。阳主表而阴主里，因此阳经整体上更与身体的表部相关。根据《难经》记载：“阳维脉病者，苦寒热”。《杂病穴法歌》载：“凡风寒暑湿，头痛发热，皆用**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)**。”在《标幽赋》中，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 的使用被称为“八法”之一。在这段描述八脉交会穴影响特定症状和身体区域的应用中，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 主治外感风寒兼头痛。这些经典文献强调了**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 的两个主要应用：i. 祛除外邪，和 ii. 治疗头痛。

外邪首先侵袭体表，引起典型的恶寒发热症状，在此进展阶段，治疗原则是解表。在当前的临床实践中，一些权威强调**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 专门清解风热，而**[LI4 合谷](/point/LI4.md)** 专门清解风寒，另一些则持相反观点。这不可避免地提出了何时使用**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 何时使用**[LI4 合谷](/point/LI4.md)** 的问题。虽然没有明确的答案，但值得注意的是，**[LI4 合谷](/point/LI4.md)** 经典主治恶寒发热伴无汗（风寒特点），而经典文献中提及汗出与**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 的唯一参考是“汗出伴体表发热”（风热特点）。**[LI4 合谷](/point/LI4.md)** 属阳明经，主面部中央，强烈主治引起喷嚏和鼻涕的病邪，而**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 属三焦经，循行于颈部外侧，主治腺体肿胀（包括瘰疬和痄腮）。

当风湿侵袭身体时，除恶寒发热外，通常伴有关节疼痛，《玉龙歌》推荐**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 治疗“寒热百节痛”。在温病四层辨证理论中，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 主要治疗卫分和气分病邪。在三焦辨证中，它侧重于更表层的上焦肺证，而非心包证。这与其相表里的心包经穴位形成对比，后者主要治疗四层辨证中的营分和血分病邪，以及三焦辨证中的心包证。

除恶寒发热外，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 还主治风寒或风热侵袭眼睛和耳朵，引起红肿疼痛、流泪、耳聋耳鸣等症状。

**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 在经典中主治多种头痛，包括颞部、头顶、前额和枕部头痛，以及肾虚头痛、头风和“雷头风”（头部有雷鸣般响声的剧烈头痛）。**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 对如此多样头痛的广泛作用可由多种不同因素解释：i. 如前所述，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 是祛除病邪的重要穴位，可用于治疗风寒、风热或风湿侵袭引起的头痛；ii. 阳维脉联络所有阳经（枕部由督脉和太阳经主宰，颞部由阳维脉和少阳经主宰，前额由阳明经主宰）；iii. 三焦经明确连接 **[GB13 本神](/point/GB13.md)** 至 **[GB20 风池](/point/GB20.md)**、 **[ST8 头维](/point/ST8.md)** 和 **[DU16 风府](/point/DU16.md)** 等治疗头痛的重要穴位；iv. 三焦和胆经少阳根据六经理论相连，三焦经在 **[GB1 瞳子髎](/point/GB1.md)**、**[GB3 上关](/point/GB3.md)**、 **[GB4 颔厌](/point/GB4.md)**、**[GB6 悬厘](/point/GB6.md)** 和 **[GB21 肩井](/point/GB21.md)** 等穴与胆经交会，而胆经与肝经互为表里。因此，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 在治疗肝脏失调引起的头痛方面尤其重要，特别是当其引起单侧颞部头痛（少阳区域）时。为此，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 常与胆经穴位如 **[GB41 足临泣](/point/GB41.md)**（主要用于肝气郁结引起的头痛，包括经前头痛）以及 **[GB38 阳辅](/point/GB38.md)** 和 **[GB43 侠溪](/point/GB43.md)**（主要用于肝火或肝阳上亢引起的头痛）联合使用。

阳维脉经过耳后，三焦经既环绕又入耳。因此，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 是治疗耳鸣、耳聋、耳痛和耳部瘙痒等多种耳部疾病的重要穴位，与 **[SJ3 中渚](/point/SJ3.md)** 一起是治疗此区域疾病的主要远端穴位之一。

三焦经属火，其内部循行经过三焦。根据《医宗金鉴·针灸法》，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 能化解五脏六腑热结。其清泻中焦和下焦热邪的作用体现在其治疗便秘、腹痛、胁肋部疼痛和呕吐的能力中，但它在治疗头部热病（面颊肿痛，鼻出血，牙痛，口舌溃疡，口唇皲裂等）以及清泻颈部三焦经的热邪和火毒（瘰疬和痄腮）方面尤其有效。

**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 也是治疗影响肩、臂、肘、腕、手和指的各种经络疾病的关键穴位。在这方面，它几乎像手阳明大肠经的穴位一样发挥作用，常与 **[LI15 肩髃](/point/LI15.md)**、**[LI11 曲池](/point/LI11.md)** 和 **[LI4 合谷](/point/LI4.md)** 联合用于治疗上肢的痹痛、痿证和偏瘫。**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 对肘部区域的作用在《针灸大成》中得到强调，该书列出了络穴虚实证的具体主治。**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 在实证时主治肘部挛缩；虚证时主治肘部痿软。

最后，**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 是三焦经的络穴，络脉由此上行汇合于胸部的心包经。虽然这强化了三焦-心包的关系，但除胸部憋闷外，没有明确的这种联系的主治。然而，在临床实践中，**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 和 **[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)** 常透刺联合治疗放射至背部的胸痛。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 解表重要穴位：治疗风寒，特别是风热，尤伴有腺体肿胀和/或眼耳疾病时，以及伴有关节疼痛的急性风湿痹痛。
* 治疗多种病因引起的头痛，影响头部任何区域，但主要用于颞部头痛和肝气郁结（如与**[GB41 足临泣](/point/GB41.md)** 联合）或肝阳肝火（如与**[GB38 阳辅](/point/GB38.md)** 或**[GB43 侠溪](/point/GB43.md)** 联合）引起的头痛。
* 治疗耳部疾病的重要远端穴位（耳鸣，耳聋，耳痛，耳部瘙痒）。
* 清泻头部热邪（面颊肿痛，鼻出血，牙痛，口舌溃疡，口唇皲裂），清泻颈部三焦经的热邪和火毒（瘰疬和痄腮）。
* 广泛用于治疗肩、臂、肘、腕、手和指的经络疾病。
