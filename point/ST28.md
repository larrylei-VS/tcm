### Water Passage SHUǏDÀO
![[ST-28 水道.png|250]]
#### Location

On the lower abdomen, 2 cun lateral to the midline and 3 cun inferior to the umbilicus, level with [Guānyuán REN-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-4).

#### Location Notes

The 2 cun line is located halfway between the midline and the palpable lateral border of the rectus abdominis muscle at the level of the umbilicus.

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.  
**Caution:** deep insertion may penetrate the peritoneal cavity in thin patients or may penetrate a full bladder; the patient should therefore be asked to empty the bladder before needling.

#### Actions

- Regulates the lower jiao and dispels stagnation
- Benefits the Bladder and the uterus

#### Indications

- Retention of urine and faeces, oedema, cold in the Bladder, distention and fullness of the lower abdomen, shan disorder.
- Hypogastric pain in women extending to the genitals, dysmenorrhoea, infertility, cold in the uterus that radiates down the thigh to the knee, uterine (jia) masses, retention of dead foetus, retention of the placenta, lumbar pain accompanying menstruation, heat binding the three jiao.
- Pain of the lumbar vertebrae, pain of the shoulder and back.

#### Commentary

Although this point is most commonly known as Shuidao (Water Passage), the great 7th century physician Sun Si-miao referred to left Shuidao as Baomen (Gate of the Uterus) and right Shuidao as Zihu (Child's Door)[14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references), emphasising its action on gynaecological disorders. These two ways of naming this point reflect its dual actions of clearing stasis from the Bladder and promoting urination on the one hand, and clearing stasis of qi and blood from the uterus on the other. As far as the former action is concerned, Shuǐdào ST-28 is indicated when excess pathogenic factors obstruct the qi transformation function of the Bladder leading to retention of urine. As for the latter action, Shuǐdào ST-28 is particularly indicated for cold congealing the uterus (see [Guīlái ST-29](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-29) for a fuller discussion) and giving rise to obstruction of blood, manifesting with a variety of symptoms of blood stasis such as dysmenorrhoea, infertility, menstrual pain radiating to the lumbar region or down the thighs, and retention of the placenta or retention of dead foetus. It is also specifically indicated for uterine (jia) masses, implying masses of indefinite form and changing location, primarily due to qi stagnation. The unifying factor between these two spheres of action is obstruction, emphasising that this point is almost exclusively used for excess patterns.

Shuǐdào ST-28 is also indicated for pain of the lumbar region and pain of the shoulder and back and appears in at least two classical combinations for this purpose. This is an example of the method of selecting points from the front of the body to treat the back, and also reflects the fact that the Stomach sinew channel passes through the lower ribs and connects with the spine.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Regulates the Bladder and urination: retention of urine and faeces, cold in the Bladder, oedema.
- Moves stagnant qi and blood in the lower jiao: shan disorder, hypogastric pain in women extending to the genitals, dysmenorrhoea (with lumbar pain), infertility, cold in the uterus that radiates down the thigh to the knee, uterine masses, retention of the placenta.

#### Combinations

- Stiffness of the spine: Shuǐdào ST-28 and [Jīnsūo DU-8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-8) (One Hundred Symptoms).
- Pain of the shoulder and back: Shuǐdào ST-28, [Tiānyǒu SJ-16](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-16), [Quēpén ST-12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-12), [Shéndào DU-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-11), [Dàzhù BL-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-11), [Tiāntú REN-22](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-22) and [Jùgǔ LI-16](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-16) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).

### [ST28 水道](point/ST28.md) (SHUǏDÀO)

* **英文名称：** Water Passage
* **中文名称：** 水道
* **国际代码：** ST-28

#### 定位 (Location)

位于下腹部，距前正中线旁开2寸，脐下3寸，与[REN4 关元](point/REN4.md) 平齐。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 旁开2寸线位于脐水平的前正中线与可触及的腹直肌外缘之间中点。

#### 针刺方法 (Needling)

垂直刺1至1.5寸。

* **注意 (Caution):** 瘦弱患者深刺可能刺穿腹膜腔，或可能刺入充满的膀胱；因此，针刺前应请患者排空膀胱。

#### 作用 (Actions)

* 调理下焦，消散瘀滞
* 宣利膀胱和子宫

#### 主治病症 (Indications)

大小便潴留，水肿，膀胱虚寒，小腹胀满，疝气。
女性少腹疼痛放射至生殖器，痛经，不孕症，子宫寒冷放射至大腿及膝盖，子宫癥瘕积聚，死胎不下，胞衣不下，伴月经的腰痛，三焦热结。
腰椎疼痛，肩背疼痛。

#### 阐释 (Commentary)

尽管此穴最常被称为“水道”，但七世纪名医孙思邈将左侧的[ST28 水道](point/ST28.md) 称为“胞门”，右侧的[ST28 水道](point/ST28.md) 称为“子户”，强调其对妇科疾病的作用。这两种命名方式反映了此穴的双重作用：一方面清泻膀胱瘀滞，促进排尿；另一方面清泻子宫气血瘀滞。就前一个作用而言，当邪气阻滞膀胱气化功能导致尿潴留时，[ST28 水道](point/ST28.md) 主治。至于后一个作用，[ST28 水道](point/ST28.md) 特别主治寒凝胞宫（详见[ST29 归来](point/ST29.md)）引起血瘀，表现为痛经、不孕症、月经期腰痛或向下肢放射的疼痛，以及胞衣不下或死胎不下等多种血瘀症状。它还特别主治子宫癥瘕积聚，指形态不定、位置变化的包块，主要由气滞引起。这两个作用领域的统一因素是阻滞，强调此穴几乎只用于实证。

[ST28 水道](point/ST28.md) 也主治腰部疼痛和肩背疼痛，并且至少出现在两个经典的治疗此目的的组合中。这是选取身体前部穴位治疗背部疾病的例子，也反映了胃经筋经过下肋并与脊柱相连的事实。

#### 临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)

* 调理膀胱和排尿：大小便潴留，膀胱虚寒，水肿。
* 活血化瘀，调理下焦气血：疝气，女性少腹疼痛放射至生殖器，痛经（伴腰痛），不孕症，子宫寒冷放射至大腿及膝盖，子宫癥瘕积聚，胞衣不下。

#### 穴位组合 (Combinations)

* 脊柱僵硬：[ST28 水道](point/ST28.md) 和[DU8 筋缩](point/DU8.md)（《百症赋》）。
* 肩背疼痛：[ST28 水道](point/ST28.md)，[SJ16 天牖](point/SJ16.md)，[ST12 缺盆](point/ST12.md)，[DU11 神道](point/DU11.md)，[BL11 大杼](point/BL11.md)，[REN22 天突](point/REN22.md) 和[LI16 巨骨](point/LI16.md)（《千金方》）。
