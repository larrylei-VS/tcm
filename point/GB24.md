### Sun and Moon RÌYUÈ
![[GB-24 日月.png|250]]
- Meeting point of the Gall Bladder and Spleen channels Front-Mu point of the Gall Bladder

#### Location

On the anterior chest wall, in the seventh intercostal space, directly below the nipple, 4 cun lateral to the midline.

#### Location Notes

First locate the costal cartilage of the second rib which is level with the sternal angle, locate the second intercostal space below it and then locate the seventh intercostal space, five spaces below that. In males the nipple lies in the fourth intercostal space.

#### Needling

Transverse-oblique insertion along the intercostal space, 0.5 to 1 cun.  
**Caution:** deep or perpendicular needling may induce a pneumothorax.

#### Actions

- Benefits the Gall Bladder and spreads Liver qi
- Lowers rebellious qi and harmonises the middle jiao
- Resolves damp-heat

#### Indications

- Distention, fullness and pain of the lateral costal region, pain of the ribs, epigastric pain, abdominal distention, jaundice.
- Vomiting, much spittle, acid regurgitation, hiccup.
- Sighing with tendency to sadness, heat of the lower abdomen, heat of the hypogastrium, loss of use of the four limbs.

#### Commentary

According to the Essential Questions[6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-gall-bladder-channel/references) "The Gall Bladder is the upright official from where judgement emanates". When the characters for sun and moon (the name of this point) are combined, they form a new character 'ming' which means 'clear', intelligent' or 'to understand'. 'Ming' describes the quality of judgement emanating from a healthy Gall Bladder. Rìyuè GB-24 has traditionally been ascribed the ability to rectify deficiency patterns of the Gall Bladder and was indicated as early as the Essential Questions (with [Dǎnshū BL-19](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-19)) for the treatment of Gall Bladder deficiency giving rise to indecisiveness, and elsewhere for sighing with a tendency to sadness.

Rìyuè GB-24 was established as the front-mu point of the Gall Bladder by the time of the Yellow Emperor's Inner Classic. Although many centuries later the Great Compendium of Acupuncture and Moxibustion designated [Zhéjīn GB-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-23) as the Gall Bladder front-mu point, it is Rìyuè GB-24 which has retained this status to the present day. The term 'mu' means to gather or to collect, and the front-mu points are where the qi of the zangfu gathers and concentrates on the anterior surface of the body. Rìyuè GB-24 is an important point to treat diseases of the Gall Bladder fu which may derive from the following three aetologies: i. emotional disharmony results in stagnation of Liver qi which impairs the circulation of qi in its interiorly-exteriorly related Gall Bladder channel, and gives rise to distention, fullness and pain of the lateral costal region, sighing and a sensation of heat in the lower abdomen; ii. disharmony of the Stomach and Spleen transportation and transformation function leads to accumulation of dampness which transforms to damp-heat and ferments in the Liver and Gall Bladder; bile does not flow and seeps into the muscles and skin giving rise to jaundice; iii. Gall Bladder qi invades the Stomach and interferes with its descending function resulting in vomiting, acid regurgitation, hiccup and epigastric pain. All these three patterns may be encountered in diseases such as cholecystitis, cholelithiasis and hepatitis.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Tonifies Gall Bladder qi: indecisiveness, sighing with a tendency to sadness.
- Resolves Gall Bladder qi stagnation, Gall Bladder damp-heat and disharmony of the Gall Bladder and Stomach: distention, fullness and pain of the lateral costal region, abdominal distention, jaundice, vomiting, acid regurgitation, hiccup, epigastric pain.

#### Combinations

- "When a person is frequently indecisive, the Gall Bladder is deficient. The qi will flow upwards giving rise to a bitter taste in the mouth. To treat this use the Mu and the Shu of the Gall Bladder" [Rìyuè GB-24 and [Dǎnshū BL-19](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-19)] (Essential Questions).
- Sighing with propensity to sadness: Rìyuè GB-24 and [Shāngqiū SP-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-5) (Supplementing Life).
- Loss of use of the four limbs: Rìyuè GB-24, [Jíquán HE-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/HE-1) and [Píshū BL-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-20) (Supplementing Life).
- Cholecystitis: Rìyuè GB-24, [Bùróng ST-19](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-19), Dannang-xue (M-LE-23), [Zhīgōu SJ-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-6) and Qiuxu GB‑40.

### 1. [GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md)

#### 1.1. Sun and Moon 日月

- 胆经与脾经的交会穴，胆的前募穴

#### 1.2. 定位

位于前胸壁，第7肋间隙，乳头正下方，距中线4寸。

#### 1.3. 定位说明

首先定位第2肋软骨（与胸骨角平齐），下方为第2肋间隙，再向下数5个肋间隙为第7肋间隙。男性乳头位于第4肋间隙。

#### 1.4. 针刺方法

沿肋间隙横斜刺，深0.5至1寸。  
**注意：** 深刺或垂直刺入可能引起气胸。

#### 1.5. 作用

- 益胆疏肝气
- 降逆和中焦
- 化湿热

#### 1.6. 主治

- 胁部胀满痛、肋痛、胃脘痛、腹胀、黄疸。
- 呕吐、多涎、反酸、呃逆。
- 叹息易悲、下腹热、脐下热、四肢不用。

#### 1.7. 论述

根据《素问》，“胆为中正之官，决断出焉”。“日”和“月”（此穴名称）组合成“明”字，意为“清晰”、“聪慧”或“理解”。“明”描述了健康胆的决断品质。[GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md)传统上被认为能纠正胆的虚证，《素问》早将其（与[BL19 胆俞](/point/BL19.md)）用于治疗胆虚引起的优柔寡断，并在其他文献中指用于叹息易悲。

[GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md)在《黄帝内经》时期被确立为胆的前募穴。虽然数世纪后《针灸大成》将[GB23 辄筋](/point/GB23.md)指定为胆募穴，但[GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md)至今仍保持此地位。“募”意为聚集或收集，前募穴是脏腑之气在前胸腹壁聚集之处。[GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md)是治疗胆腑疾患的重要穴位，可能源于以下三种病因：  
i. 情绪失调导致肝气郁滞，影响其表里相关的胆经气机，引起胁部胀满痛、叹息及下腹热感；  
ii. 脾胃运化失调导致湿聚，转化为湿热，在肝胆中发酵，胆汁不畅渗入肌肉皮肤，引起黄疸；  
iii. 胆气侵胃，干扰胃的下降功能，导致呕吐、反酸、呃逆及胃脘痛。  
这些模式常见于胆囊炎、胆石症和肝炎等疾病。

#### 1.8. 临床应用总结

- 补益胆气：优柔寡断、叹息易悲。
- 疏解胆气郁滞、胆湿热及胆胃失和：胁部胀满痛、腹胀、黄疸、呕吐、反酸、呃逆、胃脘痛。

#### 1.9. 配伍

- “人常多疑，胆虚也，气上涌致口苦，治之取胆之募俞” ([GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md) + [BL19 胆俞](/point/BL19.md))（《素问》）。
- 叹息易悲：[GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md) + [SP5 商丘](/point/SP5.md)（《补命》）。
- 四肢不用：[GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md) + [HE1 极泉](/point/HE1.md) + [BL20 脾俞](/point/BL20.md)（《补命》）。
- 胆囊炎：[GB24 日月](/point/GB24.md) + [ST19 不容](/point/ST19.md) + [MLE23 胆囊穴](/point/MLE23.md) + [SJ6 支沟](/point/SJ6.md) + [GB40 丘墟](/point/GB40.md)。
