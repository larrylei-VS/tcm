### Inner Pass NÈIGUĀN
![[PC-6.png|250]]


- Luo-Connecting point of the Pericardium channel Confluent point of the Yin Linking vessel

##### Location 

On the flexor aspect of the forearm, 2 cun proximal to [Dàlíng PC-7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/manual/points/P/262), between the tendons of palmaris longus and flexor carpi radialis.

##### Location note 

In the absence of the tendon of palmaris longus, locate this point on the ulnar side of the tendon of flexor carpi radialis.

##### Needling 

i. Perpendicular insertion 0.5 to 1 cun, or joined to [Wàiguān SJ-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/manual/points/SJ/326); ii. Oblique proximal insertion 1 to 1.5 cun for diseases of the chest; iii. Oblique distal insertion 1 to 1.5 cun for numbness of the fingers.  
**Caution:** the median nerve lies directly under this point and needling commonly induces a significant electric sensation. This is an acceptable manifestation of deqi (arrival of qi), but once elicited, further manipulation is inappropriate and may damage the nerve.

##### Actions 

- Unbinds the chest and regulates qi
- Regulates the Heart and calms the spirit
- Harmonises the Stomach and alleviates nausea and vomiting
- Clears heat
- Opens the Yin Linking vessel

##### Indications 

- Heart pain, sudden Heart pain, stuffiness of the chest with agitation of the Heart, palpitations, fright palpitations, pounding sensation of the Heart, disorders of Heart rate and rhythm, pain of the lateral costal region and Heart in women, pain of the lateral costal region, cough, asthma.
- Insomnia, the five types of epilepsy, mania, poor memory, apprehension, fear and fright, sadness, loss of memory following windstroke, inability to speak after windstroke, windstroke, hypertension.
- Nausea, vomiting, hiccup, deficiency and cold of the Spleen and Stomach with incessant vomiting, Spleen and Stomach qi deficiency with distention and fullness, disharmony of the Spleen and Stomach, epigastric pain, stabbing epigastric pain, low-grade abdominal pain, food (ji) masses, blood (jia) masses, focal distention, borborygmus, diarrhoea, blood in the stools, prolapse of the rectum.
- Febrile disease, fever with absence of sweating, headache, stiffness and pain of the head and neck, jaundice, yellow eyes, red eyes, red face with hot skin, malaria, cracked and bleeding tongue, dizziness, post-partum dizziness, irregular menstruation, painful urinary dysfunction.
- Pain and contraction of the elbow and upper arm, swelling of the axilla, stiffness and pain of the head and neck.

##### Commentary 

The Pericardium primary channel originates in the chest, its divergent channel enters the chest, its sinew channel disperses in the chest and over the anterior and posterior ribs and its luo-connecting channel connects with the Heart. Nèiguān PC-6, a point of the Pericardium channel, is also the confluent point of the Yin Linking vessel which ascends through the chest. According to the Classic of Difficulties[9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-pericardium-channel/references) "When the Yin Linking vessel is diseased, Heart pain will result". In the Ode of the Obstructed River the use of Nèiguān PC-6 is referred to as one of 'the eight therapeutic methods'. In this description of the application of the eight confluent points of the extraordinary vessels to affect specific symptoms and areas of the body, Nèiguān PC-6 is indicated for disorders of the chest. The Investigation into Points Along the Channels by the Ming dynasty author Yan Zhen-shi says "Nèiguān PC-6 treats all kinds of pain of the zangfu, chest and lateral costal region". Furthermore, Nèiguān PC-6 was included among the 'six command points' (a grouping derived by later commentators from Gao Wu's 'four command points') for its pre-eminent effect on diseases of the chest and lateral costal region. Nèiguān PC-6, therefore, has long been considered the single most important distal point for disorders of the chest and it is one of the primary points in acupuncture analgesia for chest surgery. Its range of actions extends not only to the Heart, but also to the Lung.

Pain and stuffiness of the Heart and chest may result from various aetiologies including i. Heart qi and yang deficiency, ii. accumulation of cold, iii. accumulation of phlegm, iv.. blood stasis, v.. constrained Liver qi, vi. traumatic injury. Whatever the pattern, Nèiguān PC-6 forms an essential part of any point combination. In the treatment of angina pectoris, however, certain doctors emphasise the use of [Xīmén PC-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/manual/points/P/259) (the xi-cleft point of the Pericardium channel) during acute attacks and Nèiguān PC-6 during remission. Nèiguān PC-6 is also effective to treat other disorders of the chest such as asthma and cough, as well as pain in the lateral costal region and hypochondrium. These last two symptoms reflect not only the pathway of the Pericardium sinew channel, but also the relationship of Nèiguān PC-6 to stagnation of Liver qi, explained by the paired relationship of the Pericardium and Liver jueyin channels. Nèiguān PC-6, therefore, is especially effective in treating stagnation of Liver qi anywhere in the upper and middle jiao.

The Pericardium is the 'wrapping' of the Heart which stores the spirit, and the Pericardium luo-connecting channel links Nèiguān PC-6 directly with the Heart. The action of Nèiguān PC-6 on regulating the Heart zang and calming the spirit emphasises its dual effect on both the physical and emotional aspects of the Heart. It is an important point in the treatment of palpitations, pounding of the Heart and disorders of Heart rhythm, and is also one of the main acupuncture points for regulating and calming the spirit and treating a wide range of emotional disorders whatever the underlying pattern. It is therefore indicated for insomnia, epilepsy, mania, poor memory, loss of memory following windstroke, fright, sadness, fear and apprehension.

The Investigation into Points Along the Channels, echoing many other classical texts, states that Nèiguān PC-6 is indicated "for disharmony of the Stomach and Spleen". The Pericardium primary and divergent channels both descend through the diaphragm to connect with the middle and lower jiao, and this explains the powerful effect of Nèiguān PC-6 on the function of the middle jiao. Nèiguān PC-6 is the pre-eminent point to treat nausea and vomiting due to any aetiology, including vomiting of pregnancy and the side-effects of chemotherapy and radiotherapy. Its considerable effect in the treatment of such side-effects has rendered it the subject of more research in recent years than any other acupuncture point. Nèiguān PC-6 is further indicated for distention, fullness and pain of the epigastrium and abdomen, and is often combined with [Gōngsūn SP-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/manual/points/SP/346), the confluent point of the Penetrating vessel, for this purpose.

The Pericardium channel belongs to fire, and like many other points of the channel Nèiguān PC-6 is indicated for a variety of manifestations of heat, including fevers, jaundice, red face with hot skin, painful urinary dysfunction, cracked and bleeding tongue etc.

Finally the Great Compendium of Acupuncture and Moxibustion gives specific indications for excess and deficiency of the luo-connecting points. In the case of Nèiguān PC-6 these are sudden Heart pain (excess); stiffness [and pain] of the head [and neck] (deficiency).

##### Summary of clinical application 

- “Neiguan PC-6 treats all kinds of pain of the zangfu, chest and lateral costal region” (Yan Zhen-shi, Ming dynasty). Essential point for all disorders of the chest and Heart: Heart pain, stuffy chest, palpitations, disorders of Heart rate and rhythm, angina pectoris, pain of the lateral costal region, asthma, cough.
- Regulates the spirit: insomnia, epilepsy, mania, poor memory, fright, sadness, fear, apprehension.
- The Pericardium channel descends through the middle jiao: essential point for treating nausea or vomiting due to any aetiology including pregnancy and the side-effects of radiotherapy or chemotherapy.

- 络-心包经络交接点 阴络脉交汇点

##### 地点

在前臂屈侧，大陵[穴](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/manual/points/P/262)近2寸，掌长肌腱与腕桡侧屈肌腱之间。

##### 位置说明

当没有掌长肌腱时，此穴位于腕屈肌腱的尺侧。

##### 针刺

i.直刺 0.5 至 1 寸，或与[外关穴 SJ-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/manual/points/SJ/326)相连； ii.斜刺 1 至 1.5 寸，用于治疗胸部疾病；iii.斜刺 1 至 1.5 寸，用于治疗手指麻木。  
**注意：**正中神经位于此穴位正下方，针刺通常会产生明显的电击感。这是得气（气到）的可接受表现，但一旦引起，进一步的操作是不合适的，可能会损伤神经。

##### 操作

- 舒胸理气
- 调理心脏、安神
- 调和胃部，缓解恶心和呕吐
- 清热
- 打开阴脉

##### 适应症

- 心痛、突发心痛、胸闷心悸、心悸、惊悸、心脏剧烈跳动、心律失常、女性侧肋及心脏疼痛、侧肋疼痛、咳嗽、哮喘。
- 失眠，五痫，狂躁，记忆力差，忧虑，惊恐，悲伤，中风后记忆力减退，中风后失语，中风，高血压。
- 恶心呕吐，呃逆，脾胃虚寒呕吐不止，脾胃气虚胀满，脾胃不和，胃脘疼痛，胃脘刺痛，腹痛少许，积块，血块，腹胀，肠鸣，腹泻，便血，脱肛。
- 热病发热无汗，头痛，头项强痛，黄疸，目赤，面赤热，疟疾，舌干舌燥，头晕，产后眩晕，月经不调，小便不利痛。
- 肘部和上臂疼痛和收缩，腋窝肿胀，头部和颈部僵硬和疼痛。

### **[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** (NÈIGUĀN)

* **英文名称：** Inner Pass
* **中文名称：** 内关
* **国际代码：** PC-6
* **穴位属性：** **[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 是心包经的络穴，阴维脉的交会穴（八脉交会穴之一）。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于前臂屈侧，**[PC7 大陵](/point/PC7.md)** 上方2寸，掌长肌腱与桡侧腕屈肌腱之间。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 若无掌长肌腱，此穴位于桡侧腕屈肌腱尺侧。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

i. 垂直刺0.5至1寸，或透刺**[SJ5 外关](/point/SJ5.md)**； ii. 向近端斜刺1至1.5寸治疗胸部疾病； iii. 向远端斜刺1至1.5寸治疗手指麻木。

* **注意 (Caution):** 正中神经位于此穴正下方，针刺常引起明显的电击感。这是得气（气至）的可接受表现，但一旦引出，不宜再进行进一步操作，否则可能损伤神经。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 宽胸理气
* 调理心脏，宁心安神
* 调和胃腑，止呕
* 清热
* 通利阴维脉

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

心痛，心痛暴作，胸闷伴心烦，心悸，惊悸，心动不安，心率失常，女性胁肋部和心痛，胁肋部疼痛，咳嗽，哮喘。
失眠，五种癫痫，癫狂，记忆力差，心神不安，恐惧惊吓，悲伤，中风后失忆，中风后不能言语，中风，高血压。
恶心，呕吐，呃逆，脾胃虚寒伴呕吐不止，脾胃气虚伴胀满，脾胃不和，胃脘痛，胃脘刺痛，小腹疼痛，食积，血积，痞块，肠鸣，腹泻，便血，脱肛。
热病，发热无汗，头痛，头颈僵硬疼痛，黄疸，眼睛发黄，眼睛红赤，面赤皮肤灼热，疟疾，舌裂出血，眩晕，产后眩晕，月经不调，淋证（小便涩痛）。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

心包经正经起于胸中，其经别入胸，其经筋散布于胸部和前后肋，其络脉通心。**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 是心包经的穴位，也是阴维脉的交会穴，阴维脉上行通过胸部。根据《难经》记载：“阴维脉病，苦心痛。”在《标幽赋》中，**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 的使用被称为“八法”之一。在这段描述八脉交会穴如何影响特定症状和身体区域的应用中，**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 被指出主治胸部疾病。明代严震时著《循经考穴编》记载：“**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 治脏腑、胸胁一切疼痛”。此外，**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 被列入“六总穴”（后世医家从高武的“四总穴”发展而来），因其对胸胁疾病有卓越疗效。因此，**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 长期以来被认为是治疗胸部疾病最重要的远端穴位，也是胸部手术针灸镇痛的主要穴位之一。其作用范围不仅限于心脏，还延伸至肺脏。

心胸疼痛憋闷可能源于多种病因，包括：i. 心气心阳不足，ii. 寒邪积聚，iii. 痰湿积聚，iv. 血瘀，v. 肝气郁结，vi. 外伤。无论何种证型，**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 都是任何穴位组合的关键组成部分。然而，在治疗心绞痛时，有些医生强调在急性发作时使用**[PC4 郄门](/point/PC4.md)**（心包经的郄穴），在缓解期使用**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)**。**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 也能有效治疗哮喘和咳嗽等其他胸部疾病，以及胁肋部疼痛。后两个症状不仅反映了心包经筋的循行，也反映了**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 与肝气郁结的关系，这由心包与肝脏的表里关系（厥阴）来解释。因此，**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 在治疗上、中焦任何部位的肝气郁结方面尤其有效。

心包是心藏神的“外衣”，心包络脉将**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 直接与心相连。**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 调理心脏、宁心安神的作用强调其对心脏生理和情志双重方面的影响。它是治疗心悸、心动不安和心律失常的重要穴位，也是调理宁心安神、治疗各种情志障碍（无论潜在病机如何）的主要穴位之一。因此，它主治失眠、癫痫、癫狂、记忆力差、中风后失忆、惊恐、悲伤、恐惧和心神不安。

《循经考穴编》呼应许多其他经典文献，记载**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 主治“脾胃不和”。心包经正经和经别都下行通过膈膜，连接中焦和下焦，这解释了**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 对中焦功能的强大作用。**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 是治疗各种病因引起的恶心呕吐的首要穴位，包括妊娠呕吐和放化疗副作用。它在治疗这些副作用方面的显著疗效使其近年来成为比其他任何穴位都更受研究的对象。**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 进一步主治胃脘和腹部胀满疼痛，常与冲脉的交会穴**[SP4 公孙](/point/SP4.md)** 联合使用。

心包经属火，像该经脉的许多其他穴位一样，**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 主治多种热邪表现，包括发热、黄疸、面赤皮肤灼热、淋证（小便涩痛）、舌裂出血等。

最后，《针灸大成》列出了络穴虚实证的具体主治。**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 在实证时主治心痛暴作；虚证时主治头颈僵硬疼痛。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* “**[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)** 治脏腑、胸胁一切疼痛”（明·严震时）。治疗胸部和心脏所有疾病的关键穴位：心痛，胸闷，心悸，心率失常，心绞痛，胁肋部疼痛，哮喘，咳嗽。
* 调理心神：失眠，癫痫，癫狂，记忆力差，惊恐，悲伤，恐惧，心神不安。
* 心包经下行通过中焦：治疗各种原因引起的恶心呕吐的关键穴位，包括妊娠和放化疗副作用。
