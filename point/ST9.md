### Man's Welcome RÉNYÍNG
![[ST-9 人迎.png|250]]
- Meeting point of the Stomach and Gall Bladder channels
- Point of the Window of Heaven
- Point of the Sea of Qi

#### Location

Level with the tip of and 1.5 cun lateral to the laryngeal prominence, in the depression between the anterior border of the sternocleidomastoid muscle and the lateral border of the thyroid cartilage.  
**Note:** the carotid artery lies just deep to, and can be readily palpated at, the anterior border of the sternocleidomastoid muscle. This point therefore lies between the carotid artery and the lateral border of the thyroid cartilage.

#### Location Notes

i. Ask the patient to lie flat and remove any pillow. Palpate the laryngeal prominence, and then, laterally, the lateral border of the thyroid cartilage. A little more laterally than this, the carotid artery may be felt. ii. In females the laryngeal prominence is not as pronounced as in males. If it is indistinct, palpate the depression formed by the lower border of the hyoid bone and the upper border of the thyroid cartilage at the midline. The laryngeal prominence lies just below this.

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion 0.5 to 1 cun. Note: according to most classical texts, this point is contraindicated to moxibustion.  
**Caution:** care should be taken to avoid puncturing the carotid artery which must be palpated and then held laterally during needling. Use the index finger of one hand to define and enlarge the space between the lateral border of the thyroid cartilage and the artery and needle into this space with the other hand. This needling method should not be attempted by those who have not had appropriate clinical supervision.

#### Actions

- Regulates qi and blood and lowers rebellion
- Benefits the throat and neck
- Alleviates pain

#### Indications

- Headache, dizziness, visual dizziness, red face, fullness of the chest, shortness of breath, asthma, sudden turmoil disorder, vomiting, pulseless syndrome, hypertension, hypotension.
- Swelling and pain of the throat, scrofula, goitre, difficulty in swallowing.
- Lumbar pain.

#### Commentary

Rényíng ST-9 is also known by its alternative name Tianwuhui (Heaven's Five Meetings). It is one of five points known as 'window of heaven' (literally 'five regions of the window of heaven') points in the Spiritual Pivot[5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) which states "headache due to rebellion of yang, fullness of the chest with difficulty in breathing, choose Rényíng ST-9". Located in the pivotal neck region, Rényíng ST-9 dramatically illustrates the characteristic ability of this group of points to re-establish harmony in the flow of qi between the body and the head. The rebellion of qi, which arises when the qi in the lower regions is disordered and chaotic, may manifest in various ways. In the head it gives rise to headache, dizziness and redness of the face; in the neck region it stagnates giving rise to scrofula and goitre; in the middle and upper jiao it injures the normal descent of Stomach and Lung qi and gives rise to vomiting, sudden turmoil disorder, wheezing, asthma and fullness of the chest.

According to Zhou Zhi-cong's commentary on the above passage from the Spiritual Pivot, the condition known as inversion qi (disordered, chaotic and rebellious qi) underlies the clinical manifestations of all the points of the window of heaven. One of the manifestations of inversion qi is sudden acute pain, and this echoes the important modern use of Rényíng ST-9 for acute lumbar sprain, as well as sprain and severe pain in any region of the body. The ability of Rényíng ST-9 to reorder rebellion and upsurge of qi also explains its strong action in reducing hypertension.

According to the chapter 'Discourse On the Seas' of the Spiritual Pivot[6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) Rényíng ST-9 (along with [Shānzhōng REN-17](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-17), [Yǎmén DU-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-15) and [Dàzhuī DU-14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-14)) is a point of the 'sea of qi'. This passage gives the following indications for insufficiency and excess of the sea of qi "When the sea of qi is in excess there is fullness in the chest, urgent breathing and a red complexion. When the sea of qi is insufficient, there is scanty energy insufficient for speech". The dual and overlapping properties of Rényíng ST-9 as a point of the sea of qi and a point of the window of heaven underline its importance in harmonising and redistributing disordered qi in the body.

As long ago as the Yellow Emperor's Inner Classic, observation and palpation of the pulse at Rényíng ST-9 was considered an important diagnostic method. The Essential Questions7 in discussing obstructed urination describes a pattern of "heat in the body like charcoal, the neck and chest are obstructed as if separated, a restless and abundant pulse at Rényíng ST-9 and dyspnoea with counterflow qi". Elsewhere in the Essential Questions great diagnostic significance is ascribed to the relative strengths of the pulses at the wrist and at Rényíng ST-9, whilst Zhang Zhong-jing refers to three methods of pulse diagnosis (Rényíng ST-9 in the upper, the wrist pulse in the middle and [Chōngyáng ST-42](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-42) in the lower). Because of the complications inherent in these methods, they were abandoned relatively early in the history of Chinese medicine in favour of focusing on the wrist pulse.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Window of heaven and sea of qi point for headache, dizziness, red face, vomiting and asthma.
- For acute lumbar sprain and any acute pain, needled bilaterally or on the affected side.
- For hypertension.

#### Combinations

- Sudden turmoil disorder, headache, chest pain and dyspnoeic rales: Rényíng ST-9, [Nèiguān PC-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/PC-6), [Guānchōng SJ-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-1), [Sānyīnjiāo SP-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-6) and [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36) (Compilation).
- Scrofula: moxa Rényíng ST-9 and [Shǒuwǔlǐ LI-13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-13) thirty times (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Tinnitus with lumbar pain: first needle Rényíng ST-9, then needle [Ěrmén SJ-21](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-21) and [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36) (Secrets of the Celestial Star).
- Hypertension: Rényíng ST-9, [Qūchí LI-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-11) and [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36).
- Hypertension: Rényíng ST-9, [Bǎihuì DU-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-20), [Qūchí LI-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-11) and [Tàichōng LIV-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LIV-3).
- Acute lumbar sprain or any acute pain: Rényíng ST-9 bilaterally or on the affected side.

### [ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) (RÉNYÍNG)

* **英文名称：** Man's Welcome
* **中文名称：** 人迎
* **国际代码：** ST-9
* **穴位属性：** [ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 是胃经与胆经的交会穴，天窗穴之一，气海穴之一。

#### 定位 (Location)

位于喉结旁开1.5寸，胸锁乳突肌前缘与甲状软骨外侧缘之间的凹陷中，平喉结。

* **注意 (Note):** 颈总动脉位于胸锁乳突肌前缘的深部，可轻易触及。因此，此穴位于颈总动脉与甲状软骨外侧缘之间。
* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 请患者平躺，拿掉枕头。触诊喉结，然后向外侧触诊甲状软骨外侧缘。再向外侧一点，可触及颈总动脉。
    * 女性喉结不像男性那样突出。如果喉结不明显，可在中线触诊舌骨下缘与甲状软骨上缘形成的凹陷，喉结就在此下方。

#### 针刺方法 (Needling)

垂直刺0.5至1寸。
注意：根据大多数经典文献，此穴禁灸。

* **注意 (Caution):**
    * 针刺时应注意避免刺破颈总动脉，必须在进针前触诊并将其向外侧固定。用一只手的食指界定并扩大甲状软骨外侧缘与动脉之间的空间，用另一只手在此空间内进针。
    * 未经适当临床指导者不应尝试此针法。

#### 作用 (Actions)

* 调理气血，降逆
* 宣利咽喉颈部
* 止痛

#### 主治病症 (Indications)

头痛，眩晕，视物眩晕，面赤，胸中胀满，气短，哮喘，霍乱吐泻，呕吐，无脉症，高血压，低血压。
咽喉肿痛，瘰疬，瘿瘤，吞咽困难。
腰痛。

#### 阐释 (Commentary)

[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 又名“天五会”。它是《灵枢》中“五处天窗”的五个穴位之一，该篇记载“阳气逆头痛，胸满气短者，取[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md)。”[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 位于颈部枢纽区域，生动地展现了这组穴位在重新建立身体与头部之间气流和谐方面的特征性能力。气逆（下部气机紊乱失调）可能表现为多种方式。在头部，引起头痛、眩晕和面赤；在颈部，气滞导致瘰疬和瘿瘤；在中上焦，损伤胃气和肺气的正常肃降，引起呕吐、霍乱吐泻、喘息、哮喘和胸中胀满。

根据周纸窗对《灵枢》上述段落的注释，气逆（紊乱、失调、上逆之气）是所有天窗穴主治临床表现的基础。气逆的表现之一是突然的剧烈疼痛，这与[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 现代在治疗急性腰扭伤以及身体任何部位扭伤和剧烈疼痛方面的重要应用相呼应。[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 调理气逆和气机上冲的能力也解释了其显著的降血压作用。

根据《灵枢·海论》篇，[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md)（与[REN17 膻中](/point/REN17.md)、[DU15 哑门](/point/DU15.md) 和[DU14 大椎](/point/DU14.md)）是“气海”穴之一。该段经文给出了气海不足和过剩的主治：“气海有余者，气满胸中，气急，面赤。气海不足者，则气少不能言”。[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 作为气海穴和天窗穴的双重且重叠的特性，突显了其在调和和重新分配身体紊乱之气方面的重要性。

早在《黄帝内经》时期，观察和触诊[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 的脉搏就被认为是一种重要的诊断方法。《素问·热论》在讨论小便不利时，描述了一种“身热如炭，颈胸如隔，[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 脉躁盛，气逆而喘”的证候。在《素问》其他地方，手腕脉与[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 脉的相对强弱被赋予重要的诊断意义，而张仲景则提及三种脉诊方法（上部[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md)、中部腕部脉、下部[ST42 冲阳](/point/ST42.md)）。由于这些方法固有的复杂性，它们在中国医学史上相对较早地被放弃，转而专注于腕部脉诊。

#### 临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)

* 天窗穴和气海穴，主治头痛、眩晕、面赤、呕吐和哮喘。
* 治疗急性腰扭伤和任何急性疼痛，可双侧取穴或取患侧。
* 治疗高血压。

#### 穴位组合 (Combinations)

* 霍乱吐泻，头痛，胸痛，喘鸣：[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md)，[PC6 内关](/point/PC6.md)，[SJ1 关冲](/point/SJ1.md)，[SP6 三阴交](/point/SP6.md) 和[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md)（《聚英》）。
* 瘰疬：灸[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 和[LI13 手五里](/point/LI13.md) 三十壮（《千金方》）。
* 耳鸣伴腰痛：先针[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md)，后针[SJ21 耳门](/point/SJ21.md) 和[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md)（《天星秘诀》）。
* 高血压：[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md)，[LI11 曲池](/point/LI11.md) 和[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md)。
* 高血压：[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md)，[DU20 百会](/point/DU20.md)，[LI11 曲池](/point/LI11.md) 和[LIV3 太冲](/point/LIV3.md)。
* 急性腰扭伤或任何急性疼痛：[ST9 人迎](/point/ST9.md) 双侧取穴或取患侧。
