### Heaven's Pivot TIĀNSHŪ
![[ST-25 天枢.png|250]]
- Front-Mu point of the Large Intestine

#### Location

On the abdomen, 2 cun lateral to the umbilicus.

#### Location Notes

The 2 cun line on the abdomen is located halfway between the midline and the palpable lateral border of the rectus abdominis muscle.

#### Needling

_i_. Perpendicular insertion 1 to 1.5 cun; _ii_. oblique inferior insertion towards the uterus for diseases of the uterus.  
**Caution:** in thin subjects, deep needling may penetrate the peritoneal cavity.

#### Actions

- Regulates the intestines
- Regulates the Spleen and Stomach
- Resolves dampness and damp-heat
- Regulates qi and blood and eliminates stagnation

#### Indications

- Diarrhoea, Spleen-diarrhoea, persistent diarrhoea with undigested food (in the stool), dysenteric disorder, borborygmus, lower yuan (origin) deficient and cold, constipation.
- Oedema, drum distention, swelling of the face, turbid painful urinary dysfunction.
- Vomiting, retching, difficult ingestion, poor appetite, sudden turmoil disorder.
- Abdominal pain, abdominal distention with laboured breathing, intestinal abscess, generalised swelling with severe cutting periumbilical pain, umbilical shan disorder with localised pain that periodically surges up to the Heart, shan disorder, running piglet qi.
- Abdominal (zheng jia) masses in women, pain of the uterus, dysmenorrhoea, irregular menstruation, infertility, red and white leucorrhoea.
- Cold shivering from malaria, severe heat with manic raving.

#### Commentary

The close relationship between the Stomach and intestines is emphasised in the Spiritual Pivot[11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) which states "the Small and Large Intestines both pertain to the Stomach". Furthermore, the Stomach is interiorly-exteriorly coupled with the Spleen which dominates transportation and transformation and plays an important role in regulating the intestines. Tiānshū ST-25 is the front-mu point of the Large Intestine, located on the Stomach channel, and is thus the point where the qi of the Large Intestine gathers and concentrates on the anterior surface of the body.

Tiānshū ST-25, located on either side of the umbilicus, was said by the Ode to Elucidate Mysteries to dominate the middle region[12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references), and its name 'Heaven's Pivot' or 'Celestial Pivot' refers to its central position between the upper and lower abdomen, the Stomach and the intestines. The Essential Questions states "[The area] above the celestial pivot is ruled by celestial qi; [the area] below the celestial pivot is ruled by the earthly qi. The place where these qi intersect is the origin of man's qi and the ten thousand things"[13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references).

The two principal actions of Tiānshū ST-25 are i. treating intestinal disorders, and ii. regulating qi and eliminating stasis in the lower abdomen. Tiānshū ST-25 is the single most important point for the treatment of the widest variety of intestinal disorders. Due to its ability to regulate the Spleen and transform dampness, its forte is in the treatment of diarrhoea; indeed the Ode of the Jade Dragon states "The pattern of Spleen diarrhoea, look no further, needle bilateral Tiānshū ST-25". Diarrhoea and dysenteric disorder may be acute and due to accumulation of cold-damp, damp-heat or fire-poison, or may be chronic and due to deficiency of the Spleen and Kidneys or disharmony of the Liver and Spleen. Whatever the aetiology, Tiānshū ST-25 should form a pivotal role in the prescription, in combination with other suitable points selected according to differentiation. In modern practice Tiānshū ST-25 is much used in the treatment of bacillary dysentery and acute simple appendicitis in combination with such points as [Shàngjùxū ST-37](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-37) and [Qūchí LI-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-11). Although an important local point in the treatment of constipation, many authorities consider the more lateral [Dàhéng SP-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-15) to be more effective.

The damp draining action of Tiānshū ST-25 is further evidenced by its indications for oedema, swelling of the face, drum distention and turbid painful urinary dysfunction.

Because of its central position between the upper and lower abdomen, the effect of Tiānshū ST-25 extends upwards to the Stomach. It is indicated for vomiting, retching and sudden turmoil disorder in which vomiting is accompanied by diarrhoea. Various classics also emphasise the ability of Tiānshū ST-25 to tonify deficiency, the Investigation Into Points Along the Channels recommending it for 'lower yuan (origin) deficient and cold' and the Ode to Elucidate Mysteries for 'deficiency damage'.

Tiānshū ST-25 also has an important action on regulating qi and eliminating stagnation in the lower abdomen. It is indicated for a wide range of problems including distention and pain of the abdomen, intestinal abscess, shan disorder, severe cutting periumbilical pain etc., as well as the treatment of menstrual disorders and abdominal or uterine masses due to stasis of either qi or blood.

#### Summary of clinical application

- The principal local point for the treatment of intestinal diseases: appendicitis, diarrhoea, undigested food in the stool, dysenteric disorder, bacillary dysentry, borborygmus, constipation.
- Also treats vomiting, retching and poor appetite.
- Regulates qi and eliminates stagnation in the lower abdomen: abdominal pain and distention, intestinal abscess, shan disorder, abdominal masses in women, pain of the uterus, dysmenorrhoea, irregular menstruation.

#### Combinations

- Ceaseless diarrhoea: Tiānshū ST-25, [Zhōngwǎn REN-12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-12) and [Zhōngjí REN-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-3) (Great Compendium).
- Undigested food (in the stool), no pleasure in eating, periumbilical pain: Tiānshū ST-25, [Nèitíng ST-44](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-44) and [Lìduì ST-45](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-45) (Supplementing Life).
- Red (bloody) dysenteric disorder: Tiānshū ST-25, [Nèitíng ST-44](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-44), [Yǐnbài SP-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-1), [Qìhǎi REN-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-6), [Zhàohǎi KID-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-6) and [Nèiguān PC-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/PC-6) (Great Compendium).
- Vomiting and sudden turmoil disorder: Tiānshū ST-25 and [Zhīgōu SJ-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-6) (Supplementing Life).
- Irregular menstruation: Tiānshū ST-25 and [Shuǐquán KID-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-5) (One Hundred Symptoms).
- Umbilical shan disorder: Tiānshū ST-25, [Shénquè REN-8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-8) and [Shímén REN-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-5) (Supplementing Life).
- Hypogastric shan disorder: Tiānshū ST-25, [Shímén REN-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-5), [Qìhǎi REN-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-6) and [Shénquè REN-8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-8) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Facial swelling: Tiānshū ST-25, [Fēnglóng ST-40](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-40), [Chōngyáng ST-42](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-42), [Xiàngǔ ST-43](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-43) and [Lìduì ST-45](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-45) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).

### [ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) (TIĀNSHŪ)

* **英文名称：** Heaven's Pivot
* **中文名称：** 天枢
* **国际代码：** ST-25
* **穴位属性：** [ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 是大肠经的腹募穴。

#### 定位 (Location)

位于腹部，脐旁开2寸处。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 腹部旁开2寸线位于前正中线与可触及的腹直肌外缘之间中点。

#### 针刺方法 (Needling)

i. 垂直刺1至1.5寸； ii. 治疗子宫疾病时，可向下斜刺，针尖朝向子宫。

* **注意 (Caution):** 瘦弱患者深刺可能刺穿腹膜腔。

#### 作用 (Actions)

* 调理肠道
* 调理脾胃
* 化湿，清利湿热
* 调理气血，消积化滞

#### 主治病症 (Indications)

腹泻，脾虚泻，久泻完谷不化，痢疾，肠鸣，下元虚寒，便秘。
水肿，鼓胀，面部肿胀，淋证（小便混浊疼痛）。
呕吐，干呕，吞咽困难，食欲不振，霍乱吐泻。
腹痛，腹胀伴呼吸困难，肠痈，全身肿胀伴脐周剧烈绞痛，脐疝，奔豚气。
女性腹部癥瘕积聚，子宫疼痛，痛经，月经不调，不孕症，赤白带下。
疟疾恶寒发抖，高热伴狂言谵语。

#### 阐释 (Commentary)

《灵枢》强调了胃与肠道的密切关系，其中记载“小肠、大肠皆属于胃”。此外，胃与脾互为表里，脾主运化，在调理肠道方面发挥重要作用。[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 是大肠经的募穴，位于胃经上，因此是大肠之气在身体前部汇聚和集中的穴位。

[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 位于脐旁两侧，《标幽赋》认为它主宰中焦，其名称“天枢”或“天枢”指的是其位于上腹部和下腹部之间、胃与肠道之间的中心位置。《素问·离合真邪论》记载：“天枢之上，天气主之；天枢之下，地气主之。其交人中，人气之所生，万物之所祖也。”（此引用可能与原文有出入，但大意是此穴为天地之气交汇，人体气机之枢纽。）

[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 的两个主要作用是：i. 治疗肠道疾病；ii. 调理下腹部气血，消积化滞。[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 是治疗各种肠道疾病最重要的穴位。由于其调理脾胃、化湿的能力，其特长是治疗腹泻；《玉龙歌》记载：“脾泻之症实难医，只须[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 两边推。”腹泻和痢疾可能是急性病症，由寒湿、湿热或火毒积聚引起，也可能是慢性病症，由脾肾不足或肝脾不和引起。无论病因如何，[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 都应在处方中发挥关键作用，并结合辨证选取其他合适的穴位。在现代实践中，[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 广泛用于治疗细菌性痢疾和急性单纯性阑尾炎，常与[ST37 上巨虚](point/ST37.md) 和[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md) 等穴联合使用。尽管它是治疗便秘的重要局部穴位，但许多医家认为更偏外侧的[SP15 大横](point/SP15.md) 更有效。

[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 利湿的作用还体现在其主治水肿、面部肿胀、鼓胀和淋证（小便混浊疼痛）。

由于[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 位于上腹部和下腹部之间，其作用向上延伸至胃。它主治呕吐、干呕和霍乱吐泻（伴有呕吐和腹泻）。各种经典也强调[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 补益虚损的能力，《循经考穴编》推荐其治疗“下元虚寒”，《标幽赋》推荐其治疗“虚损”。

[ST25 天枢](point/ST25.md) 还在调理下腹部气血、消积化滞方面发挥重要作用。它主治多种问题，包括腹部胀痛、肠痈、疝气、脐周剧烈绞痛等，以及治疗因气滞或血瘀引起的月经不调和腹部或子宫肿块。

#### 临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)

* 治疗肠道疾病的主要局部穴位：阑尾炎，腹泻，大便完谷不化，痢疾，细菌性痢疾，肠鸣，便秘。
* 也治疗呕吐，干呕和食欲不振。
* 调理下腹部气血，消积化滞：腹痛腹胀，肠痈，疝气，女性腹部肿块，子宫疼痛，痛经，月经不调。
