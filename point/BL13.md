### Lung Shu FÈISHŪ
![[BL-13 肺俞.png|250]]
- Back-Shu point of the Lung

#### Location

1.5 cun lateral to the lower border of the spinous process of the third thoracic vertebra (T3).

#### Location Notes

Locate at the visible highest point of the paraspinal muscles.

#### Needling

Oblique insertion towards the spine, 0.5 to 1 cun, or transverse-oblique insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.  
**Caution:** perpendicular needling or oblique needling away from the spine carries a substantial risk of causing a pneumothorax.

#### Actions

- Tonifies Lung qi and nourishes Lung yin
- Descends and disseminates Lung qi
- Clears heat from the Lung
- Releases the exterior

#### Indications

- Cough, dyspnoea, asthma, fullness of the chest, shortness of breath with no desire to speak, persistent cough in children, cold Lung, heat sensation in the chest, chest pain, difficult breathing on lying down, coughing of phlegm, Lung abscess, attack of the Lung by wind, rapid pulse, fever, night sweating with chills and fever and aversion to cold, cold shivering, absence of sweating, throat painful obstruction.
- Lung atrophy, consumption, consumption with steaming bone disorder, deficiency-taxation fever, tidal fever, night sweating, spitting blood, wasting and thirsting disorder, dryness of the mouth and tongue, deficiency agitation.
- Mania, heat in the body, mad walking with desire to commit suicide, epilepsy.
- Fullness with no pleasure in eating, vomiting, vomiting fluid after eating, vomiting of foam, goitre, jaundice, pain of the flesh and itching of the skin, urticaria.
- Pain of the upper back and shoulder, crooked spine, turtle back in children, lumbar pain and stiffness.

#### Commentary

Fèishū BL-13 is the back-shu point of the Lung, where the qi of the Lung emanates from the interior to the body surface, and in common with all the back-shu points, especially those of the yin zang, has a strong action on regulating and tonifying its corresponding zangfu at the deepest level. Fèishū BL-13 is the pre-eminent acupuncture point to treat all disorders of the Lung zang.

Fèishū BL-13 is an essential point to tonify the Lung qi and nourish Lung yin. Lung qi deficiency may derive from constitutional deficiency, previous Lung disease, chronic illness, extreme sadness and grief, excessive desk work (especially with a hunched back), excessive physical labour or lack of exercise. It is characterised by symptoms such as weak cough, dyspnoea and asthma that are worsened by exertion, and shortness of breath with no desire to speak. According to a saying of Chinese medicine "The Lung is the gateway of the voice". When there is insufficiency of Lung qi therefore, there is lack of vigour in the voice, speaking readily tires a person and there is unwillingness to speak. According to the Essential Questions[9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "All qi is subordinate to the Lung". Since the Lung dominates the qi of the whole body, there may also be signs of general qi deficiency such as lassitude, facial pallor and a weak pulse. In such cases Fèishū BL-13 is often combined with points such as [Tàiyuān LU-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-9), [Shānzhōng REN-17](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-17), [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36) and [Píshū BL-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-20). In cases of chronic Lung qi deficiency giving rise to asthma or cough that is worse in winter, intensive moxibustion is often applied to Fèishū BL-13 in the summer months.

Lung yin deficiency may derive from prolonged Lung qi deficiency, overwork, febrile disease which consumes yin, or Kidney yin deficiency which fails to nourish and support Lung yin. It is characterised by cough or asthma with dry, stringy or blood-tinged sputum, night sweating and dryness of the mouth, throat and tongue. In more severe cases there may be tidal fever, steaming bone disorder, deficiency agitation and spitting of blood. In such cases Fèishū BL-13 is often combined with points such as [Gāohuāngshū BL-43](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-43), [Tàiyuān LU-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-9), [Guānyuán REN-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-4) and [Shènshū BL-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-23).

Fèishū BL-13 is also classically indicated for Lung atrophy and consumption, both terms denoting conditions of severe exhaustion and depletion. In Lung atrophy, the Lungs are said to wither and shrivel, in the same way that the limbs are seen to wither and shrivel in atrophy disorder, whilst consumption broadly corresponds to pulmonary tuberculosis.

The action of Fèishū BL-13 is not restricted to tonifying and nourishing the Lung however, and it is equally important in all excess Lung patterns. Through its actions of regulating Lung qi and clearing heat, it may be used for such patterns as i. excess heat afflicting the Lung, ii. retention of phlegm-damp or phlegm-heat, and iii. toxic heat obstructing the Lung. It is consequently indicated for fullness of the chest, chest pain, Lung abscess, coughing of phlegm and difficult breathing on lying down. In such cases, in addition to other appropriate points, Fèishū BL-13 is often combined with [Zhōngfǔ LU-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-1), an example of the principle of combining the back-shu and front-mu points.

Fèishū BL-13 is also an important point for the treatment of pathogenic factors lodging at the defensive (or taiyang) level. Combined with points such as [Fēngmén BL-12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-12), [Lièquē LU-7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-7) and [Hégǔ LI-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-4) it may be used to release and expel pathogenic factors binding the exterior and giving rise to chills and fever with cough, and to harmonise nutritive qi and defensive qi in cases of night sweating with chills and fever and aversion to cold.

According to Warp and Woof of Warm Febrile Diseases "The Lung and Heart are mutually connected, thus when there is Lung heat it most easily enters the Heart". Because of its ability to clear heat of excess or deficiency type from the Lung, and due to its profound effect on the gathering qi and the upper jiao in general, the action of Fèishū BL-13 extends to the Heart and various classics mention its use for disorders of the spirit such as mania, mad walking and even desire to commit suicide.

The Lung and Stomach have a particularly close relationship. Both have a strongly descending action, whilst the Lung channel originates in the middle jiao in the region of the Stomach. Failure of the Stomach qi to descend due to deficiency or food stagnation may therefore impair the Lung's descending function leading to coughing and dyspnoea, whilst failure of the Lung qi to descend may injure the Stomach's descending function and lead to rebellion of Stomach qi. Fèishū BL-13 is therefore indicated for fullness with no pleasure in eating, vomiting, vomiting of foam and vomiting of fluid after eating, and especially for vomiting accompanied by coughing.

A further important use of Fèishū BL-13 is for Lung disorders which give rise to pain of the upper back. In cases of children with severe Lung qi deficiency, there may even be rounding and deformity of the back (turtle back). This condition is referred to in the Essential Questions[10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) which says "The back is the residence of the chest and when the back is curved and the shoulders are hunched, the residence is about to fall into ruin".

Finally, according to the Illustrated Appendices to The Classic of Categories, Fèishū BL-13 is one of five points (Fèishū BL-13, [Xīnshū BL-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-15), [Gānshū BL-18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-18), [Píshū BL-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-20) and [Shènshū BL-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-23)) which "drain heat from the five zang".

#### Summary of clinical application

- Lung back-shu point for all Lung patterns, both deficient and excess: cough, dyspnoea, asthma, fullness of the chest, shortness of breath with no desire to speak, persistent cough in children, cold Lung, heat sensation in the chest, chest pain, difficult breathing on lying down, coughing of phlegm, Lung abscess, attack of the Lung by wind, fever, night sweating with chills and fever and aversion to cold, cold shivering, absence of sweating, Lung atrophy, consumption, consumption with steaming bone disorder, deficiency-taxation fever, tidal fever, night sweating, spitting blood, wasting and thirsting disorder, dryness of the mouth and tongue.
- Descends Stomach qi: fullness with no desire to eat, vomiting, vomiting fluid after eating, vomiting of foam.
- Pain of the upper back and shoulder, crooked spine, ‘turtle back’ in children.

#### Combinations

- Cough with phlegm: Fèishū BL-13 and [Fēnglóng ST-40](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-40) (Ode of the Jade Dragon).
- Cough that reaches the voice (hoarse voice): Fèishū BL-13 and [Tiāntú REN-22](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-22) (One Hundred Symptoms).
- Lung distention: Fèishū BL-13 and [Tàiyuān LU-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-9) (Systematic Classic).
- Chest pain: Fèishū BL-13, [Yúnmén LU-2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-2), [Zhōngfǔ LU-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-1), [Yǐnbài SP-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-1), [Qímén LIV-14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LIV-14), [Húnmén BL-47](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-47) and [Dàlíng PC-7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/PC-7) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Spitting blood: Fèishū BL-13, [Kǒngzuì LU-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-6) and [Qūzé PC-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/PC-3) (Supplementing Life).
- Seasonal fever: Fèishū BL-13 and [Táodào DU-13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-13) (Glorious Anthology).
- Mad walking with desire to commit suicide: Fèishū BL-13 and [Fēngfǔ DU-16](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-16) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Stiffness of the lumbar region and back with inability to bend to the side: Fèishū BL-13 and [Yāoshū DU-2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-2) (Great Compendium).

### **[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** (FÈISHŪ)

* **英文名称：** Lung Shu
* **中文名称：** 肺俞
* **国际代码：** BL-13
* **穴位属性：** **[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 是肺的背俞穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于第三胸椎（T3）棘突下缘旁开1.5寸。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 位于脊柱旁肌肉的最高点。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

斜刺，向脊柱方向0.5至1寸，或横斜刺1至1.5寸。

* **注意 (Caution):** 垂直针刺或向远离脊柱方向斜刺有很大风险引起气胸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 补益肺气，滋养肺阴
* 宣肺降气
* 清泄肺热
* 解表

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

咳嗽，呼吸困难，哮喘，胸中胀满，气短不欲言，小儿久咳，肺寒，胸中灼热感，胸痛，卧位呼吸困难，咳痰，肺痈，风邪袭肺，脉速，发热，盗汗伴恶寒发热怕冷，恶寒发抖，无汗，咽喉肿痛。
肺痿，痨瘵（肺痨），肺痨伴骨蒸潮热，虚劳发热，潮热，盗汗，咯血，消渴病，口干舌燥，虚烦。
癫狂，身热，循衣摸床伴欲自杀，癫痫。
食欲不振，呕吐，食后吐水，呕吐泡沫，瘿瘤，黄疸，肌肉疼痛伴皮肤瘙痒，荨麻疹。
上背部和肩部疼痛，脊柱歪斜，小儿龟背，腰痛僵硬。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 是肺的背俞穴，是肺气从内向体表输布的地方。与所有背俞穴一样，特别是阴脏的背俞穴，它在最深层次上调理和补益其相应的脏腑。**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 是治疗所有肺脏疾病的首要穴位。

**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 是补益肺气、滋养肺阴的关键穴位。肺气虚可源于先天不足、既往肺病、慢性疾病、极度悲伤忧虑、过度伏案工作（特别是弓背）、过度体力劳动或缺乏锻炼。其特点是咳嗽无力、呼吸困难和哮喘（劳累后加重），以及气短不欲言。中医有句俗语：“肺为声音之门户”。因此，肺气不足时，声音无力，说话易疲倦，不愿多言。根据《素问·阴阳应象大论》记载：“诸气者，皆属于肺”。由于肺主一身之气，也可能出现全身气虚的症状，如倦怠、面色苍白、脉弱。在这种情况下，**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 常与**[LU9 太渊](/point/LU9.md)**、**[REN17 膻中](/point/REN17.md)**、**[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md)** 和**[BL20 脾俞](/point/BL20.md)** 等穴联合使用。在慢性肺气虚导致冬季哮喘或咳嗽加重时，常在夏季对**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 进行强化艾灸。

肺阴虚可源于长期肺气虚、过劳、热病耗伤阴液，或肾阴不足不能滋养肺阴。其特点是咳嗽或哮喘伴痰干、痰丝状或痰中带血、盗汗、口干咽燥。更严重者可出现潮热、骨蒸潮热、虚烦和咯血。在这种情况下，**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 常与**[BL43 膏肓俞](/point/BL43.md)**、**[LU9 太渊](/point/LU9.md)**、**[REN4 关元](/point/REN4.md)** 和**[BL23 肾俞](/point/BL23.md)** 等穴联合使用。

**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 在经典中也主治肺痿和痨瘵，两者都指严重的耗竭状态。肺痿是指肺脏萎缩枯槁，如同痿证中肢体萎缩枯槁一样，而痨瘵大致对应肺结核。

然而，**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 的作用不仅限于补益和滋养肺脏，它在所有肺实证中同样重要。通过其调理肺气和清热的作用，可用于以下证型：i. 肺热实证，ii. 痰湿或痰热内停，以及 iii. 热毒壅肺。因此，它主治胸中胀满、胸痛、肺痈、咳痰和卧位呼吸困难。在这种情况下，除了其他合适穴位外，**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 常与**[LU1 中府](/point/LU1.md)** 联合使用，这是背俞穴与募穴合用的原则的一个例子。

**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 也是治疗病邪留滞卫分（或太阳经）的重要穴位。与**[BL12 风门](/point/BL12.md)**、**[LU7 列缺](/point/LU7.md)** 和**[LI4 合谷](/point/LI4.md)** 等穴联合使用，可用于解表祛邪，治疗风寒束表引起的恶寒发热伴咳嗽，以及在盗汗伴恶寒发热怕冷的情况下调和营卫之气。

根据《温病条辨》记载：“肺与心通，肺热最易入心。”因为其能够清泻肺部实热或虚热，以及其对宗气和整个上焦的深远影响，**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 的作用延伸至心脏，各种经典文献提及它可用于治疗精神疾病，如癫狂、循衣摸床甚至欲自杀。

肺和胃有特别密切的关系。两者都有很强的下降作用，而肺经起源于中焦的胃部区域。因此，胃气不降（因虚或食滞）可能影响肺的肃降功能，导致咳嗽和呼吸困难，而肺气不降可能损伤胃的下降功能，导致胃气上逆。因此，**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 主治食欲不振伴胀满、呕吐、食后吐水、呕吐泡沫，特别是伴有咳嗽的呕吐。

**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 的另一个重要用途是治疗引起上背部疼痛的肺部疾病。在小儿肺气虚弱严重的情况下，甚至可能出现背部弯曲变形（龟背）。《素问·脉要精微论》中提及此症，谓“背者，胸之府也，背曲而肩随，其府将坏矣。”

最后，根据《类经图翼》记载，**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)** 是“泻五脏之热”的五个穴位之一（**[BL13 肺俞](/point/BL13.md)**、**[BL15 心俞](/point/BL15.md)**、**[BL18 肝俞](/point/BL18.md)**、**[BL20 脾俞](/point/BL20.md)** 和**[BL23 肾俞](/point/BL23.md)**）。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 肺的背俞穴，治疗所有肺部证型，包括虚证和实证：咳嗽，呼吸困难，哮喘，胸中胀满，气短不欲言，小儿久咳，肺寒，胸中灼热感，胸痛，卧位呼吸困难，咳痰，肺痈，风邪袭肺，发热，盗汗伴恶寒发热怕冷，恶寒发抖，无汗，肺痿，痨瘵，痨瘵伴骨蒸潮热，虚劳发热，潮热，盗汗，咯血，消渴病，口干舌燥。
* 肃降胃气：食欲不振伴胀满，呕吐，食后吐水，呕吐泡沫。
* 治疗上背部和肩部疼痛，脊柱歪斜，小儿“龟背”。
