### Jueyin Shu JUÉYĪNSHŪ
![[BL-14 心包俞.png|250]]
- Back-Shu point of the Pericardium

#### Location

1.5 cun lateral to the lower border of the spinous process of the fourth thoracic vertebra (T4).

#### Location Notes

Locate at the visible highest point of the paraspinal muscles.

#### Needling

Oblique insertion towards the spine, 0.5 to 1 cun, or transverse-oblique insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.  
**Caution:** perpendicular needling or oblique needling away from the spine carries a substantial risk of causing a pneumothorax.

#### Actions

- Spreads Liver qi and unbinds the chest
- Regulates the Heart
- Regulates and descends qi

#### Indications

- Heart pain, oppression of the chest, pain of the chest and diaphragm due to accumulation of qi, palpitations, agitation and restlessness, agitation and oppression, restless zang disorder.
- Cough, shortness of breath, rebellious qi vomiting, toothache.

#### Commentary

Juéyīnshū BL-14 is an exception among the back-shu points in that all the others are named after their respective zang or fu. The fact that Juéyīnshū BL-14 is named after the jueyin (Pericardium and Liver) channel emphasises its ability to unbind the chest by invigorating the circulation of qi. When Liver qi stagnates, usually as a result of emotional factors, it frequently binds the qi of the upper jiao giving rise to symptoms such as pain and oppression of the Heart, chest and diaphragm, restlessness, agitation and oppression.

As the back-shu point of the Pericardium, Juéyīnshū BL-14 is not exclusively reserved for chest and Heart disorders due to qi stagnation, but in combination with [Xīnshū BL-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-15) is widely used to regulate the Heart in many kinds of dysfunction.

The descending function of both the Stomach and Lung is assisted by the smooth and unobstructed movement of Liver qi. When Liver qi stagnates and impairs their descent, there may be cough and vomiting respectively.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Back-shu point of the Pericardium (jueyin) which is intimately linked to the Liver (jueyin).
- Regulates qi in the chest, Heart and Lung: pain or oppression of the Heart, chest and diaphragm due to accumulation of qi, palpitations, cough, shortness of breath, rebellious qi vomiting.

#### Combinations

- Heart pain: Juéyīnshū BL-14, [Shénmén HE-7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/HE-7) and [Zúlínqì GB-41](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-41) (Supplementing Life).

### **[BL14 厥阴俞](/point/BL14.md)** (JUÉYĪNSHŪ)

* **英文名称：** Jueyin Shu
* **中文名称：** 厥阴俞
* **国际代码：** BL-14
* **穴位属性：** **[BL14 厥阴俞](/point/BL14.md)** 是心包的背俞穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于第四胸椎（T4）棘突下缘旁开1.5寸。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 位于脊柱旁肌肉的最高点。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

斜刺，向脊柱方向0.5至1寸，或横斜刺1至1.5寸。

* **注意 (Caution):** 垂直针刺或向远离脊柱方向斜刺有很大风险引起气胸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 疏肝理气，宽胸散结
* 调理心脏
* 调理降气

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

心痛，胸部憋闷，气积胸膈疼痛，心悸，烦躁不安，烦躁憋闷，脏躁。
咳嗽，气短，气逆呕吐，牙痛。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[BL14 厥阴俞](/point/BL14.md)** 是背俞穴中的一个例外，因为所有其他背俞穴都以其相应的脏腑命名。**[BL14 厥阴俞](/point/BL14.md)** 以厥阴（心包和肝）命名，强调其通过活络气机以宽胸散结的能力。当肝气郁结时（通常是情志因素所致），常会阻滞上焦气机，引起心痛、胸膈疼痛、烦躁不安、憋闷等症状。

作为心包的背俞穴，**[BL14 厥阴俞](/point/BL14.md)** 不仅限于治疗气滞引起的心胸疾病，而是与**[BL15 心俞](/point/BL15.md)** 联合广泛用于调理心脏的多种功能失调。

胃气和肺气的下降功能都依赖于肝气顺畅无阻的运行。当肝气郁滞并影响其下降时，可能分别引起咳嗽和呕吐。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 心包（厥阴）的背俞穴，与肝（厥阴）密切相关。
* 调理胸部、心脏和肺部气机：气积导致的心痛、胸膈疼痛，心悸，咳嗽，气短，气逆呕吐。

#### **穴位组合 (Combinations)**

* 心痛：**[BL14 厥阴俞](/point/BL14.md)**，**[HE7 神门](/point/HE7.md)** 和 **[GB41 足临泣](/point/GB41.md)**（《资生经》）。
