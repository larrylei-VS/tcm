### Girdling Vessel DÀIMÀI
![[GB-26 带脉.png|250]]
- Meeting point of the Gall Bladder channel with the Girdling vessel

#### Location

Directly below [Zhāngmén LIV-13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LIV-13) (anterior and inferior to the free end of the eleventh rib), level with the umbilicus.

#### Location Notes

i. To locate the free end of the eleventh rib, place the entire hand on the upper abdomen and with gentle finger pressure palpate downwards along the costal margin, until the end of the eleventh rib is located just above the level of the umbilicus; ii. The free end of the eleventh rib usually lies on or near the mid-axillary line which is drawn vertically down from the apex of the axilla ([Jíquán HE-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/HE-1)).

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion, 0.5 to 1 cun.  
**Caution:** in thin subjects, deep needling may penetrate the peritoneal cavity.

#### Actions

- Regulates the Girdling vessel and drains dampness
- Regulates menstruation and stops leucorrhoea
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications

- Red and white leucorrhoea, disorders of menstruation, irregular menstruation, amenorrhoea, infer-tility, uterine prolapse, hardness and pain of the hypogastrium in women, lower abdominal pain in women.
- Unendurable pain of the lateral costal region which radiates to the back, pain of the lateral costal region, lumbar pain, shan disorder, tenesmus, clonic spasm.

#### Commentary

The Girdling vessel, encircling the waist like a belt, is the only major channel that runs horizontally. It passes through the points Dàimài GB-26, [Wǔshū GB-27](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-27) and [Wéidào GB-28](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-28), and encircles and binds the Penetrating, Conception and Governing vessels and the Kidney, Liver and Spleen channels. The Girdling vessel plays an important role in controlling leucorrhoea and may be damaged by turbid dampness which sinks downwards, deficiency of Kidney yin or Kidney yang, invasion of exterior dampness which transforms into heat, or pouring down of Liver and Gall Bladder channel damp-heat, all of which may result in leucorrhoea. In fact, the term 'dai xia' (leucorrhoea) is made up of the characters 'dai', meaning belt or girdle, and 'xia' meaning downward. As the most important point to regulate the qi of the Girdling vessel, Dàimài GB-26 is indispensable in the treatment of leucorrhoea resulting from any of the above patterns.

According to the Spiritual Pivot[7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-gall-bladder-channel/references) "The Penetrating and Conception vessels both start in the uterus", whilst Confucians' Duties to Their Parents, the 13th century classic by Zhang Cong-zheng, states "The Penetrating, Conception and Governing vessels have the same starting point but different circulation, the same source but different branches, each of them connects with the Girdling vessel". The Conception vessel, Penetrating vessel and Governing vessel therefore all originate in the uterus in females and are all bound by the Girdling vessel. Dàimài GB-26 is not just an important point to activate the Girdling vessel itself, but has a significant influence on the Conception and Penetrating vessels, and is able to treat disorders of the uterus and menstruation such as infertility, irregular menstruation and amenorrhoea, and sinking of qi giving rise to uterine prolapse.

As well as regulating the qi of the Girdling vessel, Dàimài GB-26 is effective as a local point to resolve stagnation of qi deriving from disharmony of the Liver. Disorder of the Girdling vessel and qi stagnation in the lower jiao may lead to pain, distention and hardness of the lower abdomen in women, pain of the lateral costal region, shan disorder and lumbar pain.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Important point in the treatment of leucorrhoea due to any aetiology.
- Treats disorders of the uterus and menstruation: infertility, irregular menstruation, amenorrhoea, uterine prolapse.
- Moves qi stagnation in the lower jiao: pain, distention and hardness of the lower abdomen in women, pain of the lateral costal region, shan disorder, lumbar pain.

### 1. [GB26 带脉](/point/GB26.md)

#### 1.1. Girdling Vessel 帶脈

- 胆经与带脉的交会穴

#### 1.2. 定位

位于[LIV13 章门](/point/LIV13.md)（第11肋游离端的前下方）正下方，与脐平齐。

#### 1.3. 定位说明

i. 为定位第11肋游离端，将整个手置于上腹部，用手指轻压沿肋缘向下探查，直至找到第11肋末端（略高于脐水平）；  
ii. 第11肋游离端通常位于或接近腋中线，腋中线从腋窝顶点（[HE1 极泉](/point/HE1.md)）垂直向下引出。

#### 1.4. 针刺方法

垂直刺入，深0.5至1寸。  
**注意：** 在瘦弱者中，深刺可能穿透腹膜腔。

#### 1.5. 作用

- 调带脉，泄湿
- 调经止带
- 活络止痛

#### 1.6. 主治

- 赤白带下、月经失调、不规则月经、闭经、不孕、子宫脱垂、女子脐下硬痛、女子下腹痛。
- 胁痛难忍放射至背、胁痛、腰痛、疝病、里急后重、痉挛。

#### 1.7. 论述

带脉如腰带般环绕腰部，是唯一水平走向的主要经脉。它经过[GB26 带脉](/point/GB26.md)、[GB27 五枢](/point/GB27.md)和[GB28 维道](/point/GB28.md)，环绕并约束冲脉、任脉、督脉及肾、肝、脾经。带脉在控制带下病中起重要作用，可能受浊湿下沉、肾阴或肾阳不足、外湿入侵转化为热，或肝胆经湿热下注的损害，导致带下病。实际上，“带下”一词由“带”（腰带）和“下”（向下）组成。作为调节带脉气的主要穴位，[GB26 带脉](/point/GB26.md)在治疗上述任何模式引起的带下病中不可或缺。

根据《灵枢》，“冲脉、任脉皆起于胞中”，而13世纪张从正的经典《儒门事亲》称：“冲、任、督脉同起点而异流，同源而异支，皆与带脉相连。”因此，任脉、冲脉和督脉均起于女子子宫，并受带脉约束。[GB26 带脉](/point/GB26.md)不仅是激活带脉本身的重要穴位，还对任脉和冲脉有显著影响，可治疗子宫和月经疾患，如不孕、不规则月经、闭经及气下沉引起的子宫脱垂。

除了调理带脉之气，[GB26 带脉](/point/GB26.md)作为局部穴位能有效疏解肝失调引起的下焦气滞。带脉失调及下焦气滞可能导致女子下腹痛胀硬、胁痛、疝病和腰痛。

#### 1.8. 临床应用总结

- 治疗任何病因引起的带下病的重要穴位。
- 治疗子宫和月经疾患：不孕、不规则月经、闭经、子宫脱垂。
- 疏通下焦气滞：女子下腹痛胀硬、胁痛、疝病、腰痛。
