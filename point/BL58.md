### Soaring Upwards FĒIYÁNG
![[BL-58 飞扬.png|250]]
- Luo-Connecting point of the Bladder channel

#### Location

On the lower leg, 7 cun directly superior to [Kūnlún BL-60](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-60), lateral to and approximately 1 cun inferior to [Chéngshān BL-57](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-57).

#### Needling

Perpendicular or oblique insertion directed proximally or distally, 1 to 1.5 cun.

#### Actions

- Harmonises the upper and lower
- Expels wind from taiyang channel
- Treats haemorrhoids
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications

- Heat in the head, dizziness, visual dizziness, headache and dizziness, pain of the neck and occiput, pain of the head and back, rhinitis, rhinitis with obstruction and pain of the head and back, nosebleed, nasal congestion, mania, epilepsy.
- Chills and fever, fever with absence of sweating, malaria, malaria with absence of thirst.
- Lumbar pain, heaviness of the body with inability to sit or stand, shivering with inability to sit or stand for long, atrophy disorder of the lower limb, coldness of the lower part of the body, weakness of the legs, difficulty in walking, swelling and pain of the lower limb, sciatica, wind painful obstruction of the joints, inability to flex or extend the toes.
- Haemorrhoids, swollen and painful haemorrhoids, bleeding haemorrhoids.

#### Commentary

Fēiyáng BL-58 is the luo-connecting point of the Bladder channel. An examination of its modern application compared with its traditional indications reveals a significant disparity. Clinically it is most commonly used nowadays for disorders of the lower limb and lumbar pain, particularly for sciatic pain which is located either along the course of both the Bladder and Gall Bladder channels, or between these two channels. This reflects the location of Fēiyáng BL-58 at the place where the Bladder channel, which runs down the back of the leg from [Chéngfú BL-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-36), passes laterally towards the Gall Bladder channel on the lower leg.

Its traditional indications however, show a much wider application, characterised by excess in the upper region, sometimes with accompanying deficiency below (a pattern which may explain the name of this point, 'Soaring Upwards' as well as its alternative name 'Flying Yang)'.

From Fēiyáng BL-58 the luo-connecting channel meets with the Kidney channel, strengthening the relationship between these coupled channels. According to the Guide to the Classics of Acupuncture "the luo-connecting points are located between two channels ... if they are punctured, symptoms of the interiorly-exteriorly related channels can be treated"[69](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references). When the Kidneys are depleted there may be deficiency below manifesting as lumbar pain, coldness of the lower part of the body, inability to stand and weakness of the legs. At the same time there may be excessive yang rising up the coupled Bladder channel to the head and manifesting as dizziness, headache, pain of the neck and occiput, heat in the head and nosebleed.

As the Bladder channel descends towards the foot, its points are increasingly indicated for disorders of the spirit such as mania, and Fēiyáng BL-58 is the first of these. The Bladder primary channel connects with the Governing vessel at points [Táodào DU-13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-13), [Dàzhuī DU-14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-14), [Nǎohù DU-17](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-17) and [Bǎihuì DU-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-20), where it enters the brain, whilst the Bladder divergent channel enters the Heart. Since both the brain and the Heart have been cited as the residence of the spirit in different traditions of Chinese medicine, these two channel pathways help explain the ability of points such as Fēiyáng BL-58 to calm the spirit and treat disorders of the brain such as mania and epilepsy.

When pathogenic factors, principally exterior wind, attack the taiyang channel they may give rise to chills and fever or fever with absence of sweating, as well as various symptoms affecting the head such as pain of the neck and occiput, heat in the head, nasal congestion and rhinitis, nosebleed and dizziness. Fēiyáng BL-58, a distal point of the foot taiyang channel, is able to expel pathogens from the channel, release the exterior and clear the upper portion of the channel.

The Bladder divergent channel encircles the anal region, and like [Chéngjīn BL-56](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-56) and [Chéngshān BL-57](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-57), Fēiyáng BL-58 is classically indicated for the treatment of haemorrhoids, swollen and painful haemorrhoids and bleeding haemorrhoids.

Finally, the Great Compendium of Acupuncture and Moxibustion gives specific indications for excess and deficiency of the luo-connecting points. In the case of Fēiyáng BL-58, these are rhinitis with obstruction and pain of the head and back (excess); rhinitis with nosebleed (deficiency).

#### Summary of clinical application

- For fullness above (heat in the head, headache, dizziness, pain of the neck and occiput, pain of the head and back, rhinitis, nosebleed, nasal congestion etc.) and deficiency below (lumbar pain, coldness of the lower part of the body, weakness of the legs etc.).
- For sciatic pain which is located either along the course of both the Bladder and Gall Bladder channels, or between these two channels.

#### Combinations

- Dizziness and eye pain: Fēiyáng BL-58 and [Yánggǔ SI-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-5) (Supplementing Life).
- Headache and dizziness: Fēiyáng BL-58, [Kūnlún BL-60](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-60), [Qūquán LIV-8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LIV-8), [Qiángǔ SI-2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-2), [Shàozé SI-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-1) and [Tōnglǐ HE-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/HE-5) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Heat in the head and rhinitis with nosebleed: Fēiyáng BL-58, [Chéngshān BL-57](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-57), [Kūnlún BL-60](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-60), [Jīnggǔ BL-64](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-64) and [Yǐnbài SP-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-1) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Neck pain, joint pain and sweating: Fēiyáng BL-58, [Yǒngquǎn KID-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-1) and [Hànyàn GB-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-4) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Madness and mania disorder with tongue thrusting: Fēiyáng BL-58, [Tàiyǐ ST-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-23) and [Huáròumén ST-24](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-24) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).

### **[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** (Fēiyáng)

* **英文名称：** Soaring Upwards
* **中文名称：** 飞扬
* **国际代码：** BL-58
* **穴位属性：** **[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 是膀胱经的络穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于小腿部，**[BL60 昆仑](point/BL60.md)** 上方7寸，在**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 外侧，下方约1寸。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

垂直或斜刺，针尖向近端或远端，1至1.5寸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 调和上下（上实下虚）
* 祛太阳经风邪
* 治疗痔疮
* 通经活络，止痛

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

头热，眩晕，视物眩晕，头痛眩晕，颈项和枕部疼痛，头背疼痛，鼻炎，鼻炎伴头背阻塞疼痛，鼻出血，鼻塞，癫狂，癫痫。
恶寒发热，发热无汗，疟疾，疟疾无渴。
腰痛，身体沉重不能坐立，战栗不能久坐久立，下肢痿证，下半身发冷，腿部无力，行走困难，下肢肿痛，坐骨神经痛，关节风痹，足趾屈伸不利。
痔疮，痔疮肿痛，痔疮出血。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 是膀胱经的络穴。对其现代应用与传统主治的比较揭示出显著差异。临床上，它现在最常用于治疗下肢疾病和腰痛，特别是沿膀胱经和胆经循行或介于两者之间的坐骨神经痛。这反映了**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 位于膀胱经从**[BL36 承扶](point/BL36.md)** 沿腿后侧下行，并向外侧通向小腿胆经的位置。

然而，其传统主治显示出更广泛的应用，特点是上部实证，有时伴有下部虚证（这种模式可能解释了此穴名称“飞扬”以及其别名“飞阳”）。

从**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 发出的络脉与肾经相交，强化了这些表里经脉之间的关系。根据《针灸大成》记载：“络穴位于两经之间……若刺之，可治表里经脉的病症”。当肾精不足时，可能出现下部虚证，表现为腰痛、下半身发冷、不能站立和腿部无力。同时，可能出现过盛的阳气沿相表里的膀胱经上冲头部，表现为眩晕、头痛、颈项和枕部疼痛、头热和鼻出血。

随着膀胱经向下肢循行，其穴位越来越主治癫狂等情志疾病，而**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 是其中的第一个。膀胱经正经在**[DU13 陶道](point/DU13.md)**、**[DU14 大椎](point/DU14.md)**、**[DU17 脑户](point/DU17.md)** 和**[DU20 百会](point/DU20.md)** 等穴与督脉交会，入脑，而膀胱经别则入心。由于脑和心在中医不同流派中都被认为是藏神之所，这两条经脉的循行路径有助于解释**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 等穴位宁心安神、治疗癫狂和癫痫等脑部疾病的能力。

当病邪（主要是外风）侵袭太阳经时，可能引起恶寒发热或发热无汗，以及各种影响头部的症状，如颈项和枕部疼痛、头热、鼻塞和鼻炎、鼻出血和眩晕。**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 作为足太阳经的远端穴位，能够祛除经络中的病邪，解表并清利经络上部。

膀胱经别环绕肛门区域，与**[BL56 承筋](point/BL56.md)** 和**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)** 一样，**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 在经典中主治痔疮、痔疮肿痛和痔疮出血。

最后，《针灸大成》列出了络穴虚实证的具体主治。**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 在实证时主治鼻炎伴头背部阻塞疼痛；虚证时主治鼻炎伴鼻出血。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 主治上部实（头热，头痛，眩晕，颈项和枕部疼痛，头背疼痛，鼻炎，鼻出血，鼻塞等）和下部虚（腰痛，下半身发冷，腿部无力等）。
* 治疗坐骨神经痛，其疼痛位于膀胱经和胆经循行路线或两者之间。

#### **穴位组合 (Combinations)**

* 眩晕和眼痛：**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)** 和 **[SI5 阳谷](point/SI5.md)**（《资生经》）。
* 头痛眩晕：**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)**，**[BL60 昆仑](point/BL60.md)**，**[LIV8 曲泉](point/LIV8.md)**，**[SI2 前谷](point/SI2.md)**，**[SI1 少泽](point/SI1.md)** 和 **[HE5 通里](point/HE5.md)**（《千金方》）。
* 头热和鼻炎伴鼻出血：**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)**，**[BL57 承山](point/BL57.md)**，**[BL60 昆仑](point/BL60.md)**，**[BL64 京骨](point/BL64.md)** 和 **[SP1 隐白](point/SP1.md)**（《千金方》）。
* 颈痛，关节痛和多汗：**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)**，**[KID1 涌泉](point/KID1.md)** 和 **[GB4 颔厌](point/GB4.md)**（《千金方》）。
* 狂病和癫狂伴舌体强硬：**[BL58 飞扬](point/BL58.md)**，**[ST23 太乙](point/ST23.md)** 和 **[ST24 滑肉门](point/ST24.md)**（《千金方》）。
