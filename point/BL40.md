### Middle of the Crook WĚIZHŌNG
![[BL-40 委中.png|250]]
- He-Sea and Earth point of the Bladder channel
- Gao Wu Command point
- Ma Dan-yang Heavenly Star point

#### Location

At the back of the knee, on the popliteal crease, in a depression midway between the tendons of biceps femoris and semitendinosus.

#### Location Notes

Locate with the knee slightly flexed.

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion 1 to 1.5 cun, or prick and bleed the superficial veins.  
Caution: the tibial nerve and the popliteal artery and vein lie deep to this point.

#### Actions

- Benefits the lumbar region and knees
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain
- Cools the blood
- Clears summer-heat and stops vomiting and diarrhoea
- Benefits the Bladder

#### Indications

- Pain and stiffness of the lumbar spine, heaviness of the lumbar region and buttock, cold sensation of the sacrum, coccyx and thigh, knee pain extending to the big toe, difficulty in flexing and extending the hip and knee joints, contraction of the sinews around the popliteal fossa, weakness of the legs, atrophy disorder and painful obstruction of the lower limb, wind painful obstruction, windstroke, hemiplegia.
- Injury by summer-heat, febrile disease with absence of sweating, injury by cold with heat of the four limbs, alternating chills and fever, malaria, ceaseless thirst, sudden turmoil disorder with abdominal pain, fullness of the lower abdomen, vomiting and diarrhoea, dysenteric disorder.
- Enuresis, difficult urination, dark urination, distention and pain of the hypogastrium.
- Nosebleed, pain of the lower teeth, headache, throat painful obstruction, epilepsy, haemorrhoid pain.
- Clove sores, erysipelas (cinnabar toxin), eczema, urticaria.

#### Commentary

Wěizhōng BL-40 is the he-sea point of the Bladder channel and has long been considered one of the most important of the acupuncture points. According to Ma Dan-yang the great physician of the Jin dynasty, Wěizhōng BL-40 is one of the 'eleven heavenly star points'[54](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references), indicated for "lumbar pain with inability to straighten up, severe lumbar pain that radiates up the back with pain and stiffness of the sinews and bones, wind painful obstruction that frequently recurs, and difficulty in stretching and bending the knee". The Ming dynasty author Gao Wu in the Glorious Anthology of Acupuncture and Moxibustion included it among his 'four command points' with a special action in the treatment of lumbar pain. Sun Si-miao in the Thousand Ducat Formulas says of Wěizhōng BL-40 "pain of the lumbar region and spine that almost reaches the head; in any case of heaviness and pain of the lumbar region and leg, bleed this point".

Wěizhōng BL-40 is equally effective for acute or chronic lumbar pain, whatever the aetiology, and for sciatic pain radiating down the Bladder channel, and is an important point for atrophy disorder and painful obstruction of the lower limb. In the case of acute lumbar sprain, Wěizhōng BL-40 may either be needled, or bled by the following method. The region of Wěizhōng BL-40 is examined (on the affected side if the sprain is unilateral and on both sides if bilateral). If dark and engorged blood vessels are seen at or around this point, the patient is asked to stand (resting their hands on the couch), the blood vessels are slapped to further engorge them and promote the flow of blood, then wiped with alcohol and quickly pricked to induce bleeding.

Wěizhōng BL-40 is also an important local point in the treatment of knee disorders, whether the location of the discomfort is at the back or the front of the knee, and needling at this point allows deep insertion into the joint.

An alternative name for Wěizhōng BL-40 used in a number of classics is Xue Xi, (Blood Xi-Cleft). According to the Spiritual Pivot[55](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "taiyang channel is abundant in blood and limited in qi ... [it is thus suitable to] prick to bleed taiyang and drain blood ...". This not only explains the powerful effect of bleeding Wěizhōng BL-40 to move blood stasis in acute lumbar sprain, but also its ability to cool the blood in cases of nosebleed and a variety of skin disorders, in which case the point may also be bled. Interestingly, one of the other two channels abundant in blood and limited in qi according to the Spiritual Pivot[56](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) is the jueyin channel, and this may help to explain certain similarities between Wěizhōng BL-40 and [Qūzé PC-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/PC-3), the he-sea point of the Pericardium jueyin channel. Both points (located in the flexure of the lower and upper limb respectively) are bled to clear heat from the blood level and are used in the treatment of summer-heat stroke, sudden turmoil disorder with heat of the four limbs, ceaseless thirst, vomiting and diarrhoea. As far as Wěizhōng BL-40 is concerned, not only is it indicated for fever due to summer-heat, but also for febrile disease with absence of sweating, injury by cold with heat of the four limbs, alternating chills and fever and malaria.

The Spiritual Pivot[57](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) states "When the disease is at the yang within yang (skin), needle the he-sea points of the yang channels". Whilst this is not a universally applicable principle, it is [Qūchí LI-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-11) and Wěizhōng BL-40, the he-sea points of the Large Intestine and Bladder channels, which are two of the most important points in the treatment of skin diseases. Wěizhōng BL-40 is indicated for erysipelas (cinnabar toxin), eczema and urticaria.

Relatively few points on the Bladder channel treat their related Bladder fu, notably [Pángguāngshū BL-28](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-28) (the back-shu point of the Bladder), [Shènshū BL-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/points/BL-23) (the back-shu point of the Kidneys), [Wěiyáng BL-39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-39) (the lower he-sea point of the Sanjiao) and points adjacent to the bladder on the lower back. Wěizhōng BL-40 itself is indicated for a variety of urinary disorders characterised by enuresis, difficult urination, dark urination, and distention and pain of the hypogastrium, and this may in part be explained by its close proximity to [Wěiyáng BL-39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-39).

The Bladder divergent channel separates from its primary channel at the popliteal fossa and ascends to wind around the anus. Wěizhōng BL-40 is indicated and included in classical combinations for the treatment of haemorrhoids, often in combination with [Chéngshān BL-57](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-57) which is considered the primary distal point for this purpose.

Finally, according to the Essential Questions[58](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) Wěizhōng BL-40 is one of the 'eight points for draining heat from the extremities' (although in fact only seven are listed) namely [Yúnmén LU-2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-2), [Jiānyú LI-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-15), Wěizhōng BL-40 and [Yāoshū DU-2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-2).

#### Summary of clinical application

- Much used for acute or chronic lumbar pain (needled or pricked to bleed), sciatica, knee disorders, weakness of the legs, atrophy disorder and painful obstruction of the lower limb, hemiplegia.
- Treats injury by summer-heat and other febrile diseases, especially with vomiting and diarrhoea. Also febrile disease with absence of sweating, injury by cold with heat of the four limbs, alternating chills and fever, malaria, ceaseless thirst, sudden turmoil disorder, dysenteric disorder.
- Clears heat from the blood: nosebleed, erysipelas, eczema, urticaria, clove sores.

### **[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** (Wěizhōng)

* **英文名称：** Middle of the Crook
* **中文名称：** 委中
* **国际代码：** BL-40
* **穴位属性：** **[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 是膀胱经的合穴和土穴，四总穴之一（腰背委中求），马丹阳天星穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于膝部后方，腘横纹中央，股二头肌腱与半腱肌腱之间的凹陷中。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 膝关节微屈时定位。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

垂直刺1至1.5寸，或点刺放血。

* **注意 (Caution):** 胫神经、腘动脉和腘静脉位于此穴深部。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 宣利腰膝
* 通经活络，止痛
* 凉血
* 清暑，止呕泻
* 宣利膀胱

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

腰脊疼痛僵硬，腰骶部沉重，骶骨、尾骨和股部寒冷感，膝痛放射至足大趾，髋膝关节屈伸不利，腘窝周围筋脉挛缩，腿部无力，下肢痿证和痹证，风痹，中风，半身不遂。
中暑，热病无汗，伤寒伴四肢发热，寒热往来，疟疾，口渴不止，霍乱吐泻伴腹痛，小腹胀满，呕吐腹泻，痢疾。
遗尿，小便不利，尿黄，少腹胀痛。
鼻出血，下牙痛，头痛，咽喉肿痛，癫痫，痔疮痛。
疔疮，丹毒（赤游丹），湿疹，荨麻疹。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 是膀胱经的合穴，长期以来被认为是针灸穴位中最重要的穴位之一。根据金代名医马丹阳的说法，**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 是“十一穴天星穴”之一，主治“腰痛不能直立，腰痛剧烈放射至背部，伴筋骨疼痛僵硬，风痹频发，以及膝关节屈伸不利”。明代高武在《针灸大成》中将其列入“四总穴”之一，专门治疗腰痛。孙思邈在《千金方》中论及**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 时说：“腰脊疼痛几及于头；凡腰腿沉重疼痛者，皆可点刺此穴放血。”

**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 对急性或慢性腰痛，无论病因如何，以及沿膀胱经向下放射的坐骨神经痛都同样有效，是治疗下肢痿证和痹证的重要穴位。在急性腰扭伤的情况下，**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 可针刺，或采用以下方法放血：检查**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 区域（如果扭伤是单侧则检查患侧，双侧则检查双侧）。如果在此穴或周围见到暗红色充盈的血管，请患者站立（双手扶着沙发），拍打血管使其进一步充盈并促进血液流动，然后用酒精擦拭并迅速点刺放血。

**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 也是治疗膝关节疾病的重要局部穴位，无论不适部位是膝盖后方还是前方，针刺此穴均可深达关节。

**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 在一些经典中还有别名“血郄”。根据《灵枢·经脉》记载：“太阳经多血少气……[因此适宜]点刺太阳放血……”。这不仅解释了点刺**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 放血在急性腰扭伤中活血化瘀的强大作用，也解释了其在鼻出血和多种皮肤病中凉血的能力，在这些情况下也可对此穴放血。有趣的是，根据《灵枢》，另外两条多血少气的经脉是厥阴经，这可能有助于解释**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 与心包厥阴经的合穴**[PC3 曲泽](/point/PC3.md)** 之间的某些相似之处。这两个穴位（分别位于下肢和上肢的屈侧）都可放血以清泻血分之热，用于治疗中暑、四肢发热的霍乱吐泻、口渴不止、呕吐腹泻。就**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 而言，它不仅主治中暑引起的发热，还主治热病无汗、伤寒伴四肢发热、寒热往来和疟疾。

《灵枢·本输》记载：“病在阳之阳者 [皮肤]，刺其合”。虽然这并非普遍适用的原则，但**[LI11 曲池](/point/LI11.md)** 和**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)**，分别是大肠经和膀胱经的合穴，是治疗皮肤病最重要的两个穴位。**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 主治丹毒（赤游丹）、湿疹和荨麻疹。

膀胱经上相对较少的穴位治疗其相关的膀胱腑，主要是**[BL28 膀胱俞](/point/BL28.md)**（膀胱的背俞穴）、**[BL23 肾俞](/point/BL23.md)**（肾的背俞穴）、**[BL39 委阳](/point/BL39.md)**（三焦的下合穴）以及膀胱附近腰部的穴位。**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 本身主治遗尿、小便不利、尿黄以及少腹胀痛等泌尿系统疾病，这部分可以解释为其与**[BL39 委阳](/point/BL39.md)** 的密切关系。

膀胱经别从腘窝分支，向上绕行肛门。**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 主治并被纳入经典组合中，用于治疗痔疮，常与被认为是此目的主要远端穴位的**[BL57 承山](/point/BL57.md)** 联合使用。

最后，根据《素问·热病论》记载，**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 是“泻诸肢节之热”的八个穴位之一（尽管实际上只列出了七个），即**[LU2 云门](/point/LU2.md)**、**[LI15 肩髃](/point/LI15.md)**、**[BL40 委中](/point/BL40.md)** 和**[DU2 腰俞](/point/DU2.md)**。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 广泛用于治疗急性或慢性腰痛（可针刺或点刺放血）、坐骨神经痛、膝关节疾病、腿部无力、下肢痿证和痹证、半身不遂。
* 治疗中暑及其他热病，特别是伴有呕吐腹泻者。也治疗热病无汗、伤寒伴四肢发热、寒热往来、疟疾、口渴不止、霍乱吐泻、痢疾。
* 凉血：鼻出血，丹毒，湿疹，荨麻疹，疔疮。
