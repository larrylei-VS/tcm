### Yin Portals of the Head TÓUQIÀOYĪN
![[GB-11 头窍阴.png|250]]
- Meeting point of the Gall Bladder, Bladder, Small Intestine and Sanjiao channels

#### Location

Posterior to the ear, along a curved line drawn from [Tiānchōng GB-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-9) to [Wángǔ GB-12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-12) running within the hairline and more or less parallel to the line of the rim of the ear, in a depression slightly greater than two thirds of the distance between [Tiānchōng GB-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-9) and [Wángǔ GB-12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-12).

#### Needling

Transverse insertion 0.5-1.5 cun. See note [Hànyàn GB-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-4).

#### Actions

- Clears the head and benefits the sense organs
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications

- Headache, dizziness, eye pain, ear pain, tinnitus, deafness, stiff tongue, bleeding from the root of the tongue, nauseating bitter taste in the mouth.
- Stiffness and pain of the neck, goitre, throat painful obstruction.
- Pain of the lateral costal region, cough, absence of sweating, contraction of the sinews of the four limbs, bone taxation, agitation and heat of the hands and feet.

#### Commentary

The name of Tóuqiàoyīn GB-11 (Yin Portals of the Head) is said to refer to its ability to treat diseases of the sense organs associated with the five zang, namely the eyes (Liver), ears (Kidneys), tongue (Heart), mouth (Spleen) and nose (Lung). In fact, classical indications appear for all of these except the nose. In this respect, Tóuqiàoyīn GB-11 in the upper body mirrors [Zúqiàoyīn GB-44](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-44) (Yin Portals of the Foot) in the lower body. For further discussion of Tóuqiàoyīn GB-11 see [Fúbái GB-10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-10).

#### Summary of clinical application

- Rarely used.
- Headache, dizziness, eye pain, ear pain, tinnitus, deafness, stiff tongue, bleeding from the root of the tongue, nauseating bitter taste in the mouth.
- Stiffness and pain of the neck, goitre, throat painful obstruction.
- Pain of the lateral costal region, cough, absence of sweating, contraction of the sinews of the four limbs, bone taxation, agitation and heat of the hands and feet.

#### Combinations

- Bleeding from the root of the tongue: Tóuqiàoyīn GB-11, [Fútú LI-18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-18) and [Dàzhōng KID-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-4) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Stabbing pain of the head with inability to move: Tóuqiàoyīn GB-11 and [Qiángjiān DU-18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-18) (Supplementing Life).
- Pain of the nape of the neck: Tóuqiàoyīn GB-11 and [Xiāoluò SJ-12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-12) (Supplementing Life).

### 1. [GB11 头窍阴](/point/GB11.md)

#### 1.1. Yin Portals of the Head 頭竅陰

- 胆经、膀胱经、小肠经和三焦经的交会穴

#### 1.2. 定位

位于耳后，沿从[GB9 天冲](/point/GB9.md)到[GB12 完骨](/point/GB12.md)的发际内弧线，弧线大致平行于耳廓边缘，在[GB9 天冲](/point/GB9.md)与[GB12 完骨](/point/GB12.md)之间略超过三分之二处的凹陷中。

#### 1.3. 针刺方法

横刺，深0.5至1.5寸。参见[GB4 颔厌](/point/GB4.md)的针刺说明。

#### 1.4. 作用

- 清头益感官
- 活络止痛

#### 1.5. 主治

- 头痛、眩晕、目痛、耳痛、耳鸣、耳聾、舌僵、舌根出血、口中恶苦味。
- 颈项僵痛、瘿病、咽痹。
- 胁痛、咳嗽、无汗、四肢筋挛、骨痨、手足烦热。

#### 1.6. 论述

[GB11 头窍阴](/point/GB11.md)的名称据说反映了其治疗与五脏相关的感官疾患的能力，即眼（肝）、耳（肾）、舌（心）、口（脾）和鼻（肺）。事实上，除鼻外，经典适应症涵盖了所有这些感官。在这方面，[GB11 头窍阴](/point/GB11.md)在上半身对应于下半身的[GB44 足窍阴](/point/GB44.md)（足部窍阴）。关于[GB11 头窍阴](/point/GB11.md)的进一步讨论，参见[GB10 浮白](/point/GB10.md)。

#### 1.7. 临床应用总结

- 使用较少。
- 适用于头痛、眩晕、目痛、耳痛、耳鸣、耳聾、舌僵、舌根出血、口中恶苦味。
- 适用于颈项僵痛、瘿病、咽痹。
- 适用于胁痛、咳嗽、无汗、四肢筋挛、骨痨、手足烦热。

#### 1.8. 配伍

- 舌根出血：[GB11 头窍阴](/point/GB11.md) + [LI18 扶突](/point/LI18.md) + [KID4 大钟](/point/KID4.md)（《千金方》）。
- 头部刺痛伴不能动：[GB11 头窍阴](/point/GB11.md) + [DU18 强间](/point/DU18.md)（《补命》）。
- 项颈痛：[GB11 头窍阴](/point/GB11.md) + [SJ12 消泺](/point/SJ12.md)（《补命》）。
