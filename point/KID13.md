### Qi Cave QÌXUÉ
![[KID-13 气穴.png|250]]
- Meeting point of the Kidney channel with the Penetrating vessel

#### Location

On the lower abdomen, 3 cun below the umbilicus, 2 cun superior to the superior border of the symphysis pubis, 0.5 cun lateral to the midline ([Guānyuán REN-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-4)). See location note for [Hénggǔ KID-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-11).

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion 0.5 to 1 cun.  
**Caution:** deep insertion will penetrate a full bladder which should therefore be emptied before treatment.

#### Actions

- Regulates the Penetrating and Conception vessels
- Regulates the lower jiao

#### Indications

- Amenorrhoea, irregular menstruation, uterine bleeding, leucorrhoea, infertility in women.
- Difficult urination, incessant diarrhoea, abdominal pain, running piglet qi, rushing sensation up and down the lumbar spine, lumbar pain, redness of the eye originating at the inner canthus.

#### Commentary

Qìxué KID-13 (like [Sìmǎn KID-14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-14) and [Zhōngzhù KID-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-15)) is an important point in the treatment of running piglet qi. According to Zhang Zhong-jing in the Essentials from the Golden Cabinet "Running piglet disorder arises from the lower abdomen; it rushes up to the throat with such ferocity that the patient feels he is close to death. It attacks and then remits. It is brought about by fear and fright". In the Classic of Difficulties[26](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-kidney-channel/references) running piglet was classified as one of the 'five accumulations' pertaining to the Kidneys. Running piglet qi primarily arises when stagnant Liver qi transforms to heat, or when Kidney yang deficiency leads to accumulation of cold in the lower jiao. In both cases, qi is violently discharged and rushes upwards along the Penetrating vessel. In clinical practice, running piglet qi may be encountered in a number of variants, all involving a rushing sensation, usually upwards, along the trunk, back or limbs. According to the Classic of Difficulties[27](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-kidney-channel/references) "When the Penetrating vessel is diseased there is counterflow qi". The application of Qìxué KID-13, [Sìmǎn KID-14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-14) and [Zhōngzhù KID-15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-15) in the treatment of this distressing condition reflects both their location on the Kidney channel and their status as points of the Penetrating vessel, which both ascends through the abdomen to disperse in the chest, and enters the spine and circulates through the back.

Many classical sources list Baomen (Gate of the Uterus) and Zihu (Child's Door) as alternative names for this point. The Supplement to the Thousand Ducat Formulas, however, is quite clear that these names correspond to [Shuǐdào ST-28](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-28), referring to the points located 2 cun either side of [Guānyuán REN-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-4).

#### Summary of clinical application

- Regulates menstruation: amenorrhoea, uterine bleeding, irregular menstruation, leucorrhoea, infertility in women.
- Treats running piglet qi giving rise to a rushing sensation (usually upwards) along the trunk, back or limbs accompanied by fear and panic. “Running piglet disorder arises from the lower abdomen; it rushes up to the throat with such ferocity that the patient feels he is close to death. It attacks and then remits. It is brought about by fear and fright” (_Essentials from the Golden Cabinet)._

#### Combinations

- Irregular menstruation and infertility: Qìxué KID-13, [Shènshū BL-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-23), [Qìhǎi REN-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-6), [Sānyīnjiāo SP-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-6) and [Shāngqiū SP-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-5).

### **[KID13 气穴](point/KID13.md)** (QÌXUÉ)

* **英文名称：** Qi Cave
* **中文名称：** 气穴
* **国际代码：** KID-13
* **穴位属性：** **[KID13 气穴](point/KID13.md)** 是肾经与冲脉的交会穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于下腹部，脐下3寸，耻骨联合上缘上方2寸，距中线旁开0.5寸（**[REN4 关元](point/REN4.md)** 旁开0.5寸）。请参阅**[KID11 横骨](point/KID11.md)** 的定位要点。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

垂直刺0.5至1寸。

* **注意 (Caution):** 深刺会刺入充盈的膀胱，因此治疗前应排空膀胱。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 调理冲脉和任脉
* 调理下焦

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

闭经，月经不调，崩漏（子宫出血），带下，女性不孕症。
小便不利，腹泻不止，腹痛，奔豚气，腰部上下冲动感，腰痛，内眼角红赤。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[KID13 气穴](point/KID13.md)**（与**[KID14 四满](point/KID14.md)** 和**[KID15 中注](point/KID15.md)** 类似）是治疗奔豚气的重要穴位。张仲景在《金匮要略》中记载：“奔豚气从少腹上冲咽喉，发作欲死，复还止。皆从惊恐得之。”在《难经》中，奔豚被归类为肾的“五积”之一。奔豚气主要发生于肝气郁结化热，或肾阳不足导致寒邪积聚下焦时。两种情况下，气机都剧烈冲动，沿冲脉向上奔涌。在临床实践中，奔豚气可表现为多种变异形式，都涉及一种冲动感，通常向上，沿躯干、背部或四肢。根据《难经》记载：“冲脉病者，苦气逆。”**[KID13 气穴](point/KID13.md)**、**[KID14 四满](point/KID14.md)** 和**[KID15 中注](point/KID15.md)** 在治疗这种令人痛苦的病症中的应用，反映了它们位于肾经上的位置以及作为冲脉穴位的地位，冲脉既上行通过腹部散布于胸部，又入脊柱循行于背部。

许多经典文献将胞门和子户列为此穴的别名。然而，《千金方翼方》明确指出，这些名称对应**[ST28 水道](point/ST28.md)**，指的是**[REN4 关元](point/REN4.md)** 旁开2寸的穴位。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 调理月经：闭经，崩漏（子宫出血），月经不调，带下，女性不孕症。
* 治疗奔豚气，引起沿躯干、背部或四肢的冲动感（通常向上），并伴有恐惧和恐慌。“奔豚气从少腹上冲咽喉，发作欲死，复还止。皆从惊恐得之”（《金匮要略》）。

#### **穴位组合 (Combinations)**

* 月经不调和不孕症：**[KID13 气穴](point/KID13.md)**，**[BL23 肾俞](point/BL23.md)**，**[REN6 气海](point/REN6.md)**，**[SP6 三阴交](point/SP6.md)** 和 **[SP5 商丘](point/SP5.md)**。
