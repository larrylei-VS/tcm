### Sea of Qi QÌHǍI
![[CV-6 气海.png|250]]
#### Location

On the midline of the lower abdomen, 1.5 cun inferior to the umbilicus and 3.5 cun superior to the pubic symphysis.

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion 0.8 to 1.5 cun.  
**Caution:** deep needling may penetrate the peritoneal cavity.

#### Actions

- Fosters original qi
- Tonifies qi
- Tonifies the Kidneys and fortifies yang
- Rescues collapse of yang
- Regulates qi and harmonises blood

#### Indications

- Zang deficiency with qi exhaustion, original qi defi- ciency, sudden collapse of yang, slow and minute pulse, yin deficiency with sudden collapse of yang, loss of consciousness from windstroke, all kinds of chronic qi disease that do not respond to treatment, emaciation of the muscles and body, feebleness and weakness of the muscles, failure of the fontanelle to close in babies, upward attack of the qi of the five zang.
- Impotence, seminal emission, prolapse of the uterus, prolapse of the rectum.
- Deficiency and cold of the lower jiao, inversion coun-terflow of the four limbs, cold illness with dark face, diarrhoea, dysenteric disorder, white and turbid urine, dark urine, hesitant urination, childhood enuresis.
- Uterine bleeding, persistent flow of lochia, post-partum bleeding, irregular menstruation, dysmenorrhoea, red and white leucorrhoea, infertility.
- The seven kinds of shan disorder, twisting pain of the umbilicus, abdominal (zheng jia) masses, abdominal distention, pain and cold sensation below the umbilicus, retraction of the testicles.
- Upward staring eyes, constipation, sudden pain of the Heart, dyspnoea, lumbar sprain.

#### Commentary

Qìhǎi CV-6, like [Guānyuán CV-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/CV-4), is also known by the name of 'Dantian' (Cinnabar Field). This reflects its location in the vital centre of the body where the deepest energies are stored and generated, and which plays a pivotal role in the treatment of disease and in Chinese martial arts and qigong practices. (For a fuller discussion of the dantian see the commentary on [Guānyuán CV-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/CV-4)).

The name Qihai (Sea of Qi) emphasises the principal difference between Qìhǎi CV-6 and [Guānyuán CV-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/CV-4). Whilst both have a powerful action on tonifying the Kidneys, the forte of Qìhǎi CV-6 is generating qi and yang and it does not share the yin and blood nourishing qualities of [Guānyuán CV-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/CV-4).

According to the Great Compendium of Acupuncture and Moxibustion Qìhǎi CV-6 is indicated for deficiency of the original qi and for qi deficiency of all the five zang. In fact the original qi is considered to be the basis for the formation and activity of all the zangfu. This concept is explained in the Classic of Difficulties [11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-conception-vessel/references) which states "The dynamic qi that moves between the Kidneys is [the basis] of human life, the source of the five zang and six fu, the root of the twelve channels, the door of respiration and the origin of the sanjiao", and in the Discourse into the Origin and Development of Medicine [12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-conception-vessel/references) which says "Original qi is distributed to the five zang and forms the essence of the five zang". Original qi is formed from the combination of pre- and post- heaven qi. In comparison with [Zúsānlǐ ST-36](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-36) which tonifies the source of post-heaven qi in the Stomach and Spleen, Qìhǎi CV-6 activates and mobilises the pre-heaven qi stored in the Kidneys. It is thus indicated in the widest possible range of disorders involving qi deficiency and exhaustion. By promoting the pre-heaven qi, Qìhǎi CV-6 is able to foster the post-heaven qi of the Stomach and Spleen and is thus indicated for emaciation and feebleness of the muscles and diarrhoea. By warming and firming the Kidney yang, Qìhǎi CV-6 is able to treat impotence, seminal emission, cold illness and dark face. By promoting the central qi, Qìhǎi CV-6 is able to treat prolapse of the rectum and uterus. So strong are its qi and yang restoring properties that it is a vital point for rescuing yang in cases of collapse with a slow and minute pulse. For this purpose strong moxibustion either by moxa stick or by large moxa cones mediated by sliced fresh ginger or aconite cake is administered. Zhu Dan-xi of the Jin-Yuan dynasty describes a case of "yin depletion followed by sudden expiry of yang" with pouring sweat, faint respiration, urinary incontinence and a large irregular and arrhythmic pulse. He applied moxa to Qìhǎi CV-6"with the moxa cones as big as the small finger. When the eighteenth cone was burned up, his right hand was able to move. Another three cones and his lips began to move a little". He also describes a case of violent diarrhoea resulting in loss of consciousness and extremely faint breathing as if on the verge of death. Moxa was performed at Qìhǎi CV-6 without delay13. The ability of Qìhǎi CV-6 to treat disorders of profound deficiency is further reflected in its designation in the Spiritual Pivot [14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-conception-vessel/references) as the shu point of the 'huang'. The huang, which refers to the area just above the diaphragm and which also appears in the name of [Gāohuāngshū BL-43](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-43), implies one of the deepest and most vital regions of the body.

It is important to emphasise that due to its intimate relationship with the qi, Qìhǎi CV-6 is equally important in the treatment of diseases due to qi stagnation affecting the lower jiao. It is indicated for distention and pain of the abdomen, constipation, qi stagnation due to pathogenic cold giving rise to retraction of the testicles, and cold pain of the abdomen. Since "qi is the master of blood" Qìhǎi CV-6 is also indicated for abdominal masses whether due to stagnation of qi or stasis of blood.

By virtue of its dual actions of tonifying and regulating qi, Qìhǎi CV-6 is able to treat a variety of gynaecological disorders. Uterine bleeding, persistent flow of lochia and post-partum bleeding may be due to failure of the qi to hold the blood, and along with irregular menstruation and dysmenorrhoea may also involve blood stasis, either as a sequela of haemorrhage or as a consequence of qi stagnation.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Generates qi and yang and supplements original qi: zang deficiency with qi exhaustion, deficiency of original qi, sudden collapse of yang, slow and minute pulse, yin deficiency with sudden collapse of yang, loss of consciousness from windstroke, all kinds of chronic qi disease that do not respond to treatment, emaciation of the muscles and body, feebleness and weakness of the muscles, failure of the fontanelle to close in babies, upward attack of the qi of the five zang, impotence, seminal emission, prolapse of the uterus, prolapse of the rectum, deficiency and cold of the lower jiao, cold illness with dark face, diarrhoea, dysenteric disorder, white and turbid urine, hesitant urination, childhood enuresis.
- Regulates qi in the lower jiao: distention and pain of the abdomen, constipation, qi stagnation due to pathogenic cold giving rise to retraction of the testicles and cold pain of the abdomen, abdominal masses due to stagnation of qi or stasis of blood.
- Regulates qi and blood in the uterus: uterine bleeding, persistent flow of lochia, post-partum bleeding, irregular menstruation, dysmenorrhoea.

### 1. [CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)

#### 1.1. Sea of Qi 氣海

#### 1.2. 定位

位于下腹部中线，脐下1.5寸，耻骨联合上缘上方3.5寸。

#### 1.3. 针刺方法

垂直刺入，深0.8至1.5寸。  
**注意：** 深刺可能穿透腹膜腔。

#### 1.4. 作用

- 培元气  
- 补气  
- 补肾壮阳  
- 救阳衰  
- 调气和血  

#### 1.5. 主治

- 脏虚气竭、元气虚、突发阳衰、脉迟微、阴虚突发阳衰、中风昏迷、各种慢性气病难治、肌肉及身体消瘦、肌肉无力、小儿囟门不闭、五脏气上攻。  
- 阳痿、遗精、子宫脱垂、直肠脱垂。  
- 下焦虚寒、四肢厥逆、寒病面色暗、腹泻、痢疾、白浊尿、尿色深、尿涩、小儿遗尿。  
- 子宫出血、恶露不尽、产后出血、月经不调、痛经、赤白带下、不孕症。  
- 七种疝病、脐绞痛、腹中瘕块、腹胀、脐下痛及冷感、睾丸上缩。  
- 目上视、便秘、心突发痛、呼吸困难、腰扭伤。  

#### 1.6. 论述

[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)与[CV4 关元](/point/CV4.md)同名“丹田”，反映其位于体内核心，储存及生成最深层能量，在疾病治疗及中国武术、气功中至关重要。（详见[CV4 关元](/point/CV4.md)关于丹田的论述）。  

“气海”之名强调其与[CV4 关元](/point/CV4.md)的主要区别。两者皆强效补肾，但[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)擅长生成气及阳，不具[CV4 关元](/point/CV4.md)滋阴养血的特性。  

《针灸大成》指[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)用于元气虚及五脏气虚。元气被视为脏腑形成及活动的基础。《难经》称：“肾间动气，人之生命，五脏六腑之本，十二经之根，呼吸之门，三焦之原。”《医源论》谓：“元气分布五脏，成五脏之精。”元气由先天与后天之气结合而成。与补后天之气（脾胃）的[ST36 足三里](/point/ST36.md)相比，[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)激活并调动肾中储存的先天之气，适用于涉及气虚及气竭的广泛疾患。通过促先天之气，[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)能培植脾胃后天之气，用于肌肉消瘦无力及腹泻；通过温固肾阳，治疗阳痿、遗精、寒病及面色暗；通过升提中气，治疗直肠及子宫脱垂。其气阳恢复作用极强，是救阳衰（脉迟微）的重要穴位，常用大艾炷或隔姜、附子饼灸治。金元朱丹溪述一“阴竭突发阳绝”病例，汗出、呼吸微、尿失禁、脉大无伦，灸[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)，“艾炷如小指大，至十八壮，右手能动，再三壮，唇微动。”他还述一剧泻致昏迷、呼吸极微似死的病例，立即灸[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)。[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)治疗深层虚证的能力进一步体现在《灵枢》将其指定为“肓”的输穴。“肓”指膈上深层要区，亦见于[BL43 膏肓俞](/point/BL43.md)之名。  

需强调，因与气密切相关，[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)同样重要于治疗下焦气滞疾患，用于腹胀痛、便秘、寒邪气滞致睾丸上缩及腹冷痛。因“气为血帅”，[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)也适用于气或血瘀引起的腹中瘕块。  

凭借补气调气的双重作用，[CV6 气海](/point/CV6.md)能治多种妇科病。子宫出血、恶露不尽及产后出血可因气不摄血，与月经不调、痛经一样，也可涉及血瘀，或为出血后遗，或因气滞所致。  

#### 1.7. 临床应用总结

- 生成气阳，补元气：脏虚气竭、元气虚、突发阳衰、脉迟微、阴虚突发阳衰、中风昏迷、各种慢性气病难治、肌肉及身体消瘦、肌肉无力、小儿囟门不闭、五脏气上攻、阳痿、遗精、子宫脱垂、直肠脱垂、下焦虚寒、寒病面色暗、腹泻、痢疾、白浊尿、尿涩、小儿遗尿。  
- 调下焦气：腹胀痛、便秘、寒邪气滞致睾丸上缩及腹冷痛、气或血瘀引起的腹中瘕块。  
- 调子宫气血：子宫出血、恶露不尽、产后出血、月经不调、痛经。
