### Outside of the Crook WĚIYÁNG
![[BL-39 委阳.png|250]]
- Lower He-Sea point of the Sanjiao

#### Location

At the back of the knee, on the popliteal crease and towards its lateral end, in the depression medial to the tendon of biceps femoris.

#### Location Notes

Locate with the knee slightly flexed.

#### Needling

Perpendicular insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.

#### Actions

- Harmonises the Sanjiao and regulates urination
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications

- Difficult urination, retention of urine, painful urinary dysfunction, enuresis.
- Stiffness and pain of the lumbar spine, lumbar pain radiating to the abdomen, contraction and pain of the leg and foot.
- Fullness of the chest, pain and swelling of the axilla, distention and fullness of the lower abdomen, haemorrhoids, constipation.

#### Commentary

The Spiritual Pivot[49](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) states "The he-sea points treat the internal fu". The three yang channels of the upper limb (Sanjiao, Large Intestine and Small Intestine) each have a he-sea point on their respective channel ([Tiānjǐng SJ-10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SJ-10), [Qūchí LI-11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-11) and [Xiǎohǎi SI-8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-8)). It is evident however, when one examines the indications for each of these points, that they have relatively little action on their related fu. This is not unexpected in view of the fact that whilst the channels are on the upper limb, in the case of the intestines, their associated fu are in the lower jiao. The same chapter of the Spiritual Pivot then goes on to rectify this contradiction by designating a lower he-sea point on the lower limb for each of the yang channels of the arm (Wěiyáng BL-39, [Shàngjùxū ST-37](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-37) and [Xiàjùxū ST-39](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-39) respectively).

Wěiyáng BL-39, as the lower he-sea point of the Sanjiao, has a specific action on the treatment of urinary disorders. The Spiritual Pivot[50](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) emphasises the close relationship between the Sanjiao, the Kidneys and the Bladder, and states that there is a direct link between Wěiyáng BL-39 and the Bladder fu. According to the Essential Questions "The Sanjiao is the official in charge of drainage and controls the water passages"[51](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references), whilst the Ming dynasty classic Introduction to Medicine elaborated as follows "The lower jiao dominates moving and draining the urine and faeces, and at the right time discharging them ... therefore it is said the lower jiao is like a drain". The Spiritual Pivot says of Wěiyáng BL-39 "When excess there is retention of urine; when deficient there is enuresis; [when there is] enuresis, then reinforce, [when there is] retention then reduce"[52](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) and "In diseases of the Sanjiao [there will be] fullness of the abdomen, hardness of the lower abdomen with inability to urinate causing extreme anguish, water will overflow and give rise to oedema and abdominal distention ... select Wěiyáng BL-39"[53](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references). As the lower he-sea point of the Sanjiao on the Bladder channel, Wěiyáng BL-39 specifically acts on the qi transforming action of the Bladder (ultimately controlled by the Kidneys) and hence is an important point in the treatment of retention of urine or difficult urination as well as enuresis.

An interesting classical indication for this point is axillary pain and swelling. This may be explained either by the pathway of the Bladder sinew channel, or more pertinently as a symptom of the Sanjiao channel.

#### Summary of clinical application

- As the lower he-sea point of the Sanjiao is able to regulate urination: difficult urination, retention of urine, painful urinary dysfunction, enuresis.
- “In diseases of the Sanjiao [there will be] fullness of the abdomen, hard ness of the lower abdomen with inability to urinate causing extreme anguish, water will overflow and give rise to oedema and abdominal distention ... select Weiyang BL‑39” (_Spiritual Pivot)._
- Also indicated for stiffness and pain of the lumbar spine, lumbar pain radiating to the abdomen, contraction and pain of the leg and foot, distention and fullness of the lower abdomen, haemorrhoids, constipation.

#### Combinations

- Hardness and pain in the lower abdomen that radiates to the genitals with inability to urinate: Wěiyáng BL-39, [Shímén REN-5](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-5) and [Yīnjiāo REN-7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-7) (Supplementing Life).
- Dribbling urination: Wěiyáng BL-39, [Zhìshì BL-52](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-52) and [Zhōngliáo BL-33](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-33) (Supplementing Life).
- Involuntary erection with difficult urination: Wěiyáng BL-39, [Yīngǔ KID-10](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/KID-10), [Dàdūn LIV-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LIV-1), [Qímén LIV-14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LIV-14) and [Wěizhōng BL-40](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-40) (Supplementing Life).
- Swelling of the axilla: Wěiyáng BL-39 and [Tiānchí PC-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/PC-1) (One Hundred Symptoms).
- Swelling of the axilla: Wěiyáng BL-39, [Tiānchí PC-1](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/PC-1), [Shēnmài BL-62](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-62), [Dìwǔhùi GB-42](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-42), [Yángfǔ GB-38](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-38) and [Zúlínqì GB-41](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-41) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).
- Lumbar pain with inability to bend and extend: Wěiyáng BL-39, [Yīnmén BL-37](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-37), [Tàibái SP-3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-3), [Yīnlíngquán SP-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SP-9) and [Xíngjiān LIV-2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LIV-2) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).

### **[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)** (WĚIYÁNG)

* **英文名称：** Outside of the Crook
* **中文名称：** 委阳
* **国际代码：** BL-39
* **穴位属性：** **[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)** 是三焦经的下合穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于膝部后方，腘横纹外侧端，股二头肌腱内侧的凹陷中。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 膝关节微屈时定位。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

垂直刺1至1.5寸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 调和三焦，调理小便
* 通经活络，止痛

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

小便不利，尿潴留，淋证（小便涩痛），遗尿。
腰脊僵硬疼痛，腰痛放射至腹部，腿脚挛缩疼痛。
胸中胀满，腋窝疼痛肿胀，小腹胀满，痔疮，便秘。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

《灵枢·本输》记载：“合治内腑”。上肢的三条阳经（三焦经、大肠经和小肠经）各自在其经脉上都有合穴（分别为**[SJ10 天井](point/SJ10.md)**、**[LI11 曲池](point/LI11.md)** 和**[SI8 小海](point/SI8.md)**）。然而，通过检查这些穴位的主治，可以明显看出它们对其各自的腑作用相对较小。考虑到经脉在上肢，而肠道等腑则在下焦，这并不出乎意料。
《灵枢》的同一篇章随后通过为手臂的每条阳经在下肢指定一个下合穴（分别为**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)**、**[ST37 上巨虚](point/ST37.md)** 和**[ST39 下巨虚](point/ST39.md)**）来纠正这一矛盾。

**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)** 作为三焦经的下合穴，在治疗泌尿系统疾病方面有特定作用。《灵枢·本输》强调了三焦、肾和膀胱之间的密切关系，并指出**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)** 与膀胱腑之间有直接联系。根据《素问·灵兰秘典论》记载：“三焦者，决渎之官，水道出焉”，而明代经典《医学入门》则进一步阐述：“下焦者，主出水谷之道路，当其时而泄……故曰下焦如渎”。
《灵枢·本输》论及**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)** 时说：“实则癃，虚则遗尿；遗尿则补之，癃则泻之”，又曰：“三焦病者，腹满，下腹硬满，小便不利，水泛滥而致水肿，腹胀……取**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)**。”作为膀胱经上的三焦经下合穴，**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)** 特别作用于膀胱的气化功能（最终由肾脏控制），因此是治疗尿潴留或小便不利以及遗尿的重要穴位。

此穴有一个有趣的经典主治是腋窝疼痛肿胀。这可以用膀胱经筋的循行来解释，或者更恰当地说，是三焦经的症状。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 作为三焦经的下合穴，能够调理小便：小便不利，尿潴留，淋证（小便涩痛），遗尿。
* “三焦病者，腹满，下腹硬满，小便不利，甚则水泛滥而致水肿，腹胀……取**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)**”（《灵枢》）。
* 也主治腰脊僵硬疼痛，腰痛放射至腹部，腿脚挛缩疼痛，小腹胀满，痔疮，便秘。

#### **穴位组合 (Combinations)**

* 小腹硬满疼痛，放射至生殖器伴小便不利：**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)**，**[REN5 石门](point/REN5.md)** 和 **[REN7 阴交](point/REN7.md)**（《资生经》）。
* 小便淋漓：**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)**，**[BL52 志室](point/BL52.md)** 和 **[BL33 中髎](point/BL33.md)**（《资生经》）。
* 阴茎异常勃起伴小便不利：**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)**，**[KID10 阴谷](point/KID10.md)**，**[LIV1 大敦](point/LIV1.md)**，**[LIV14 期门](point/LIV14.md)** 和 **[BL40 委中](point/BL40.md)**（《资生经》）。
* 腋窝肿胀：**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)** 和 **[PC1 天池](point/PC1.md)**（《百症赋》）。
* 腋窝肿胀：**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)**，**[PC1 天池](point/PC1.md)**，**[BL62 申脉](point/BL62.md)**，**[GB42 地五会](point/GB42.md)**，**[GB38 阳辅](point/GB38.md)** 和 **[GB41 足临泣](point/GB41.md)**（《千金方》）。
* 腰痛不能屈伸：**[BL39 委阳](point/BL39.md)**，**[BL37 殷门](point/BL37.md)**，**[SP3 太白](point/SP3.md)**，**[SP9 阴陵泉](point/SP9.md)** 和 **[LIV2 行间](point/LIV2.md)**（《千金方》）。
