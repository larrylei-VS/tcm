### Heart Shu XĪNSHŪ
![[BL-15 心俞.png|250]]
- Back-Shu point of the Heart

#### Location

1.5 cun lateral to the lower border of the spinous process of the fifth thoracic vertebra (T5).

#### Location Notes

Locate at the visible highest point of the paraspinal muscles.

#### Needling

Oblique insertion towards the spine, 0.5 to 1 cun, or transverse-oblique insertion 1 to 1.5 cun.  
**Caution:** perpendicular needling or oblique needling away from the spine carries a substantial risk of causing a pneumothorax.

#### Actions

- Tonifies and nourishes the Heart
- Regulates Heart qi
- Calms the spirit
- Unbinds the chest and resolves blood stasis
- Clears Heart fire

#### Indications

- Heart pain, oppression of the chest with restlessness, chest pain extending to the back, worried and oppressive sensation of the chest with inability to catch the breath, palpitations, fright palpitations, irregular pulse, Heart qi deficiency in children.
- Poor memory, anxiety, weeping with grief, frightened and cautious with Heart deficiency, insomnia, excessive dreaming, disorientation, delayed speech development, mania-depression, epilepsy, dementia, mad walking, seminal emission.
- Cough, coughing blood, vomiting, vomiting blood, abdominal distention, difficult ingestion, night sweating, redness of the lips accompanied by sweating.
- Lack of strength in the root of the tongue, lacrimation, eye pain, windstroke, hemiplegia, jaundice, nosebleed, white turbidity.

#### Commentary

Xīnshū BL-15 is the back-shu point of the Heart, where the qi of the Heart emanates from the interior to the body surface, and in common with all the back-shu points (especially those of the yin zang) Xīnshū BL-15 has a strong action on regulating and tonifying its corresponding zangfu at the deepest level. It is used equally to tonify deficiency (the Heart is unique amongst the zangfu in that it commonly suffers from all the four main kinds of deficiency: blood, yin, qi and yang), and to resolve excess pathogenic factors such as blood stasis, Heart fire and obstruction by phlegm.

According to the Essential Questions "The Heart is the monarch from which the spirit emanates"[11](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) and "The Heart stores the spirit"[12](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references). Since storage is a yin function, it is primarily the Heart blood and yin that have the function of nourishing the Heart and providing the material basis for the Heart to house the spirit. Blood and yin deficiency of the Heart may originate from physical causes such as loss of blood, chronic illness and overwork or from emotional causes. Fei Bo-xiong [13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) said "The seven emotions injure the five yin organs selectively but they all affect the Heart". According to the Essential Questions[14](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) "The Heart stores the spirit ... when the spirit is insufficient there is sadness". When Heart blood or yin are deficient, the malnourished and unrooted spirit loses its harmony and becomes restless and unquiet, resulting in such symptoms as anxiety, poor memory, fearfulness, insomnia, excessive dreaming and weeping with grief. According to Essential Readings from the Medical Tradition "That which is stored by the Heart internally is blood, externally it is emitted as sweat; sweat is the fluid of the Heart". Xīnshū BL-15 is also indicated for night sweating due to deficiency of either Heart blood or Heart yin, and is therefore ideally suited to treat the commonly encountered pattern of excessive dreaming, or waking with anxiety, accompanied by sweating.

The Heart controls the pulse, and when Heart qi is deficient and is unable to regulate the blood, there may be palpitations or irregular pulse, especially if the Heart blood is also deficient. In the latter case, palpitations will be more likely to be accompanied by anxiety, in contrast to the palpitations caused purely by qi deficiency which are usually unrelated to emotional changes. In fact palpitations may accompany any pattern of Heart disharmony, whether excess or deficient, and in all these cases Xīnshū BL-15 may be selected.

References to the relationship between the Heart, the blood and its vessels abound in the classics. The Essential Questions says "All blood pertains to the Heart"[15](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references), "The Heart dominates the blood vessels of the entire body"[16](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) and "The Heart stores the qi of the blood vessels"[17](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references). The Classic of Categories states "The vessels are the pathways of the blood qi, the movement of the vessels is dependent on qi", whilst in the Spiritual Pivot[18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-bladder-channel/references) it says "When the qi of hand shaoyin channel is exhausted, the vessels are not open, thus the blood will not flow; when the blood does not flow the circulation will eventually stop ... and the blood will die". The above statements all emphasise the close relationship between the circulation of blood, and the qi and yang of the Heart. Clinically this relationship is most clearly manifested in the chest region. When Heart yang is deficient and unable to circulate the blood in the chest and Heart, there may be consequential Heart blood stasis giving rise to pain and oppression. In clinical practice, this pattern is often complicated by deficiency of qi, blood or yin, or stagnation of qi or phlegm. Whatever the pattern, Xīnshū BL-15 is an essential point.

The action of Xīnshū BL-15 in treating blood stasis reflects its ability to treat both deficiency and excess patterns of the Heart with equal effect. According to a number of classics it is an important point to clear heat of excess or deficiency type from the Heart. The heat-clearing effect of Xīnshū BL-15 extends to the treatment of bleeding, and when Heart fire injures the blood vessels of the Lung or Stomach there may be coughing or vomiting of blood. A different explanation of these symptoms is offered by the Investigation Into Points Along the Channels which says that Xīnshū BL-15 is indicated for "Heart blood unable to enter the Liver; in the upper, there is wild movement, in the lower there is blood in the stools".

The heat-clearing action of Xīnshū BL-15 finds its most important expression in the treatment of excess type psycho-emotional disorders. When Heart fire rages out of control it agitates and excites the spirit leading to such symptoms as severe insomnia and excessive dreaming. When Heart fire combines with phlegm and obstructs the portals of the Heart, there will be severe disorders of the spirit such as epilepsy, dementia, mad walking and mania-depression.

The heat clearing action of Xīnshū BL-15 extends beyond the Heart itself, and the Illustrated Supplement to the Classic of Categories states that Xīnshū BL-15 is one of five points ([Fèishū BL-13](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-13), Xīnshū BL-15, [Gānshū BL-18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-18), [Píshū BL-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-20) and [Shènshū BL-23](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/BL-23)) which "drain heat from the five zang".

Seminal emission may present in a number of different patterns and is broadly differentiated into seminal emission accompanied by dreaming and seminal emission without dreams. Seminal emission with dreams may be due to Heart fire, Heart and Spleen deficiency, Heart and Kidney deficiency, ministerial fire or damp-heat. Even in cases where the Heart is not directly involved, however, if seminal emission is accompanied by erotic dreams, Xīnshū BL-15 is indicated.

Finally, in common with most of the yin back-shu points, Xīnshū BL-15 is able to treat the tissues and sense organs associated with its corresponding zang. The tongue is the 'sprouting forth' of the Heart and Xīnshū BL-15 is indicated for slow speech development in children and lack of strength in the root of the tongue. Xīnshū BL-15 is also indicated for lacrimation and eye pain. These indications however, reflect disorder of the Heart channel rather than the Heart zang.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Heart back-shu point for all Heart patterns, both deficient and excess: Heart pain, palpitations, fright palpitations, irregular pulse, Heart qi deficiency in children, pain and oppression of the chest, chest pain extending to the back, worried and oppressive sensation of the chest with inability to catch the breath.
- Important point to regulate the spirit: poor memory, anxiety, weeping with grief, cautiousness and fear with Heart deficiency, insomnia, excessive dreaming, disorientation, delayed speech development, mania-depression, epilepsy, dementia, mad walking.

### **[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** (XĪNSHŪ)

* **英文名称：** Heart Shu
* **中文名称：** 心俞
* **国际代码：** BL-15
* **穴位属性：** **[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 是心的背俞穴。

#### **定位 (Location)**

位于第五胸椎（T5）棘突下缘旁开1.5寸。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 位于脊柱旁肌肉的最高点。

#### **针刺方法 (Needling)**

斜刺，向脊柱方向0.5至1寸，或横斜刺1至1.5寸。

* **注意 (Caution):** 垂直针刺或向远离脊柱方向斜刺有很大风险引起气胸。

#### **作用 (Actions)**

* 补益心脏
* 调理心气
* 宁心安神
* 宽胸散结，活血化瘀
* 清泄心火

#### **主治病症 (Indications)**

心痛，胸部憋闷烦躁，胸痛放射至背部，胸中郁闷不舒伴呼吸困难，心悸，惊悸，脉搏不规律，小儿心气虚。
记忆力差，焦虑，悲伤哭泣，心虚胆怯，失眠，多梦，神志不清，言语发育迟缓，癫狂，癫痫，痴呆，循衣摸床（或行走无度），遗精。
咳嗽，咳血，呕吐，呕血，腹胀，吞咽困难，盗汗，口唇红赤伴汗出。
舌根无力，流泪，眼痛，中风，半身不遂，黄疸，鼻出血，白浊。

#### **阐释 (Commentary)**

**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 是心的背俞穴，是心气从内向体表输布的地方。与所有背俞穴一样（特别是阴脏的背俞穴），**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 在最深层次上调理和补益其相应的脏腑。它既可用于补虚（心脏在所有脏腑中独一无二，常患四种主要虚证：血虚、阴虚、气虚和阳虚），也可用于化解血瘀、心火和痰阻等实邪。

根据《素问·灵兰秘典论》记载：“心者，君主之官也，神明出焉”，《素问·灵兰秘典论》记载：“心者，神之舍也”。由于藏是阴的功能，主要是心血和心阴具有滋养心脏，并为心藏神提供物质基础的功能。心血或心阴不足可能源于失血、慢性疾病和过劳等生理原因，或源于情志原因。费伯雄认为：“七情伤五脏，皆伤心。”根据《素问·生气通天论》记载：“心者，神之舍也……神不足则悲。”当心血或心阴不足时，心神失养且无所依附，失去其和谐，变得躁动不安，导致焦虑、记忆力差、恐惧、失眠、多梦和悲伤哭泣等症状。
根据《医宗必读》记载：“心藏血，外则汗，汗者心之液也”。**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 也主治因心血或心阴不足引起的盗汗，因此非常适合治疗常见的过度做梦或伴有汗出的焦虑性失眠。

心主脉，当心气不足不能调理血液时，可能出现心悸或脉搏不规律，特别是当心血也不足时。在后一种情况下，心悸更可能伴有焦虑，这与纯粹由气虚引起的心悸不同，后者通常与情志变化无关。实际上，心悸可能伴随心脏的任何证型，无论是实证还是虚证，所有这些情况下都可选用**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)**。

经典中关于心、血及其血管之间关系的记载比比皆是。《素问·五脏生成论》记载：“诸血者，皆属于心”，《素问·经脉别论》记载：“心主身之血脉”，《素问·脉要精微论》记载：“心藏脉气”。《类经》记载：“脉者，血气之隧道，脉之动也，以气为本”，而《灵枢·本神》记载：“手少阴气绝，则脉不通，脉不通则血不流；血不流则脉遂不营，……而血死矣。”以上论述都强调了血液循环与心气和心阳之间的密切关系。临床上这种关系最清楚地表现在胸部区域。当心阳不足不能鼓动血液在胸部和心脏循环时，可能继发心血瘀滞，引起疼痛和憋闷。在临床实践中，这种证型常伴有气、血或阴不足，或气滞、痰阻。无论何种证型，**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 都是关键穴位。

**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 活血化瘀的作用反映了其能够同等有效地治疗心脏的虚证和实证。根据多部经典记载，它是清泻心部实热或虚热的重要穴位。**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 的清热作用延伸至治疗出血，当心火损伤肺或胃的血管时，可能出现咳血或呕血。
《循经考穴编》对这些症状提供了不同的解释，认为**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 主治“心血不能入肝；其上者妄行，其下者血在便”。

**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 的清热作用在治疗实证精神疾病方面表现最为重要。当心火炽盛失控时，会扰乱和兴奋心神，导致严重失眠和过度多梦。当心火与痰结合并阻滞心窍时，会出现癫痫、痴呆、循衣摸床（或行走无度）和癫狂等严重神志疾病。

**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 的清热作用超越了心脏本身，《类经图翼》记载，**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 是“泻五脏之热”的五个穴位之一（**[BL13 肺俞](point/BL13.md)**、**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)**、**[BL18 肝俞](point/BL18.md)**、**[BL20 脾俞](point/BL20.md)** 和**[BL23 肾俞](point/BL23.md)**）。

遗精可表现为多种不同证型，大致分为伴梦遗精和无梦遗精。伴梦遗精可能由心火、心脾两虚、心肾两虚、相火妄动或湿热引起。然而，即使在心脏不直接受累的情况下，如果遗精伴有性梦，**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 仍可主治。

最后，与大多数阴经背俞穴一样，**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 能够治疗与其相应脏腑相关的组织和七窍。舌为心之“苗”，**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 主治小儿言语发育迟缓和舌根无力。**[BL15 心俞](point/BL15.md)** 也主治流泪和眼痛。然而，这些主治反映的是心经病变而非心脏病变。

#### **临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)**

* 心之背俞穴，治疗所有心脏证型，包括虚证和实证：心痛，心悸，惊悸，脉搏不规律，小儿心气虚，胸痛胸闷，胸痛放射至背部，胸中郁闷不舒伴呼吸困难。
* 调理神志的重要穴位：记忆力差，焦虑，悲伤哭泣，心虚胆怯，失眠，多梦，神志不清，言语发育迟缓，癫狂，癫痫，痴呆，循衣摸床（或行走无度）。
