### Earth Granary DÌCĀNG
![[ST-4 地仓.png|250]]
- Meeting point of the Stomach and Large Intestine channels with the Yang Motility and Conception vessels

#### Location

0.4 cun lateral to the corner of the mouth.

#### Location Notes

This point lies in the continuation of the naso-labial groove; ask the patient to smile if the groove is not visible.

#### Needling

Transverse insertion to join with such points as [Jiáchē ST-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-6), [Yíngxiāng LI-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-20), [Chéngjiāng REN-24](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-24) etc.

#### Actions

- Eliminates wind from the face
- Activates the channel and alleviates pain

#### Indications

- Deviation of the mouth, pain of the cheek, trigeminal neuralgia, drooling, food and drink leak out of the mouth, numbness of the lips and face, toothache, contraction of the facial muscles, loss of speech.
- Ceaseless movement of the eyeball, inability to close the eye, twitching of the eyelids, itching of the eye, blurring of distant objects, night blindness.
- Atrophy disorder with inability to walk, swelling of the leg, inability to eat.

#### Commentary

The 12th century classic Secret Writings of Bian Que states "use Dìcāng ST-4 in all cases where treacherous wind enters the ear, mouth and eye and leads to deviation". Dìcāng ST-4 is an important local point to eliminate wind from the face and is frequently used in the treatment of facial paralysis, whether due to exterior pathogenic wind or the sequelae of windstroke, particularly when the mouth is affected with symptoms such as drooling and deviation. Dìcāng ST-4 is also important in the treatment of facial pain such as trigeminal neuralgia. Pain of the face is usually differentiated into: i. invasion of the channels of the face by wind and cold, ii. flaring up of Stomach and Liver fire, and iii. heat due to yin deficiency. Due to its location, Dìcāng ST-4 may be needled whatever the pattern.

For both these applications, Dìcāng ST-4 is commonly joined to points such as [Jiáchē ST-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-6), [Quánliáo SI-18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-18), [Yíngxiāng LI-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-20) and [Chéngjiāng REN-24](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-24) by transverse needling. The Great Compendium of Acupuncture and Moxibustion recommends needling left Dìcāng ST-4 for diseases of the right side of the face, and vice-versa. This principle of contralateral needling is discussed in the Spiritual Pivot[2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) which states "Contralateral insertion, that is, if the left is affected, the right is treated, and if the right affected, the left is needled", and the Essential Questions[3](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) which says "When evils invade the channels, if the left side is in excess, the disease occurs on the right side, and vice versa ... for these conditions, contralateral insertion should be used". Although common practice emphasises needling the diseased side, these statements underline the important principle of treating the healthy side, where the qi is plentiful, in chronic cases and those involving great deficiency.

It is interesting to note the use of Dìcāng ST-4 for leg disorders, reflecting the principle stated in the Yellow Emperor's Inner Classic[4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/book/pages/channels/the-stomach-channel/references) "When the disease is below select [points] from above". Whilst the use of points on the lower body to treat diseases of the upper body is extremely common, the reverse is relatively rare. Other examples are [Bǎihuì DU-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-20) for anal disorders, [Rénzhōng DU-26](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-26) and [Rényíng ST-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-9) for lumbar pain, and [Shuàigǔ GB-8](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-8) for vomiting.

#### Summary of clinical application

- Important local point for interior or exterior wind giving rise to: deviation of the mouth, face pain, trigeminal neuralgia, numbness of the lips and face, contraction of the facial muscles, inability to close the eye and twitching of the eyelids.
- For facial paralysis and trigeminal neuralgia, frequently joined to points such as [Jiáchē ST-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-6), [Quánliáo SI-18](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/SI-18) and [Yíngxiāng LI-20](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-20) by transverse needling.
- “Use [Dìcāng ST-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-4) in all cases where treacherous wind enters the ear, mouth and eye and leads to deviation” (Secret Writings of Bian Que).

#### Combinations

- Deviation of the mouth: Dìcāng ST-4 and [Jiáchē ST-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-6) (Ode of the Jade Dragon).
- Deviation of the mouth: Dìcāng ST-4 and [Lièquē LU-7](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-7) (Supplementing Life).
- Deviation of the mouth and eye: Dìcāng ST-4, [Jiáchē ST-6](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/ST-6), [Rénzhōng DU-26](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/DU-26), [Chéngjiāng REN-24](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/REN-24), [Tīnghuì GB-2](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/GB-2) and [Hégǔ LI-4](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LI-4) (Illustrated Supplement).
- Legs withered and lame: Dìcāng ST-4 and [Tàiyuān LU-9](https://app.amanualofacupuncture.com/point/LU-9) (Thousand Ducat Formulas).

### [ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) (DÌCĀNG)

* **英文名称：** Earth Granary
* **中文名称：** 地仓
* **国际代码：** ST-4
* **穴位属性：** [ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) 是胃经与大肠经、阳跷脉、任脉的交会穴。

#### 定位 (Location)

位于口角旁开0.4寸处。

* **定位要点 (Location Notes):**
    * 此穴位于鼻唇沟的延续线上；如果鼻唇沟不明显，可请患者微笑。

#### 针刺方法 (Needling)

横刺透向[ST6 颊车](point/ST6.md)、[LI20 迎香](point/LI20.md)、[REN24 承浆](point/REN24.md) 等穴。

#### 作用 (Actions)

* 祛面部之风
* 通经活络，止痛

#### 主治病症 (Indications)

口歪，面颊疼痛，三叉神经痛，流涎，饮食外溢，口唇面部麻木，牙痛，面肌痉挛，失语。
眼球不自主转动，眼不能闭，眼睑跳动，眼睛瘙痒，视物模糊，夜盲。
痿证不能行走，腿部肿胀，不能进食。

#### 阐释 (Commentary)

十二世纪经典《扁鹊心书》记载：“凡贼风入耳、口、眼，导致口歪者，皆用[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md)。”[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) 是祛面部之风的重要局部穴位，常用于治疗面瘫，无论是由于外感风邪还是中风后遗症，特别是当口部受累出现流涎、口歪等症状时。[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) 在治疗三叉神经痛等面部疼痛方面也很重要。面部疼痛通常分为：i. 风寒侵袭面部经络；ii. 胃火和肝火上炎；iii. 阴虚内热。由于其位置特点，[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) 适用于各种证型。

在这两种应用中，[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) 常采用横刺法透向[ST6 颊车](point/ST6.md)、[SI18 颧髎](point/SI18.md)、[LI20 迎香](point/LI20.md) 和[REN24 承浆](point/REN24.md) 等穴。
《针灸大成》建议左侧面部疾病针刺右侧[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md)，反之亦然。这种交叉取穴原则在《灵枢》中有所讨论，其中指出“刺左右者，左有病而右取之，右有病而左取之”，《素问》亦言“邪气入络，左多者，病在右，右多者，病在左……此皆刺其对侧”。尽管常见做法强调针刺病患侧，但这些论述强调了在慢性病和虚证中，应取气血充盛的健侧进行治疗这一重要原则。

值得注意的是，[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) 也用于治疗腿部疾病，这反映了《黄帝内经》中“病在下者取之上”的原则。虽然下体穴位治疗上体疾病极为常见，但反之则相对较少。其他例子包括[DU20 百会](point/DU20.md) 治疗脱肛，[DU26 人中](point/DU26.md) 和[ST9 人迎](point/ST9.md) 治疗腰痛，以及[GB8 率谷](point/GB8.md) 治疗呕吐。

#### 临床应用总结 (Summary of clinical application)

治疗内风或外风引起的面部疾病的重要局部穴位：口歪，面痛，三叉神经痛，口唇面部麻木，面肌痉挛，眼不能闭，眼睑跳动。
治疗面瘫和三叉神经痛，常横刺透向[ST6 颊车](point/ST6.md)、[SI18 颧髎](point/SI18.md) 和[LI20 迎香](point/LI20.md) 等穴。
“凡贼风入耳、口、眼，导致口歪者，皆用[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md)”（《扁鹊心书》）。

#### 穴位组合 (Combinations)

口歪：[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) 和[LU7 列缺](point/LU7.md)（《资生经》）。
口眼歪斜：[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md)，[ST6 颊车](point/ST6.md)，[DU26 人中](point/DU26.md)，[REN24 承浆](point/REN24.md)，[GB2 听会](point/GB2.md) 和[LI4 合谷](point/LI4.md)（《图翼》）。
两腿痿弱无力：[ST4 地仓](point/ST4.md) 和[LU9 太渊](point/LU9.md)（《千金方》）。
